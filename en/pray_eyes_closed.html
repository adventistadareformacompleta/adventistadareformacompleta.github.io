CLOSED EYES PRAYER

The divine nature of Christ, is food for the Christian and prayer together with meditation are ways of receiving this nourishment, until we become friends with Christ, after all, prayer is the opening of the heart to God as to a friend (Questions I would ask Sister White, 143), therefore, we need to pray and recognize our dependence on the Creator, praying with a clean heart, reverence and posture, as the Scriptures taught us on our knees, hands raised and eyes closed, or we will languish in a starving state until we die. of spiritual inedia. Not forgetting that in the midst of machines, on the street, and on trips, we can lift our hearts to God in supplications that will be heard, but, in church, in private prayers, in the bedroom or in family worship, we need to fulfill the divine dictates, just as Moses showed us when he faced Amalek,

There are people eager to find enlightenment, to discover duties to fulfill that he alone is able to obey, with the aim of being the only one who knows the truth who communes with God, however, they are without Christ, they know nothing about the divine nature that enables us to love and obey the truth, reconciling ourselves with God. Many of these brethren are zealous, Satan knows they are uncovered, without the divine nature of Christ to protect them, takes advantage to direct them through crooked paths of the sinful nature, see what Sister White says:

Some zealous members who aim at originality and are using all their energy to achieve it, have made a serious mistake in seeking to achieve something sensational, wonderful, fascinating before the people, something they think others do not understand; but they themselves do not know what they are talking about. They speculate with the word of God, putting forward ideas that are not a hint of help for themselves or the church. For the moment they may excite the imagination, but there is a reaction, and these very ideas become an obstacle. Faith is confused with fancy, and its views can incline the mind in a wrong direction.
Ellen G. White, Selected Messages Vol I, page 180

They are meticulous in the performance of religious duties, meeting the insatiable desires of the sinful nature, fulfilling the words of Christ: "Hypocrites! Well did Isaiah prophesy of you, saying, This people honors me with their lips, but with their hearts. far from me. It is in vain that they worship me, teaching for doctrines the commandments of men" Matthew 15:7-9 . Though covered with duties to fulfil, as were the Jews, hearts are still far from God, because they are whitewashed graves, the blind leading the blind, there is sight, that without the righteousness of Christ, every sacrifice directed to God is offered for self-righteousness, and the fruits of original sin (sinful nature) remain separate from God:"But your iniquities have separated between you and your God; and your sins have hid his face from you, that he will not hear" Isaiah 59:2 . Even Moses could not see the face of God, he would die, because the Lord does not support the sinful nature, that is, original sin itself, conceived by Lucifer when he rebelled in heaven, is something that is within man, only Jesus, through of the atoning work can remove the sinful nature, lending its divine nature, reconciling man to God, therefore, everything we offer to God has to rise with the incense of the divine nature of Christ to be accepted by the Creator.

Brethren who are not nourished by the divine nature of Christ, are in great danger, are prone to panning for lights to illuminate the church, according to their own judgment, resulting in discord, resentment, separation, and death. They interpret texts from the Bible and the Testimonies out of context, they are entrenched in their understandings and they don't care how many people are going to kill in the church. Christ warned, "the hour is coming, when everyone who kills you will think he is doing God a service" John 16:2 . Death is not always literal, many brothers, entire families, can die spiritually in the church due to reckless attitudes, doctrinal points detached from their contexts and the poison of the tongue. In the same vein are the teachings of Sister White, verbis:

There are some who take from the word of God and also from the testimonies highlighted paragraphs or sentences that can be interpreted so as to fit their ideas, and they stop at them, and cast themselves in their own positions, when God is not leading them. There is your danger.
Ellen G. White, Selected Messages Vol I, page 179

Even knowing the truth, if our religious life is not nourished by the divine nature of Christ, in order to obey these truths out of love, our religious life is useless, everything is relegated to formalism, the appearance of an exemplary religious life, when in fact we do not of whitewashed graves in the eyes of God. This is the reason why many brothers, even knowing the truth, embark on paths of the fire of fanaticism or the ice of indifference.

The so-called lights are often truths mixed with comparisons, suppositions and deprived of a clear THUS SAITH THE LORD. In this wake, doctrines are formed and imputed in the minds and hearts of unsanctified brethren, not shielded by the righteousness of Christ. See the following writings by Sister Whie:

You have a lot of truth, precious truth, but mixed with SUPPOSITIONS. Your extreme ideas and strong language often destroy the effect of your best efforts.
Ellen G. White, Selected Messages Vol I, page 180

Regrettably, these religious confusions often happen within the church, in the subject under discussion, there are not a few religious people supporting the thesis that prayer should be done in church, in private and domestic services with eyes open, for this they list some biblical texts as the foundation of their theses, let's go to them.

Those who advocate this cause claim that the model, Jesus, prayed with his eyes open, based on two texts narrated by the apostle John. "Then they took away the stone. And Jesus, looking up to heaven, said, Father, I thank thee that thou hast heard me" John 11:41 .

"When Jesus had spoken these things, he lifted up his eyes to heaven, and said, Father, the hour has come; glorify your Son, that the Son may glorify you" John 17:1 . and "I lift up my eyes to you who dwell in heaven! As the eyes of servants are to the hand of their masters, and the eyes of a servant to the hand of her mistress, so our eyes are to the LORD our God, until he have mercy on us" Psalm 123:1-2 .

The first text occurred in the episode in which Jesus raises Lazarus, while in the second text, Jesus was on the verge of sealing the first stage of the plan of Redemption, dying on the cross. Analyzing the texts closely, it is clear that there is no THUS SAITH THE LORD, no command for the disciples or their church to pray with their eyes open. This is an extensive interpretation and an assumption, lifting your eyes is not a command to pray with your eyes open, although advocates of the infamous doctrine of praying with your eyes open maintain that it is, based on the following texts: "I lift up my eyes to the mountains; where will my help come from?" Psalms 121:1 .

Note that the expression lift up your eyes is not a command to pray with your eyes open. The expression “our eyes are fixed on the Lord” means that our understanding, our concentration must be fixed on the Creator. In another text (seen elsewhere) the Lord commands to lift up the heart to the Lord, with the same context as the previous text see: "Let us lift up our hearts together with our hands to God in heaven" Lamentations 3:1 . How can we lift our hearts to God in heaven? Indeed, the apostle Paul helps us to unravel the issue when he says: "The eyes of your hearts being enlightened, that you may know what is the hope of his calling, what are the riches of the glory of his inheritance in the saints" Ephesians 1:18. See Paul refers to illuminating the eyes of the heart, of course it's a metaphor, he's referring to giving knowledge, understanding, when the Bible speaks of lifting the eyes or the heart, the context is raising understanding, discernment, knowledge , see the following text when Daniel speaks of lifting up one's eyes to heaven as understanding coming or attaining knowledge: "But at the end of those days I, Nebuchadnezzar, lifted up my eyes to heaven, and understanding came again to me, and I blessed the Most High, and I praised and glorified him who lives forever, whose dominion is everlasting, and whose kingdom is from generation to generation" Daniel 4:34 .

Jesus himself, who says to pray with eyes open, teaches us to pray with eyes closed, according to the Scriptures. Jesus prayed prostrate on his face, therefore, he did not have his eyes fixed on heaven, much less open, as proof that he did not authorize his disciples to pray with their eyes open, verbis, the text: "Advancing a little, fell on his face, praying and saying, My Father, if it be possible, let this cup pass from me! Yet not as I will, but as you will" Matthew 26:39 .

At another time, after the resurrection, Jesus appears to his disciples who did not recognize him. An event that took place when they were on their way to Emmaus, they were talking about the latest events, and when they were talking Jesus himself approached and went with them. "His eyes were, as it were, prevented from recognizing him" Luke 24:16 . They knew that Jesus was dead, they forgot the words when he said that he would rise again, for this reason his eyes were closed so as not to recognize him. Standing in the midst of the disciples, Jesus blessed the bread. Only after asking for the blessings for the bread, did they open their eyes and recognize him, because they saw the mark of the nails in his hands, as proof that they asked for the blessings for the bread with their eyes closed."And it came to pass, as they were at table, as he took bread, I blessed it, and when he had broken it, he gave it to them; then their eyes were opened, and they recognized him; but he disappeared from their presence" Luke 24: 30-31 . When Jesus asked for the blessing for the bread, the disciples had their eyes closed, after the blessings, they opened their eyes and only recognized him because Jesus stretched out his hands to distribute the bread and not because he opened their understanding, they saw the marks of the nails recognizing that was the Master, according to White's teaching in the following terms:

The simple evening meal, consisting of bread, is readily prepared. It is placed before the Guest, who has taken his seat at the head of the table. He then stretches out his hands to bless the food. The disciples recoil in astonishment. Your Companion extends his hands in exactly the same way as the Master. They look again, and behold, they see the marks of the nails in his hands. Both immediately exclaim: It is the Lord! He rose from the dead.
Ellen G. White, The Desire of Ages, page 765

Notice that the disciples look again, that is, they look twice to notice the nail marks and recognize their Master. However, defenders of the doctrine of praying with eyes open, teach that the eyes of the disciples were closed when Jesus asked for blessings for the bread and opened after the blessing, because it is symbolic, it was a lack of understanding. Who opened their eyes because they received understanding and recognized Jesus.

Examining the Scriptures, it is clear that Jesus only begins to teach the Scriptures to the disciples after being recognized by them when they saw the marks on his hands, so he began to teach everything that was written about him, which was fulfilled according to Moses, the Psalms and the Prophets, verbis: "Then Jesus said to them, These are the words that I spoke to you while I was still with you; everything must be fulfilled that is written about me in the Law of Moses, in the Prophets and in the Psalms" Luke 24:43 . Then he opened their eyes of understanding to understand the Scriptures about Christ and his work and not to recognize Jesus. "Then he opened their minds to understand the Scriptures" Luke 24:45. It remains proven that the disciples were literally with their eyes closed when Jesus asked for the blessings for the bread, after opening their eyes they only recognized him when they saw the marks on their hands.

Another text used to support the doctrine of praying with open eyes is carved in the book of Peter. They claim that Peter was praying with his eyes open, about the sixth hour, or noon, indeed it is time for the church of God to pray, let's examine the text: "The next day, as they were going on their way, and being near the city, Peter on the housetop about the sixth hour to pray. He was hungry and wanted to eat, but while food was being prepared for him, an ecstasy came over him, and he saw heaven open and an object descending as if it were a great sheet, which was lowered to the earth by its four corners" Acts 10:9-11 .

What can be inferred from the text is that Peter was praying, it does not say whether his eyes were open or closed. In the midst of prayer he was seized by the Spirit of God in vision, then he saw heaven opened, and a great sheet with quadrupeds and birds, signifying that God had granted opportunity to the Gentiles, that the Jews would no longer be his peculiar people. The text does not say that Peter was praying with his eyes open, but that he saw heaven open because he was in vision, let's see Sister White's teachings:

Immediately after this interview with Cornelius the angel came to Peter, who, weakened and hungry from the journey, was praying on the terrace of his house. As he was praying, a vision was shown to him, and he saw heaven being opened and an object coming down like a great sheet, which was lowered to the earth by its four corners, containing all kinds of four-footed animals, reptiles of the earth, and birds of the air. .
Ellen G. White, History of Redemption, page 284

They also assert that Stephen was looking to heaven when he prayed in his last moments, therefore he was praying with his eyes open. Let us examine the aforementioned text: "But Stephen, filled with the Holy Spirit, looked up into heaven, and saw the glory of God and Jesus standing on his right hand, and said, Behold, I see the heavens opened, and the Son of Man, standing at the right hand of God" Acts 7:55-56 .

Stephen, who was in the Sanhedrin speaking to the priests at the time of his judgment, when he established connection between Christ and the prophecies, and spoke vehemently against the priests, saying, 'You stiff-necked and uncircumcised in heart and ears, you always resist the Spirit. Holy, just as your fathers did. They were furious and rushed at Stephen in fury. Knowing Estevão that he was going to die, he had this vision, which is said to be praying with his eyes open at the moment of death. Note that Stephen sees heaven opened in vision just before the stoning, according to Sister White, verbis:

The scene around him was lost to him; the portals of heaven were opened, and Stephen, looking, saw the glory of the courts of God, and Christ, as if he had just risen from his throne, ready to sustain his servant, who was about to suffer martyrdom for his name. When Stephen described the glorious scenes unfolded before him, it was more than his persecutors could sweat.
Ellen G. White, History of Redemption, pages 265-266

Note the text of sister white teaching, that Stephen was describing the wonderful scenes that his eyes beheld, so he was not praying with his eyes open, who prays is not narrating facts. Furthermore, kneeling prayer takes place after the vision ends, and at that time Scripture does not say that Stephen was praying with his eyes open. When the enraged priests sentenced him to death summarily, without even concluding the trial with a sentence. Read the text: "Then, kneeling down, he cried with a loud voice, Lord, lay not this sin against them! With these words he fell asleep" Acts 7:60 .

Finally, the text that refers to Balaam, where they argue that Balaam was praying with his eyes open, let's go to the text: "When Balaam lifted up his eyes, and saw Israel encamped according to their tribes, the Spirit of God came upon him. And he said, The word of Balaam the son of Beor, the word of a man with open eyes, the word of him who hears the sayings of God, who sees the sight of the Almighty, and falls down with open eyes" Numbers 24:2 -4. What is observed in this text is that Balaam is in vision, he looks at Israel encamped with the intention of cursing the chosen of the Lord, then, the Spirit of God came upon him and blessed the people. The Lord at that time had already departed from Balaam on account of his idolatry in earning a profit offered by Balak, Balaam was backslidden. What does the Lord want to teach when he says prostrate yourself with your eyes open? Is it an order to pray with eyes open? If it was a command, it was for the backsliders, because at that time the Lord had already rejected Balaam because of his apostasy. God considered him his adversary, because the way of Balaam was wicked before the Lord, read the text:"Then the Lord opened Balaam's eyes, and he saw the angel of the Lord standing in the way, with his drawn sword in his hand; wherefore he bowed his head and fell on his face to the ground. Lord said to him, Why have you beaten the ass three times? Behold, I have come out as your adversary, because your way is perverse before me" Numbers 22:31-32 .

It is hard to imagine God commanding prayer with open eyes, instituting a doctrine through an apostate man. In fact, when God says man with his eyes open, again, he is referring to the knowledge, science, understanding of the Most High, which Balaam lacked at that time. Read the following text to sediment this assertion: "The word of him that hears the sayings of God and knows the knowledge of the Most High; of him that sees the vision of the Almighty, and falls down, but with open eyes" Numbers 24:16 . When He says, prostrate yourself with open eyes, he is not talking about literally opening your eyes, the context is prostrating yourself with understanding, with the knowledge of the Most High. He was blinded by understanding.

It is opportune to point out that, if we take the Biblical texts literally, we are going to get complicated with the Scriptures which says: "Who enabled us to be ministers of a new covenant, not of the letter, but of the spirit; for the letter kills, but the spirit gives life" II Corinthians 3:6 . If we analyze other Biblical texts, contrary to the texts already analyzed, to the letter, we will conclude that God forbids us to raise our eyes to heaven or even to the Creator. "Take heed that you do not lift up your eyes to the heavens, and when you see the sun and the moon and the stars, that is, all the host of heaven, be tempted to bow down to them and serve those things which the Lord your God, has repeated it to all people under all the heavens" Deuteronomy 4:19 .

In another passage, Moses hid his face because he was afraid to look at God. "And he said, I am the God of thy Father, the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob. Moses hid his face, for he was afraid to look upon God" Exodus 3:6 . Here would be a beautiful opportunity for Moses to pray to the Lord with his eyes open, however he hid his face, he certainly closed his eyes.

What to say about the Pharisee and the publican, perhaps the Pharisee was praying with his eyes open, exalted, while the publican humbly prayed with his eyes closed because he did not raise his eyes to heaven, he asked the Lord for mercy, in the following terms: " The pubican, standing afar off, dared not even lift up his eyes to heaven, but beat his breast, saying, O God, be merciful to me, a sinner! Luke 18:13 .

In fact, Satan takes advantage of the existence of texts in the Bible that are difficult to understand, due to the limitation of human sinful nature and not being essential to the plan of Redemption. The enemy will seek to fill hearts not sanctified by Christ, who seek holiness by human effort and ability (self-righteousness); raising points that are difficult to understand to sow discord and doubts, freeing them from the Plan of Redemption and dependence on Christ. The church of Christ should not occupy its time on unimportant points, following biblical teaching, from the writing of Paul advising the church, when he said, "Even so avoid useless and profane speeches, for those who use them will pass away to ungodliness still." greater" II Timothy 2:16 ."And reject foolish and uneducated disputes, knowing that they produce strife" II Timothy 2:23 . "Who ever learn, and can never come to the knowledge of the truth" II Timothy 3:7 . There are passages in the Bible that will never be clearly understood by us due to our sinful nature and not being relevant to the understanding of the Plan of Redemption, therefore, the church has a duty to focus its energies on relevant texts for the improvement and preparation of the church fed by the divine nature of Cytus (Christ's righteousness). It will lead to sophistry and disputes; for innumerable points of contention will arise. Satan was defeated, his onslaught was not successful, given that the disciples and martyrs who took over the direction of the church had the greatest weapon against Satan's attack and against fanaticism, assumption and comparisons disguised as holiness. They possessed the divine nature of Christ in their hearts, therefore, they were protected from deception, sophistry, and doctrinal error.
