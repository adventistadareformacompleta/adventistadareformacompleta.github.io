THE SYMBOLISM OF CLOTHING

It is known that in the beginning, "So God created man in his own image, in the image of God he created him; male and female he created them" Genesis 1:27 . "And God said, Let us make man in our image, after our likeness; let them have dominion over the fish of the sea, over the birds of the air, over the livestock, over all the earth, and over every creeping thing that creeps on the earth" Genesis 1:26 . God created man in his image according to his likeness. It was the likeness of God in the sense of being endowed with the divine nature of the Creator, Adam enjoyed the splendor of God's glory in character, virtue, and love. According to reports from Sister White, who says:

The first Adam was created a pure, innocent being, without a stain of sin upon him, he was the image of God.
Ellen G. White, SDABC, Vol 5, page 1128 (Letter to Baker, 1895)

Adam and Eve did not know sin, they were dressed in the robe of God's righteousness, which provided perfection. However, the conception of sin took away from him the garments made on the heavenly looms and came to know human weaknesses, the original sin that separates us from God, generating fruits of rebellion and transgression of God's law. In the sayings of Sister White.

It was possible for Adam, before the fall, to form a righteous character by obedience to the law of God. But he failed to do so, and because of his sin our nature is fallen, and we cannot become righteous. Since we are sinful, unholy, we cannot perfectly obey a holy law. We have no righteousness in ourselves with which we could satisfy the demands of God's law.
Ellen G. White, Steps to Christ, page 62

Sin brought a drastic change in the lives of the Edenic couple and their future descendants. Adam and Eve were committed by a new, sinful, self-satisfying, selfish and rebellious nature (original sin), man ceased to be the temple of the Spirit of God, he was withdrawn to the divine nature that made him holy and similar to the Creator. Man knew and lived in iniquity, and it is precisely "your iniquities that have separated between you and your God; and your sins have hid his face from you so that he will not hear" Isaiah 59:2. With the sinful nature, that is, original sin commanding our feelings and reason, there was separation, and God could no longer dwell in our hearts, nor speak face to face with man, the temple of the Holy Spirit was occupied, stained by the sin. Now, Adam and Eve no longer took pleasure in obeying and satisfying God's will out of love, but in satisfying their own will.

Due to sin, humanity ceased to be the temple of God. Darkened and contaminated by sin, the heart of man no longer revealed the glory of the Godhead.
Ellen G. White, Desire of Ages, page 161

They lost the divine nature and became "we are all as an unclean thing, and all our righteousnesses as filthy rags, we all wither as a leaf, and our iniquities as the wind take us away" Isaiah 64:6 . And all this "For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God" Romans 3:23 .

In the above texts, we learn that we are filthy to God, because we have lost his glory, his divine nature, however, we need not despair, in Steps to Christ, page 62 Sister White gives us comfort when she says that "Christ has provided for us a way to escape" . The means of escape was the conception of the Plan of Redemption.

Adam and Eve, when they were created, had a knowledge of God's law; were familiar with its claims against you; his precepts were written in his heart. When man fell through transgression, the law was not changed, but a plan was established that would remedy the situation by bringing man back to obedience.
Ellen G. White, Patriarchs and Prophets, page 377

The center of the Plan of Redemption is Christ who has a preeminent role, reconciling us with God, taking away our sinful nature and lending us his divine nature. As Sister White tells us:

Christ brought his divinity to earth, veiled by humanity, to rescue man from his lost condition. Human nature is vile, and man's character must be changed before he can come into harmony with the pure and holy in the immortal kingdom of God. This transformation is the new birth.
Ellen G. White, Signs of the Times, 11/5/1883

After all, what does all this have to do with clothing? To answer this question, it is necessary to ask, How did Adam and Eve dress before sin? Could it be that they were naked, didn't they have any clothes? This is what everyone says, that Adam and Eve were completely naked and did not recognize their nakedness because they had no evil.
But this is a mistake, although the Bible says, "Now both the man and the woman were naked and were not ashamed" Genesis 2:25 . Note, that before sin Adam and Eve had no need to wear artificial clothes like our fabrics, which are symbols, however, they were not naked, as many think, were they dressed in what? Scripture itself answers: "Bendizei, ó minha alma, ao Senhor! Senhor Deus meu, Tu és magnificentíssimo; está vestido de glória e majestade. Ele se cobre de luz como de um vestido, estende os céus como de uma cortina" Salmos 104:1-2. As vestimentas confeccionadas nos teares celestiais são de luz e Glória do Altíssimo, veja que o Senhor os cobriu com uma vestimenta de luz, tal como veste os anjos, representando a natureza divina que os motivava, na mesma esteira são ensinamentos da irmã White.

Esse casal, que não tinha pecados, não fazia uso de vestes artificiais; estavam revestidos de uma cobertura de luz e glória, tal como a usam os anjos. Enquanto viveram em obediência a Deus, esta veste de luz continuou a envolvê-los.
Ellen G. White, 1989, Patriarcas e Profetas, pág 29

It can be seen that the light of the glory of the Most High or his divine nature enveloped them, keeping them pure and holy, while they were covered by this light they would be protected, they would love and obey the Creator. Knowing this, Satan plotted to overthrow the holy couple with the hope that God would forgive and he himself, thought about the possibility of being benefited by the forgiveness granted by God to Adam and Eve. Then Satan poisoned Eve's heart, inducing her to eat of the fruit, saying that they would be equal to God, and offered the fruit, "when the woman saw that the tree was good for food, pleasant to the eyes, and a tree to be desired to make one wise ." And she took some of the fruit from her and ate, and she also gave it to her husband, and he ate. Then the eyes of both of them were opened; and when they saw that they were naked, they sewed fig leaves together and made girdles for themselves." Genesis 3:6-7 .

The eyes of both were opened, they saw that they were naked and they sewed fig leaves together to cover themselves, read, they lost their divine nature, and began to live with the sinful nature, original sin, a feeling of self-satisfaction, rebellious and selfish, the doubt that Satan sowed in Eve's heart blossomed and conceived original sin, now the holy couple belonged to Satan and not to God. When they lost the divine nature, they knew not only good, but evil, man became prone to evil. The light that covered Adam and Eve, was justice or divine nature, and this was taken away, so Adam and Eve's eyes were opened to evil and they realized that they were naked. "And the Lord God called to the man and asked him, Where are you? He answered, I heard your voice in the garden, and because I was naked I was afraid and hid myself." Genesis 3:9-10 . Adam hid himself from God when he was stripped of the divine nature, because the sinful nature dominated him, and God does not support that nature. Adam can no longer see the face of God, because the glory of the divine nature annihilates the sinful nature and its possessor, now, Adam and Eve could only hear the voice of God, so he was afraid.

The robe of light that had surrounded them has now disappeared. Under a sense of guilt and the loss of their divine covering, a tremor took possession of them, and they sought to cover their exposed forms.
Ellen G. White, History of Redemption, page 37

What did God do to cover them? "The Lord God made a garment of skins for Adam and his wife, and clothed them" Genesis 3:21 . Was there symbolism in those robes? Certainly. They lost the garments of light of glory from the Most High. The artificial fur garments received by this couple are symbols of the righteousness of Christ that they have lost, with which he will cover all the contrite ones, whose sinners are forgiven. Now it is necessary to be born again, we have seen in the writings of White 's writing , that the transformation or exchange of the sinful nature for the divine nature of Christ is the new birth, the primary objective .of the Redemption Plan. When we submit our will, slave to sin, to Christ's control, he produces within the Christian a character and life similar to himself. This is how sinners are born again. So our sins are ascribed to Christ as his, his righteousness is ascribed to us. The garments that God made and gave to Adam after sin symbolize the lost divine nature, it is necessary for the vicarious lamb to restore the lost nature, this is the only way for us to contemplate the face of God and live. That's why God's people could not wear woolen and linen clothing. "Thou shalt not clothe thee with woolen material and linen together" Deuteronomy 22:11 .

The New Testament will clarify the symbolism of this passage, which represents linen, "And it was granted to her that she should be arrayed in fine linen, clean and bright; for the fine linen is the righteousness of the saints" Revelation 19:8 . And the prophet Isaiah points out the meaning of wool. "For we are all like an unclean thing, and all our righteousnesses are like filthy rags; and we all wither like a leaf, and our iniquities, like the wind, have taken us away" Isaiah 64:6. According to the Plan of Redemption, Christ will redeem sinners by reconciling them to the Creator, and the only way to do that is by exchanging the sinful nature (wool) for the divine nature (linen). Note that not even Moses can see the face of God, even Moses receiving enough divine nature still kept the sinful nature, which will be removed when we receive eternal righteousness, if Moses beheld the face of God he would be fulminated by the glory of the Creator who does not support nature. sinful. Uzzah was struck down when he touched the Ark of the Covenant, because of his sinful nature, which, as Isaiah says, separates God and man. When Christ clothes us with eternal righteousness, the symbol of which is the garment, we will have divine nature again and we can see God face to face, the nature of God will be the same as we wear,

It will be observed, that these passages show two natures, the divine nature of Christ or righteousness of the saints, represented by the linen, while the wool is prefigured by the self-righteousness, the sinful nature inherited from Adam. The search for perfection or sanctification by self-effort, without Christ, denotes that original sin was not removed, no matter the size of the human effort to purify oneself, some understand that perfection translates into wearing clothes of only one color, but, to God it is filthy rags, so Christ taught us, "No one takes a piece of a new garment and puts it on an old garment; for he will tear the new, and the patch will not fit the old" Luke 5:36 .

The Savior clearly teaches that we cannot mend our filthy rags of self-righteousness with the righteousness of Christ, the two natures do not mix, Christ must exchange the sinful nature with His, to reconcile us to God. In view, that the sinful nature (original sin separates us from God Isaiah 59:2 ).
See, what garments symbolize the righteousness of Jesus that will one day be restored, which is why the Hebrews could not wear woolen and linen clothes. The precept of dress was intended to remind the Hebrews of the necessity of the divine nature of the Messiah and of the duty of obedience to God's commandments motivated by divine love. Without the nature of Christ imputed in our hearts, we are not able to obey anything, we will only be able to feed our carnal passions, some explicitly, others in a veiled way.

It is evident, that the garments worn by the church of God, are symbols of the righteousness of Christ, with which he will cover every contrite one, whose sins are forgiven, "I will greatly rejoice in the Lord, my soul will rejoice in my God, for he has clothed me with the garments of salvation, he has covered me with the robe of righteousness, as a bridegroom decked himself with a garland, and as a bride adorned with her jewels" Isaiah 61:10 .

Before sin, man covered himself with a garment of light and glory, having lost this garment, the plan of redemption was established to rescue the penitent sinner to the fold of the sheep of Christ. God designed our garments to be a remembrance of the heavenly garment, divine nature, with which he will finally cover the redeemed, fulfilling his promise: "He that overcometh shall be clothed in white robes, and I will in no wise blot out his name from the book of life; and I will confess his name before my father and before his angels" Revelation 3:5 .

To remove any remaining doubts on the subject, namely, that clothing represents the divine nature of Christ, let's sediment this understanding with a THUS SAITH THE LORD. The Jews did not mix wool with linen, because they knew what they represented.

Those Israelites who would conscientiously refuse to mix wool and linen in their daily garments, and aim in this the lesson which God designed to teach them, would also turn from sin. All their apparel, made of only one quality of cloth, continually reminded them of the perfect garment of Christ's righteousness bestowed on the faithful.
HASKELL, Stephen N. The Cross and its Shadow, page 282

Clothing is a precept of paramount importance, it symbolizes the righteousness of Christ that will be added to the faithful, we cannot neglect this precept of vital importance for the life of the church of God. Note the teachings of the inspired word: "In the same way women dress themselves in honest attire, with modesty and modesty, not with braids, or with gold, or pearls, or costly garments, but as women make a profession of serving the God" 1 Timothy 2:9-10. The reason the Scriptures forbid unnecessary adornments and idols in clothing is not only forbidding the love of fashions, but mainly because they represent the divine nature of Christ, therefore, they cannot be contaminated by ornaments, prints, lace, jewelry, silver ornaments. , gold, jewelry or any other idol, these things overshadow the symbol of the divine nature of Christ, and the Christian starts to admire the beauty of the artificial garment, losing the object, the true meaning. To receive the divine nature lost by Adam, the faithful need to dress like the saints of the past, according to the sayings of the Scriptures, as befits women who make a profession of serving God, who were aware of this truth.

Furthermore, we cannot forget that the precepts of God were not given in vain, but each precept has its function, protecting the church as barriers of containment against the corrupting influences of the world and for the church to find in the obedience of each precept to life . This is the teaching that we take from the Scriptures: "And he said to them, Apply your hearts to all the words that I testify to you today, that ye may command your children to keep and do and do all things which are written in this law; For it was not in vain that they were prescribed for you, but that each of you might find life in them, and, putting them into practice, may dwell a long time in the land which you are going to possess after the Jordan.” Deuteronomy 32:46-47 .

The precept of dress in particular is intended to protect God's people. "And to distinguish between the holy and the profane, and between the unclean and the clean, And to teach the children of Israel all the statutes which the LORD hath spoken to them through Moses" Leviticus 10:10-11 . Ministers, Pastors and leaders of the Church of God, are in the role of priests and have a mission: "These consecrated men will guide my people to distinguish between the holy and the profane, and will teach them to discern between what is pure and what is unclean" Ezekiel 44:23 . It can be seen that the text written by Sister White complements the purpose of the precept of clothing, because it was introduced into the body of doctrines of the church:

To protect God's people from the corrupting influence of the world, as well as to promote physical and moral health, the dress reform was introduced among us. It was not intended to be a yoke of bondage, but a blessing; not to increase work, but to save work; to save expenses, not to add to clothing expenses. It would distinguish between the people of God and the world, and thus would serve as a barrier against their fashions and follies.

Shouldn't there be in their clothing a differentiation from the clothing of the world? Should not God's people, who are his peculiar treasure, seek even in their dress to glorify God? And should they not be an example in the matter of dress, and by their simple style reprove the pride, vanity, and extravagance of those who profess the truth but are worldly and pleasure-loving? Today, the garments of those who represent Jesus in everything are also sacred, symbolizing his justice. The church that is ashamed of this precept is denying or rejecting the righteousness of Christ. Aaron was given holy robes to officiate in the tabernacle, while the people were given laws about the blue tassels on their robes so that when they saw it they would remember the commandments of the Lord: "They also made the finely woven garments of blue and purple and scarlet material to in the sanctuary, and they also made the holy garments for Aaron, as the Lord had commanded Moses" Exodus 39:1 .

The precept of dress is important, which is why God commands those being baptized to reform in matters of dress, as well as all believers when they come into His presence at meetings:

One point upon which those who embrace the faith should be instructed is dress—a subject that should be carefully considered on the part of new converts. Do they reveal vanity when it comes to clothing? Caress the pride of heart? Idolatry practiced in the matter of dress is moral disease; it must not be introduced into the new life. In most cases submission to the claims of the gospel requires a decisive change in dress.
Ellen G. White, Testimonies, 2, 1985, page 393

The Lord does not accept Baptism, unless the candidate is in perfect harmony with all the Commandments and Precepts of his Church, especially clothing, he cannot enter the new life with foreign garments of self-righteousness: "On the day of the Lord's sacrifice , I will punish the officials, and the king's sons, and all who wear foreign garments" Zephaniah 1:8 .

But, it will be cast out, God does not support the sinful nature, see what Jesus will ask those who reject the wedding garments of their divine nature: "And he asked him: Friend, how did you come in here without the wedding garment? The man was silent. The king said to those who served, Bind him hand and foot, and cast him out into the darkness; there will be weeping and gnashing of teeth. For many are called, but few are chosen" Matthew 22:12-14 .

Baptism means burial of sins, when the candidate descends into the water, is burying all sins, the old man dies and rises with Christ new man, raised with the righteousness of Christ, a new creature, born again of water (baptism) , and of the Spirit, the divine nature of Christ, imputing divine love in the heart to obey all the dictates of the Creator. In conversation with Nicodemus: "Jesus answered and said to him, Truly, truly, I say to you, unless one is born again, he cannot see the kingdom of God. Nicodemus said to him, How can a man be born when he is old? Can he enter his mother's womb again and be born? Jesus answered, Truly, truly, I say to you, unless one is born of water and the Spirit, he cannot enter the kingdom of God" John 3:3 -5.
