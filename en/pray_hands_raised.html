RAISED HANDS PRAYER

It is glimpsed in Sister White's writings,

that prayer is the key in the hands of faith to open the barn of heaven, and above all we must not neglect secret prayer, for it is the life of the soul.
Ellen G. White, Questions I Would Ask Sister White, page 144

Just like the incense burned on the altar located before the veil that separated the holy from the most holy and entered the holy of holies, where the Shekinah (presence of God) was on the lid of the ark of the covenant, the prayer ascends to the Father with the incense of righteousness of Christ. God is holy, the place where we meet the Creator is sacred, he himself said that services should be celebrated in the most absolute order, descent and reverence. Then the Lord commanded Moses before the burning bush, "Take off your sandals from your feet, for the place on which you stand is holy ground" Acts 7:33 . Not only did Moses receive this command, but the commander of the army that succeeded him, Joshua, the Lord said, "Put off your sandals from your feet, for the place where you stand is holy!" And so did Joshua" Joshua 5:.

Faced with such holiness of cults, where we meet the Lord, should we communicate with the Creator in any way? Or has God himself established the way for us to communicate with him? Surely the Lord requires a proper way to address him in prayer, Jesus taught his disciples how they should pray, he said: "And when ye pray, ye shall not be like the hypocrites; for they love to pray standing in the synagogues and in the corners. the marketplaces, to be seen of men. Truly I say to you, they have already received their reward" Matthew 6:5. In his teachings Jesus warned the disciples not to pray standing, nor to pray or do good works in order to be seen by men and praised for their pseudo model of Christianity, that is, religious formalism or legalism. In order not to leave any doubt about the text presented, Sister White reports:

Prayer is the opening of the heart to God as to a friend. Prayer does not bring God down to us, but lifts us up to Him. When Jesus walked the earth, he taught His disciples how to pray.
Ellen G. White, Questions I Would Ask Sister White, page 143

In the book of Deuteronomy, Moses said that the Lord would raise up a prophet like him, that prophet is Jesus, see the passage: "Verily Moses said, The Lord God will raise up for you a prophet like me from among your brethren. ye shall listen to all that I say to you" Acts 3:22 . We have seen that Jesus taught his disciples how they should pray, however, before Christ, Moses taught us the proper position to present ourselves before the Lord in prayer, especially, in a moment of danger fomented by the rebellion and murmuring of the Hebrew people. Sister White relates the cause of the Amalekite attack when the people left Egypt in the following passage:

A new danger threatened them now. Because of their murmuring against Him, the Lord allowed them to be attacked by their enemies. The Amalekites, a fierce and warlike tribe that inhabited that region, went out against them, and wounded him who, faint and tired, had remained in the rear.
Ellen G. White, Patriarchs and Prophets, p 305

Faced with the fierce enemy, Moses ordered Joshua to prepare the army for battle, however, Israel could not win the battle against an enemy stronger and more prepared for war at that moment, surely the Lord would fight in favor of Israel, then, came the order, Moses should pray with his hands raised, symbol of the dependence we have on the Creator to receive the desired blessings. When Moses lowered his hands the blessings were withheld, when Moses raised his hands the Lord favored them, let's see these reports in the following texts:

"And Amalek came, and fought against Israel at Rephidim. And Moses said to Joshua, Choose us men, and go out, fight against Amalek: tomorrow I will stand on the top of the hill, and the rod of God will be in my hand. And Joshua did
as Moses had told him, fighting against Amalek: but Moses, Aaron, and Hur went up to the top of the hill: and it came to pass, when Moses lifted up his hand, Israel prevailed: but when he let down his hand, Amalek prevailed.
But Moses' hands were heavy, therefore they took a stone, and put it under him, to sit on it: and Aaron and Hur held up his hands, one on one side, and the other on the other; so his hands remained steady until the sun went down. And so Joshua smote Amalek and his people with the edge of the sword" Exodus 17:8-13.

Surely, there was a special reason for Moses to lift up his hands to God in prayer. According to the text, when Moses' hands were up, Israel prevailed, when Amalek lowered, he prevailed. As we lift our hands in prayer before the Lord, we are demonstrating our dependence and trust in Him alone, we are dependent and needy on the divine nature of Christ, Sister White reports the need for God's servants to trust and recognize dependence on the divine nature that replaces our sinful nature, as Sister White reports:

This act of Moses, stretching out his hands to God, was to teach Israel that, as they put their trust in God, clung to His strength, and exalted His throne, He would fight for them and subdue their enemies. However, when they lost confidence in His power and trusted in their own strength, they would be even weaker than their enemies, who had no knowledge of God, and these were to prevail over them. Then "Joshua smote Amalek, and his people, with the edge of the sword." exo. 17:13. And Moses built an altar, and called his name, the Lord is my banner. If the children of Israel had not murmured against the Lord, He would not have allowed their enemies to make war with them.
Ellen G. White, History of Redemption pg 133-134

The prophet like Moses, Jesus, taught the disciples to pray. In the book of Luke we have an example of Christ blessing his disciples with uplifted hands. "Then he took them to Bethany, and lifting up his hands he blessed them" Luke 24:50 . What is inferred from the text is that Jesus stretched out his hands to bestow blessings on his disciples, in the same way, we should extend our hands when we pray asking or thanking Christ and his Father for their love and mercy. According to the Scriptures we must not only lift our hands before Jesus, but bend our knees. "Therefore God also highly exalted him to the highest place, and gave him the name that is above every other name; that at the name of Jesus every knee should bow, of those in heaven and on earth and under the earth." Philippians 2:9-10 . See another example of Jesus lifting his hands before the Father on behalf of the sinner. Sister White presents the One to whom we lift up our hands, lifting hers also in interceding for us before the Father in the presence of the holy angels. Read:

The subject of the sanctuary and the investigative judgment must be clearly understood by the people of God. All need for themselves a knowledge of the position and work of their great High Priest. Indeed, it will be impossible for them to exercise the faith that is essential at this time, or to occupy the position that God desires to entrust to them... Jesus does not justify their sins, but presents their repentance and faith, [Hebrews, 8:3 ] and, claiming pardon for them, lifts up his wounded hands before the Father and the holy angels, [Matthew, 10:32, 33; Revelation 3:5] saying, "I know them by name. I have engraved them in the palm of My hands."
Ellen G. White, The Great Controversy, page 484

Moses also provided us with valuable teaching by praying with hands uplifted, proving forcefully that God only heard prayer and favored the petitioner when his hands were raised.
Far away from the time of Moses, and a few centuries after the death of Christ, there was a young Christian man of about 14 or 15 years old named Potito. Agnes, the daughter of the Roman Emperor Antony Pius, was possessed by a demon that tormented her a lot, so the Emperor who hated Christians sent for Potito to cure his daughter, unaware of the fact that the young man was a Christian. When Potito arrived, the emperor asked who he was,

he replied: __ I am a Christian. The emperor was furious, however the desire to see his daughter cured prevailed. Potito asked the emperor if Jesus cast the devil out of his daughter, would he believe in the Christian God, the emperor said yes, “Well,” said Potito, “if your daughter is healed, will you believe in the God I believe in? After a few seconds of hesitation, Antonio replied: — I will believe. It was a false promise; he did not intend to fulfill it. But God, who reads the secrets of the heart, made Potito see the emperor's hypocrisy and the judgment already prepared for him. Staring firmly at Antony, the noble boy spoke with majesty and strength: — False Emperor! You were weighed in the balance and found wanting. Thy heart is hardened and unrepentant. But that these people present here may believe in God, I will release, in the name of Jesus Christ, your daughter from the spirit that torments her. Let her be brought.
O'Reilly, 2005, The Martyrs of the Coliseum, page 86

The girl was cured, Potito was led to worship the Roman god Apollo, when he arrived before the statue he prayed to the Lord and the statue was pulverized, defeated and feeling deceived the Emperor condemned Potito to death, throwing him in the Roman Coliseum to be devoured by wild beasts, see how Potito prayed and was heard by the Lord:

Potito raised his arms, turned his eyes to heaven, and prayed aloud: - Oh, Lord Jesus Christ, our King and Redeemer!..
O'Reilly, 2005, The Martyrs of the Coliseum, page 108
The scene was both singular and beautiful; Potito kneeling in the midst of wild beasts, with hands and eyes raised to heaven, in prayer. It even seemed that the animals were afraid to make noise, lest they disturb him in his communication with Him who was the Creator and Lord of both the boy and theirs.
O'Reilly, 2005, The Martyrs of the Coliseum, page 86

Potito was a Christian martyr, he lived a few centuries after Christ. It is observed that he imitated the position of Moses, Ezra, Solomon and Jesus when he prayed on his knees and hands raised, the Lord heard his prayer preventing the wild beasts from devouring him. Therefore, we can assume, that the early church prayed on its knees and hands up as a rule.
Another illuminating example for remnant church members praying with uplifted hands is carved in the following text: "And Ezra praised the Lord, the great God: and all the people answered, Amen! Amen! Lifting up their hands; and they bowed down and worshiped. the Lord with his face to the ground" Nehemiah 8:6 .

Realizing the apostasy of the people, contracting marriages with the women of the heathen nations, near the evening service, Ezra tore his garments indignant at the apostasy and also taught us how we should pray to the Creator at decisive moments, "And near the Evening sacrifice, I rose from my affliction, having torn my garment and my robe, and I fell on my knees, and stretched out my hands to the Lord my God" Nehemiah 8:6 .

Note that Ezra like the other servants of the Most High God had as a rule to hold worship in the morning and at sunset, today it is the domestic services held by the remnant church, the prayers go up like incense. "Let my prayer go up before Your face like incense, and let the lifting up of my hands be like the evening sacrifice" Psalm 141:2 . Let's learn from this remarkable man of God the correct position of praying before God in services. "At the time of the evening offering I arose from my humiliation, and with my tunic and robe torn, I fell on my knees and stretched out my hands to the Lord my God" Ezra 9:5 .

A few centuries after the time of Ezra, David desired to build a house for the Lord, an intention consolidated by Solomon who built a temple worthy of worship to the Most High God. When the temple was completed, Solomon at its inauguration also left us a legacy of how we should worship the Almighty in the temple, in official and private worship. "And it came to pass, when Solomon had made an end of this prayer and this supplication unto the Lord, that he was kneeling down, and with his hands stretched out toward heaven, that he rose up from before the altar of the Lord" 1 Kings 8:54 (2 Chronicles 6: 13). According to the text, Solomon was praying on his knees and hands up in the temple. What prevents people from praying on their knees and hands up is the sinful, self-indulgent and irreverent nature. Some religious leaders go so far as to drive out members who bow their knees and lift their hands in prayer in church. Whereas, the servants of God who absorb the divine nature from Christ are compelled to love and obey the commandments and precepts of God. They are endowed with pleasure and joy in falling down and lifting their hands before their God in prayer. One thing is for sure, no one will go to heaven during the millennium or will live on the restored earth endowed with a sinful nature. The secret of salvation lies in receiving the divine nature of Christ (Holy Spirit) by obeying all the commandments and precepts out of love,"For Solomon had made a platform of brass, five cubits long, and five cubits wide, and three cubits high, and he had set it in the middle of the court, and he stood on it, and knelt down. in the presence of all the congregation of Israel, and stretched out his hands toward heaven" 2 Chronicles 6:13 .

Praying on your knees and hands raised in the sanctuary was not a practice peculiar to Solomon, but of all who presented themselves before the Most High, David, father of Solomon was the composer of the psalms and recommends us to lift our hands in the sanctuary, "Raise your hands in the sanctuary and bless the Lord" Psalm 134:2 The psalmist writes and teaches incisively, that prayers are heard by God when the speaker lifts his hands to the heavenly sanctuary. "Hear the voice of my supplications, when I cry unto thee, when I lift up my hands toward thy sanctuary" Psalm 28:2 .

Christ is in the heavenly sanctuary, where he makes intercession for the penitent sinner, so that is where we should lift our hands when we pray. The church, domestic services and the interior of our rooms, are appropriate places to pray on our knees and hands raised, according to the word of God. "Every prayer, every supplication that any man of all your people Israel makes, each one knowing the wound of his heart, and stretching out his hands toward this house, then hear thou in heaven, the seat of thy dwelling, and forgive, and do, and give to each one according to all his ways, and according to his heart, for you alone know the hearts of all the children of men" I Kings 8:38, 39 .

The Holy Scriptures tell us that the fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom, however, hearts fed by the sinful nature are resistant to the orders of the Most High, they prefer to cross their arms when they pray in comfort and irreverence. These people, the Bible calls fools for refusing to bow to the truth, praying with folded arms, let's see: "A fool folds his hands and eats his own flesh" Ecclesiastes 4:5 .

Opponents of this truth argue that the lifting of the hands to heaven is only a force of expression, based on the text of the book of Lamentations: "Let us lift up our hearts together with our hands to God in heaven" Lamentations 3:41 . In the text in question, the Lord commands us to lift up our hearts with our hands before Him. The raising of hands at the time of prayer to the sanctuary, where God is, is fully possible, indeed it is a thus saith the Lord. Doubt hangs over the argument of the possibility of lifting our hearts to God, how can we lift our hearts to God? Is this possible? The answer is yes, we just need to understand what the Lord wants to teach us with this expression. By introduction, we will analyze the following text:"If you prepare your heart, and stretch out your hands to God; if you cast away iniquity from your hand, and allow unrighteousness not to dwell in your tent, then you will lift up your face without blemish, you will be safe, and you will not be afraid." Job 11:13-15 .

When the word says that we should lift up our hearts, it is implied that we should lift up our minds, our feelings nourished by the divine nature of Christ, utterly clean and pure. The heart is clean and purified, when the sinful nature is replaced by the divine nature, then we can and should lift our hearts and our clean hands to the sanctuary of grace in prayer, to be heard and answered by the Lord. God rebukes idolaters, even among his people, if the heart is not purified by the divine nature of Christ, it is no use to pray on your knees or lift your hands because the heart is contaminated by the sinful nature that separates us from God, and separated from God. , the poor soul forgets the Creator and goes to prostrate himself after the idols stretching out his hands to them."If we have forgotten the name of our God, and have stretched out our hands to a strange god, has not God realized him, who knows the secrets of hearts?" Psalms 44:20-21 . In the past, Isaac made the mistake of worshiping idols while officiating before the Lord. When they prayed with outstretched hands in apostasy, the Lord did not hear them, on the contrary he hid his face because the hands were not clean, they were stained with blood and apostasy, therefore, the heart was not completely purified. "Therefore, when you spread out your hands, I hide my eyes from you; and though you multiply your prayers, I will not hear them, for your hands are full of blood" Isaiah 1:15 .

Carefully observe the condition required by the Lord for answering prayer, if we will cleanse our hearts with the divine nature of Christ, and stretch out clean hands to Him. The opposite will be for those who are not pure in heart or clean in hands, they may even raise their hands, but the Lord will not hear them. When the speaker refuses to prostrate himself before the Lord and extend his hands in prayer, he is already proving his rebelliousness and irreverence, so the heart is not pure, it is no use saying that the Lord only wants the heart, because the heart in this state is not clean. "The good man out of the good treasure of the heart brings forth good, and the evil man out of the evil treasure brings forth evil; for out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaks" Luke 6:45. All men are born evil (original sin) and separated from God. Man can only be good and be reconciled to God if he is nourished by the divine nature of Christ, deriving good things from the fruit of this nature, such as love, submission and obedience to God's commandments.

In view of all the above, it is clear that this doctrinal point is not based on isolated points, on the contrary, robust evidence was presented of the veracity of the duty to pray on our knees and hands raised, however, we know that many opponents will twist texts to nullify this truth, to make use of exceptions to deceive souls by saying that it is the rule. So that there is no doubt left, let's analyze a text containing ONE THUS SAITH THE LORD in the New Testament. Here is the text: "I desire therefore that men in every place pray, lifting up holy hands , without wrath or strife" 1 Timothy 2:8. When Paul says "I will therefore that men pray with hands uplifted", he is clearly saying I command men to pray with hands uplifted, this is the true context, therefore it is one thus saith the Lord. The early church had the custom of praying on their knees and hands raised, we saw the example of Potito, now let's see the example of the martyr Ignatius, who followed the teachings of Paul and was happy to be condemned to death as the renowned apostle, check out the text:

When Ignatius heard his sentence, he fell to his knees, and raising his arms to heaven, cried out in an ecstasy of joy: "- Oh Lord, I thank you for having honored me with the most precious sign of your charity, and allowed me to be chained for thy love, as was the apostle Paul..." His crime was "to carry within himself Jesus crucified."
O'Reilly, 2005, The Martyrs of the Coliseum, page 46

We have another striking example proving that the primitive Christians prayed on their knees and hands raised, an event that took place on the first and January of the year 404 when Telemachus invaded the Roman Coliseum to prevent the slaughter of gladiators, the people threw fragments from the benches in an attempt to reach the charitable Elder, succumbing to one more of the Christian martyrs:

Finally, unable to control themselves, they tore the seats and smashed the benches; in a few minutes the air was filled with fragments of benches and pavement, thrown from all sides, aiming to reach Telemachus. He knelt down, and stretching his arms to heaven, offered his life for the conversion of the great theater of tyranny.
O'Reilly, 2005, The Martyrs of the Coliseum, page 241

Last but not least, it is worth reporting an example of two hundred and sixty Christians praying in the Coliseum, condemned to death by Emperor Claudius; It is observed, the church praying on its knees and hands in position, as it was the custom of the primitive brethren to pray:

But not so in the scene before us in the Coliseum. For hours, the sighs of the dying mingled with the laughter of the archers. One group is on its knees, hands folded in prayer; the whir of the flying arrows is their death knell. They fall one by one.
O'Reilly, 2005, The Martyrs of the Coliseum, page 181

Even in the face of so much Biblical evidence and testimonies of early Church saints praying on their knees and hands uplifted, the enemies of the truth claim that these precepts are mere traditions, however, the apostle Paul responds with a THUS SAITH THE LORD, "We command you brethren, in the name of the Lord Jesus Christ, that you depart from every brother who walks disorderly and not according to the tradition you received from us; for you yourselves know how it is fitting for you to imitate us, since we never behave disorderly. among you" II Thess 3:6-7 .

Anyway, it is worth mentioning that when we are downcast, discouraged, we must bow our knees to the ground and raise our hands in search of divine help, begging the Lord with the essence of the Justice of Christ in our petitions. In order to do so, we must imitate and heed Paul's advice, "Therefore I restore the hands that hang down and the knees that are weak" Hebrews 12:12 . ). In the presence of the Most High. So are Sister White's teachings, on this very mat, Read the text:

Pray when you are down. In times of discouragement, say nothing to others; do not spread a shadow in the path of others; but tell Jesus everything. Raise your hands for help.
Ellen G. White, The Ministry of Healing, page 513

This is the attitude of the remnant church, we are proud and not ashamed to pray on our knees and hands up, so prayed the patriarchs, prophets, kings, Jesus Christ, the apostles and other members of the early church, the martyrs, reformers, the Adventist pioneers and the Remnant Church. The invitation remains: "O come, let us worship and bow down; let us kneel before the Lord our maker" Psalm 95:6 .

"My eyes fail because of affliction. Lord, I have cried out to you all day long, I have stretched out my hands to you" Psalm 88:9 .
