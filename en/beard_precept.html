WHO REMOVED THE CHURCH BEARD PRECEPT?

Among the nations of antiquity, Egypt stands out for the intolerance of wearing a beard, Egyptian laws forbade men to grow beards, forced their slaves to shave their beards, including Joseph. As we know Egypt, in a biblical sense, represents the world and its vanities, lusts and corruptions.

Later and for other reasons, Alexander the Great introduced the custom of shaving to Europe. It is reported that he compelled all the men in his army to shave their beards so that the enemies could not hold them down to slit their throats. This custom was introduced into the Roman Empire by the great general Scipio Africanus in the third century BC. It continued until the beginning of the second century AD, when Emperor Hadrian led the return to the use of beards.
