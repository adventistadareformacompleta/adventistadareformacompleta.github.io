In this vein, the aforementioned Manual of Doctrines of the Reformation has the power to contribute to souls thirsty for healthy knowledge, free from doctrinal and partial impurities, addressing difficult topics to be understood interpreted in their legitimate contexts, following the true teachings of the Holy Scriptures.

In this regard, it is urged to emphasize that the faith (doctrine) entrusted to the saints (Jude 3) in the period of the Ephesian church, in fact, will be found in the Remnant Church, although inserted in the context of the Laodicean period, the Message of the Third Angel, guides the Church of God to return to the doctrine of the Ephesian period, that is, the pure doctrine of the Early Church. In fact, doctrine was one of the pillars of the Apostolic Church, as seen in the Scriptures, "and they continued steadfastly in the apostles' teaching and in fellowship, in breaking of bread and in prayers." As the Scriptures prenote, the renowned apostle Paul, zealous for the faith, guided Christians to stay away from those who cause scandal against the doctrine, as can be seen: "I beseech you, brethren, take note of those who promote dissensions and scandals against the doctrine. which thou hast learned; turn away from them." Romans 16:17 . The removal of error prevents doctrinal contamination, the emergence of doubts and the decline of faith.
