THE HOLY LAW OF GOD


The Pact before Sinai

By way of introduction, it is urgent to point out Paul's position on the Law, in view of the evangelicals it is unacceptable to obey the Law of God after the death of Christ, however, in the line of collision with the aforementioned thesis, the celebrated apostle exhorts: "What shall we say? for? Is the law sin? Not at all. But I did not know sin except by the law; for I would not have known lust, if the law had not said, Thou shalt not covet." Romans 7:7 , from Paul's point of view, it is not a sin to obey the Law of God, meanwhile there is a growing hatred nurtured by religious denominations, fiercely opposing the validity of the Law of God.

In this bias, it is necessary to investigate the meaning of the word law. In the tenor of the Treatise on Adventist Theology, Law or Torah means order, divine instruction. Still according to the aforementioned work, Law is an expression of God's character and holiness, the law of God makes known the justice of the great Lawgiver, the Law is the revelation of God's justice and the basis of his heavenly government, founded on love: " God is love." I John 4:8 . As God is perfect, so is his law: "The law of the Lord is perfect, restoring the soul: the testimony of the Lord is faithful, and wisdom to the simple. The commandments of the Lord are upright, making the heart glad; the commandment of the The Lord is pure and enlightens the eyes." Psalms 19:7-8 . Just as God is just, so all his commandments are righteousness:"May my tongue celebrate your law, for all your commandments are righteousness." Psalms 119:172 . As God is holy his law is holy: "Therefore the law is holy, and the commandment holy, and just, and good." Romans 7:12 . As God is eternal, so are the principles of the law eternal and immutable: "I the Lord change not." Malachi 3:6 . "Jesus the Messiah is the same yesterday, today and forever." Hebrews 13:8 . The holy Law of God makes known to us the justice of its great lawgiver: "Hearken to me, ye that know righteousness, ye people in whose hearts my law is." Isaiah 51:7. Therefore, it is concluded, the sanctity of the Law of God is based on solid principles of love exposing fair rules available to every sinner, however, men lost divine justice, contaminated by original sin, they became lacking in spirituality, unable to to obey the Law of God, though it is the duty of every man: "The end of all that has been heard is, Fear God, and keep his commandments: for this is the whole duty of man." Ecclesiastes 12:13. As a matter of fact, the Holy Law of God was kept by Adam and Eve before sin, both were created clothed in the divine nature, compatible with the holiness required by the law, the Edenic couple was perfectly capable of obeying it. As seen, Adam was created endowed with the divine nature of God, or, image and likeness of the Creator: "Let us make man in our image, after our likeness; let them have dominion over the fish of the sea, over the birds of the sky, over livestock, over all the earth, and over every creeping thing that creeps on the earth. So God created man in his own image, in the image of God he created him; male and female he created them." Genesis 1:26-27. According to Irenaeus, the sinner kept the image of the Creator, that is, intelligence, reason, free will, meanwhile, the divine likeness mirroring the character, the divine nature of God covering Adam and Eve as a garment of light, was lost with the advent of evil sin.

Indeed, Adam and Eve were pure before sin, clothed in a divine nature:

The first Adam was created a pure, innocent being, without a stain of sin upon him, he was the image of God.
SDABC, Vol. 5, p. 1128 (Letter to Baker, 1895)

Therefore, they converse with God face to face in perfect harmony with the Law. In this lapse of time, Satan desired to be worshiped by resembling the Most High, magnifying himself. "Thou saidst in thine heart, I will ascend into heaven; above the stars of God I will exalt my throne, and on the mount of the congregation I will sit. I will ascend above the heights of the clouds, and I will be like the Most High." Isaiah 14:13-14 . The nefarious deceiver rebelled, opposing the government of God, conceived original sin, for this reason, there was a battle in heaven, defeated, Satan and his angels were expelled and precipitated to Earth: "There was war in heaven. and his angels fought against the dragon. The dragon and his angels also fought: yet they did not prevail: neither was their place found any longer in heaven." Revelation 12:7-8 . Satan sinned, defiling the heavenly Sanctuary, unexpectedly, seduced Adam and Eve to violate the forbidding command carved by the Lord in the book of Genesis: "Of the fruit of the tree that is in the midst of the garden, God said: You shall not eat of it, nor will I touch it. , lest you die." Genesis 3:3 . Inducing Adam and Eve to sin, Satan entertained the icy hope of receiving the bounties of divine mercy, if Adam and Eve were forgiven, so would he be. In another turn, if he were not benefited, he could exert his power on Earth, enslaving sinners, covering the august divine creation with shadows as he was precipitated here:"And the great dragon was cast out, that serpent of old, which is called the devil, and Satan, the deceiver of the whole world; yea, he was cast down to the earth, and his angels with him." Revelation 12:9 . Adam and Eve gave in to sin. Satan was expelled from heaven, cast down to Earth, on this inhospitable planet he will remain until the fulfillment of the Plan of Redemption, culminating in the elimination of sin, sinners and Satan, by the atoning sacrifice of Christ.

In this bias, after the fall of Adam, an abyss was created between God and man, motivated by the loss of the divine nature, he was no longer allowed to see the face of the Creator under pain of death: "When they heard the voice of the Lord God, walking in the garden at the turn of the day, the man and the woman hid themselves from the presence of the Lord God among the trees of the garden. The Lord God called to the man, “Where are you?” he said. the man; I was afraid because I am naked, and I hid." Genesis 3:8-10 . The imposing couple, Adam and Eve, lost the garment of light and glory, a reflection of the divine nature in which they were covered, undressed, forged to cover their nudity with fig leaves, according to the renowned author:

The robe of light that surrounded them was now gone, and under a sense of guilt and the loss of their divine covering a fear took possession of him, and they sought to cover their exposed forms.

On the other hand, humanity has degenerated, fallen into sin, becoming impotent, unable to love and obey a holy law: "For we know that the law is spiritual; but I am carnal, sold under sin... if I do what I don't want, I consent to the law, which is good." Romans 7:14;. Human degeneration longs for restoration, waiting for Christ to cover it with his robe of righteousness, restoring the status of Adam before sin, as the Testimonies foretell:

Adam and Eve, when they were created, had a knowledge of God's law; were familiar with its claims against you; his precepts were written in his heart. Genesis 26:5. Exodus 18:20 . The renowned patriarchs warmed in their hearts the care to fulfill the divine determination, teaching the commandments of the Lord to their children and grandchildren: "Teach them to your children, talking about them when you sit in your house, and when you walk by the way, and when you lie down, and rising up." The Ceremonial Law

By way of information, say, there were different types of laws in the Old Testament given to Israel, each law had a specific purpose, for example the Law of the Ten Commandments, it reflected the justice of the Creator pointing out sin, civil laws wielded the power of disciplining the coexistence among the servants of God, the dietary laws took care of health disciplining salutary rules of hygiene, the Ceremonial law, in turn, took care of the rites, ceremonies and forms of worship, with types and shadows pointing to the Messiah coming.

Having said that, it must be pointed out, the Ceremonial law was established after the fall of Adam, where our first parents brought offerings and presented them before the Lord. Abel expressed faith in the promised Savior by sacrificing an animal. He not only presented the shed blood of the sacrifice, but also the fat before the Lord, revealing a willingness to forsake sin, foreshadowed in the symbolic burning of the fat: "Abel, in turn, brought of the firstfruits of his flock, and of the fat thereof. the Lord of Abel and of his offering." Genesis 4:4 . In the sacrifices offered on the altar, the fat was burned, symbolizing the elimination of sin.

In the same way, we can see the behavior of the ancient Patriarchs erecting rustic altars in order to offer sacrifices. With remarkable propriety, the celebrated pioneer reports:

Before God's people went to Egypt, their worship was simple. The patriarchs lived in close communion with the Lord. He did not need many forms or ceremonies to teach them the great truth that sin could be expiated only by the death of Him who was without spot. They needed only a rustic altar and an innocent lamb to bind their faith in the spotless Lamb, the surety, who would bear the guilt of transgressions (1 Peter 2:24; 3:18). When the patriarchs traveled, they erected their altars, offered their sacrifices and God approached them, always demonstrating that he accepted their offerings by sending fire from heaven to consume the sacrifices.
HASKELL, Stephen N. The Cross and its Shadow. Ed. Full Life, 1997. p. 29

According to the teachings of the Scriptures, the entire ceremonial system (Ceremonial law) of the sanctuary was carried out alluding to the transgression of the moral law written by the finger of God. In fact, the transgression of the aforementioned law motivated the institution of sacrifices: "When a soul sins by mistake against some of the commandments of the Lord, concerning what must not be done, and acts against some of them; if the anointed priest sins to the offense of the people, shall offer a bull without blemish for their sin which they sinned to the Lord as a sin offering." Leviticus 4:2-3. In this particular, clarifying teachings of ANDREASEN emerge in his renowned work (The Ritual of the Sanctuary), in his view, the transgression of the Lord's commandments, made necessary the system of sacrifices similar to penumbra or shadow pointing to reality, Christ. Read, sin, violation of God's law made the temple ritual possible. Sin was the motive for morning and evening sacrifices, the Day of Atonement ceremonial, the offering of incense, and individual sacrifices for particular sins. The illustrious apostle John, in a vision concerning the heavenly sanctuary, saw the ark of the covenant in heaven: "Then the temple of God, which is in heaven, was opened, and the ark of the covenant was seen in his sanctuary.... ." Revelation 11:19. The law of God has a pre-eminent place, even in heaven, where it is called the temple of the tabernacle of the testimony, the temple of the Law of God:

The holiest city in the Old Testament was the city God had chosen as his abode. The holiest place in that city was the temple. The holiest place in the temple was the most holy. The most sacred object of the most holy was the ark in which were the tables of stone on which God had written with his own finger the ten commandments, the law of life, the oracles of God. This law was the center around which all ceremonial revolved; the basis and reason of the entire ritual. Without the law, the temple ritual would have no meaning.
ANDREASEN, ML The Ritual of the Sanctuary, 2nd ed. Ed. Brazilian Publishing House. page 166

Following Andreasen's enlightened clarification, the Law occupied the most sacred part of the Most Holy with all the symbols and offerings circulating around it, without the Law, the sanctuary service would lose its object.

The sacred Scriptures ratify these axiomatic truths alluded to by the servant of the Lord, regarding the existence of the two laws, moral and ceremonial, in the first moment the Scriptures approach the Moral Law: "And he gave to Moses... two tables of the Testimony, tables of stone , written with the finger of God." Colossians 2:14 . The Law Codified at Sinai

In fact, the Lord manifests his justice through the Law, the basis of his government, and therefore, he could not direct it only to the Hebrews.
