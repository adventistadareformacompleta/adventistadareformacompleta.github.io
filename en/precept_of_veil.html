THE PRECEPT OF THE VEIL

In apostolic times, Christian doctrine was in the making. Paul and the disciples transmitted to the believers the Christian traditions, full of worthy precepts to be followed, among them, was the precept of the veil, forcing the sisters to use this accessory in the services, thus, Paul, with praise, exhorts the church to keep the precepts taught by him: "Now I praise you, because ye remember me in all things, and keep the precepts as I delivered them to you" 1 Corinthians 11:2 . We need to ask, did these precepts have any importance for the spiritual life of the church? Or obey whomever he wanted? The apostle Paul emphatically responds, "By which also you are saved, if you hold fast, as I preached to you; if not that you believed in vain" I Corinthians 15:2 . It is seen that the promise of salvation is conditioned upon those who retain the teachings of apostolic doctrine: "And they continued steadfastly in the apostles' doctrine, and in fellowship, and in the breaking of bread, and in prayers" Acts 2:42 . Obeyed the doctrine as Paul and the disciples taught, a gospel with different teaching was considered anathema: "But even if we, or an angel from heaven, should preach any other gospel to you than what I have preached to you, let him be accursed" Galatians 1 :8 .

" Paul asks two questions (see this and the next verse) in order to confirm his argument. First he appeals to his readers' sense of propriety, when that sense is seriously and quietly investigated (see the thirteenth verse). then he appeals to the property shown by nature itself (see the fourteenth verse), which has covered the woman with a natural "veil", that is, with long hair, thus showing how decorous and proper is the use of the cloth veil. "
Champlin, The New Testament Interpreted Verse by Verse, Volume 4, Page 173

To consolidate this truth, it is worth praising the living testimony of the engravings carved in the catacombs of Rome, stage of meetings and cults of the ancient Christians, which are still preserved today to prove that Christian believers wore short hair and Christian women wore long hair and worshiped with a cloth veil completely covering the hair. This precept was universally obeyed by the early church, which under no circumstances thought to change this norm in the name of ease or social requirements, as John Wesley said, we cannot lower the norms, or you rise to them, or you will perish forever. In order to confirm this allegation, see the explanatory text of Champlin 's work :

" In the catacombs of Rome, the engravings there of the public services of Christians show men with short hair and women with tight veils around their heads, which completely hide their hair. This is the kind of scene one would see in Christian churches throughout the Greco-Roman world at that time, including Corinth. Paul therefore appealed to the appropriateness of this universal practice, not being inclined to accept challenges in order to dispute over modifications of this practice. "
Champlin, The New Testament interpreted verse by verse, volume 4, page 176

Finally, it is necessary to point out the situation of the Adventist pioneers, regarding the precept of the veil, of course, those who have an understanding contrary to the precept of the veil, will claim that Ellen White and the pioneers did not adopt the use of the veil in the church. As is known , the pioneers built the doctrine gradually, over time they were studying the early church in detail and assembling the Adventist doctrine, just look at the characteristic of the church at the beginning of its formation, which we will observe a great similarity with the apostolic doctrine. However, although the Latter Rain dripped in the days of the pioneers, it was taken up to heaven and with it the rest of the doctrine because they rejected justification by faith at the Minneapolis conference in 1888. Ellen White was a secluded church member until her death in St. Helena. California in 1915. She prophesied the downfall of the church and its apostasy, which culminated in the 1950s, when they allied with evangelicals to form part of America's ecumenical church society for profit and power.

In fact, in thoroughly investigating Adventist doctrine, we will find the pioneers facing a problem raised by Paul, when he said: "Let your women be silent in the churches; for it is not lawful for them to speak; but be in subjection, as the law. And if they want to learn anything, let them ask their own husbands at home: for it is disgraceful for women to speak in church" 1 Corinthians 14:34-35 . In the letter written to Timothy, the apostle Paul addresses the issue more forcefully: "Let the wife learn in silence, with all subjection. But I do not permit a wife to teach or to exercise authority over her husband, in silence" I Timothy 2:11-12 .

Adventist Pioneers took up the issue and argued that God gave women the ability to prophesy and pray in public and ground their thesis in another Pauline text, which says: "But every woman who prays or prophesies with her head uncovered dishonors her head, for it is the same thing as if it were shaved" I Corinthians 11:5 . Without a shadow of a doubt, the text is stating that a woman, indeed , can prophesy in the church, however, according to the text's requirement, can she speak in the church with or without a veil? Note, that the same text that authorizes women to speak in public, is demanding that women also wear the veil in church, so they were forced to comment on this verse to defend the right of women to speak in public. let's start, with a text by James White , quoting I Corinthians 11:5, admitting the possibility of women praying and prophesying in the church, let's go to the text:

In 1 Corinthians 11:5, &c. Here the apostle admits the prophecy of women in public assemblies and gives special directions respecting their behavior and appearance, while engaged in this sacred duty. "Every man who prays or prophesies with his head covered dishonors his head. But every woman who prays or prophesies with her head uncovered dishonors her head, &c." Here I think the apostle recognized the public prophesying of women.
Tiago White - Advent Review and Sabbath Herald, vol. 15, 2 February 1860, page 87

We cannot ignore that in the Old Testament, there were women who prophesied, prayed and spoke to the people about the way of the Lord like the prophetess Hulda, contemporary of the pious King Josiah who ascended the throne at only eight years old and did what was right. before the Lord.

In his reign the book of the Law was found by the High Priest: "Then Hilkiah the high priest said to Shaphan the scribe, I have found the Book of the Law in the house of the Lord. Hilkiah gave the book to Shaphan, and he read" II Kings 22: 8 . In possession of the book, the secretary brought the news to King Josiah, reading before the monarch the sentence of the Lord that was about to fall on Jerusalem, because of the apostasy of the people: "When the king heard the words of the Book of the Law, he tore the his garments" II Kings 22:11 . Desperate King Josiah ordered Hilkiah to gather a priestly commission and consult the prophetess Huldah about the Book of the Lord, he said:"Go, inquire of the LORD for me, for the people, and for all Judah, concerning the words of this book that has been found: for great is the wrath of the LORD, which is kindled against us; because our fathers have not heeded the words of this book, to do according to all that is written about us" 2 Kings 22:13 .

Biblical chronology shows us that at that time, the 18th year of the reign of Josiah 2Ki 22:3 , the ministry of Zephaniah Zep 1:1 , and of Jeremiah Jer 1:1-2 , were already functioning, however, the priestly commission was seek and consult the prophetess Huldah, 2Ki 22:14 ; 2Ch 34:22 and not the two renowned prophets, we can therefore infer, that even though women prophetesses were respected as much as male prophets. "Then Hilkiah the priest, and Ahikam, Achbor, Shaphan, and Asaiah, went to the prophetess Huldah, the wife of Shallum the son of Tikvah, the son of Harash, the keeper of the garments (and she dwelt in Jerusalem in the second part), and spoke" 2 Kings 22:14. Indeed, the Lord used the prophetess Huldah, who received the message directly from God, and immediately afterwards told them to the priestly commission, who carried the message to King Josiah 2Ki 22:20 ; 2Ch 34:28 , a message of God's fury against the people 2Ki 22:16-17 , but of mercy to the king 2Ki 22:19-20 for his fear and humiliation before the LORD, the Book of the Law found was the 28th chapter of Deuteronomy.

Another prophetess found in the sacred pages of the Old Testament is the sister of Moses and Aaron. This is Miriam, sent by the Lord before the people together with her two brothers: "For I brought you out of the land of Egypt and redeemed you from the house of bondage; and I sent before you Moses, Aaron and Miriam" Micah 6:4 . Moses' sister Miriam was also at the head of the people with her two brothers Moses and Aaron, by the command of the Lord, she had the gift of prophesying, singing and playing, as it can be seen : took a tambourine, and all the women went out after her with tambourines and with dances" Exodus 15:20 .

In the book of Judges we find Deborah, a prophetess who joined forces with Judge Barak, together they judged Israel in difficult times: "Deborah, a prophetess, wife of Lapidoth, judged Israel at that time. She sat under the palm trees of Deborah, between Ramah and Bethel, in the mountains of Ephraim; and the children of Israel went up to her for judgment." Judges 4:4-5 . At that time there was no king constituted, the people of Israel depended on the judgment of the Judges, the disputes about the law, social conflicts and the will of the Lord were taken before the prophetess and judge Deborah for consultation and conflict resolution. It was a position of leadership and great responsibility, the Spirit of the Lord rested on Deborah who prophesied and judged the people with justice.

So great was the trust placed in Deborah, that Judge Barak refused to go up to battle alone: ​​"Then Barak said to her, If you will go with me, I will go; but if you will not go with me, I will not go. And she said, I will certainly go with you, but The honor of your journey shall not be thine, for the Lord shall sell Sisera into the hand of a woman. Judges 4:8-9 . Deborah went up with the army of Israel and they won the war, fulfilling the word of the Lord, just as she had prophesied. We have yet another exuberant example of a woman used by the Spirit of the Lord to speak to the people on her behalf.
