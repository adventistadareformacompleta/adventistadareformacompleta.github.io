WHAT WAS ABOLISHED BY CHRIST

It is good to point out, man separated himself from God on account of original sin (sinful nature), the only way for the sinner to reconcile by acquiring life is by linking himself to the vine, Christ: "Abide in me, and I will remain in you As a branch cannot bear fruit of itself, unless it abides in the vine, so neither can you, unless you abide in me. I am the vine, you are the branches. in him he bears much fruit, for without me you can do nothing." John 15:4-5. This axiomatic truth is clearly labeled in ancient Levitical rituals and ceremonies, exposing respective types and shadows related to the Messiah. In effect, the ceremonial system pointed to Christ's sacrifice and its significance. When Christ died on the cross, reality replaced symbols, the types referring to the first advent were fulfilled losing their vigor, the services in the earthly sanctuary ceased, excluding the temple worship, or according to the Scriptures tearing the veil: "Behold, the The veil of the sanctuary was torn in two from top to bottom; the earth shook, and the rocks were split." Matthew 27:51. Christ surrendered the spirit on the cross at the exact moment of the evening sacrifice, when the priest sacrificed the lamb and presented the blood before the veil. It was strictly forbidden for the common priest to enter the Most Holy or even contemplate it, since only the high priest, once a year, on the Day of Atonement, entered the holy of holies, performing the symbolic cleansing of the earthly sanctuary. The breaking of the veil, allowing the priest to contemplate the interior of the most holy, indicates the fulfillment of the types of the first advent, that is, the types had met the antitypes, the ritual system had come to an end. This was to the crossed out ballot, the law consisting of ordinances:"Having blotted out the ballot paper that was against us in its ordinances, which in some way was contrary to us, and took it out from among us, nailing it to the cross." Colossians 2:14 . The ordinances referring to the types of the first advent of Christ were fulfilled, they were nailed to the cross, abolishing the ceremonial law, reconciling Jews and Gentiles : in himself, a new man, making peace." Ephesians 2:15. In the aforementioned text, Jesus is seen nailing to the cross the law of ordinances, of killing animals, ceremonial, a true wall, an insurmountable barrier of separation preventing the Gentiles from approaching the one God and not the moral law, if Jesus nailed the ten commandments on the cross, then, man is authorized to idolize, to manufacture idols, to call the name of God in vain, to transgress the Sabbath, to dishonor father and mother, to kill, to steal, commit adultery, bear false witness, and covet: "For where there is no law also there is no transgression." Romans 4:15 . If there was no prohibition in the Law, the practices listed would not be imputed sin. Because there is only sin, if the Law forbids the conduct, so if there is no Law, surely there will be no transgression. Read, sin is the transgression of the Law.

Still in the same context referring to sin, say, in the old covenant, when the people sinned through ignorance against the commandments of the Lord, an offering was sacrificed in its place, representative of the promised Messiah: "The Lord said to Moses, 'Speak to the children of Israel, saying, When anyone sins ignorantly against any of the commandments of the Lord, by doing against any of them what he ought not to do, if the anointed priest sins to offend the people, then for his sin he shall offer a bullock without blemish to the Lord. , as a sin offering." Leviticus 4:1-3 . It is noteworthy that the Lord forgives sins committed out of ignorance:"If the whole congregation of Israel sins through ignorance, and it is hidden from the eyes of the whole community, and they do what is not to be done, contrary to any of the commandments of the Lord, and they are guilty, and the sin they have committed is notorious, then the company will bring a bull for a sin offering and present it before the tent of meeting." Leviticus 4:13-14 . Unlike voluntary sins, these had neither forgiveness nor sacrificed offerings in atonement. In this beautiful example of a sacrifice for sin, when the priest, the whole congregation, the Prince, or any of the common people, sinned against the Lord's commandments through ignorance, offerings were offered and the blood brought into the tent of meeting:"The elders of the congregation shall lay their hands on the bull's head before the Lord; and the bull shall be slain before the Lord. Then the anointed priest shall bring some of the bull's blood into the tent of meeting; he shall dip his finger in the blood and sprinkle it seven times. before the Lord, before the veil." Leviticus 4:15-17 . Jesus nailed this ballot to the cross, the law of ordinances consisting in the killing of animals lost its value or its object, henceforth the sinner would be forgiven by a more perfect sacrifice and no longer with the blood of animals:"But when Christ came as a high priest of the good things already accomplished, through the greatest and most perfect tabernacle, not made with hands, that is, not of this creation, not through the blood of goats and calves, but through His own blood, entered the Holy of Holies once for all, having obtained eternal redemption." Hebrews 9:11-12 , Christ's sacrifice is perfect, does not need the blood of animals, He intercedes for the sinner offering His own blood once for all, "has no need, like the high priests, to offer sacrifices daily first for his own sins, then for the people's; for he did this once for all, when he offered himself." Hebrews 7:27. Therefore, the mediator offered his precious immaculate blood in place of the blood of animals.

In this vein, the renowned seer Daniel prophesied the death of the Messiah and the end of animal sacrifices, offerings and foods, without pointing to the end of the law of the ten commandments with the aforementioned action: "He will make a firm covenant with many for one week; in the middle of the week he will put an end to the sacrifice and the meal offering; on the wings of abominations the desolator will come, until the destruction that is determined is poured out on him." Daniel 9:27 . The aforementioned prophecy ties in with Paul's assertions, when the renowned disciple describes the ritual system, ceremonial law, or ballot as mere shadows of the good to come:"Now since the law has a shadow of good things to come, not the real image of things, it can never make the givers perfect with the same sacrifices which they offer year after year perpetually. For it is impossible that the blood of bulls and goats remove sins." Hebrews 10:1;4 . According to the teachings of the celebrated apostle, the ritual system was imperfect, based on food, drink and ceremonies, valid until the reformation or death of Christ, offering his blood in a perfect sacrifice, purifying the conscience of the justified by faith: "Which are but the ordinances of the meat, based only on food and drink, and divers ablutions, imposed until the opportune time of reformation." Hebrews 9:10. Christ's vicarious sacrifice is perfected with the help of the Spirit, imputing his righteousness to the penitent heart to effectively love and obey the law of the Lord: "Much more the blood of Christ, who through the eternal Spirit offered himself without blemish to God, will cleanse our conscience from dead works, that we may serve the living God." Hebrews 9:14 . Christ's sacrifice fostered hope, rejected formal ceremonialism, evolving into life-giving spiritual life through his righteousness renewing and perfecting the sinner.

In the present case, we see impudent theses, lacking foundations, committed to annulling Statutes, Precepts and Laws rooted in the atoning sacrifice of Christ, meanwhile, of all theological interpretations twisted or displaced from the legitimate contexts, defended by evangelical pastors and theologians, the most bizarre emerges from the work of nominal Adventists, as reported in the Treatise on Adventist Theology:

When Christ died, the ritual system with its complex sacrifices and ceremonies ceased. The real sacrifice for sin had been made. With forgiveness freely offered, the cross canceled the debt writing, which was against us and which was in an ordinance, which was harmful to us, removed it entirely, nailing it to the cross (Col. 2:14). Christians should no longer be concerned with ceremonial food or drink, or with feast ceremonies, new moons, or annual Sabbaths, because the whole ritual system was but a shadow of things to come. The reality of that shadow, the body, was Christ's sacrifice (v.16-17).
REID, George W. DEDEREN, Raquel; VYHMEISTER, Nancy J. Treatise on Adventist Theology V. 9. 1. ed. Ed. Brazilian Publishing House, 2011. pg. 539

In fact, the text of Colossians alluded to reads as follows: "Therefore, let no one judge you in regard to what you eat and drink, or in regard to a Jewish festival, the new moon or the Sabbath. These are shadows of what will come, but the body belongs to the Messiah." Colossians 2:16-17 . The text listed exposes serious concern, "do not allow anyone to judge you" , the context does not indicate a lack of concern with food, drink, parties, New Moon, Saturday or ceremonialism as the opponents defend, in the cited text Paulo exhorts in the sense of taking care of of the doctrine: "Take heed to yourself and to the doctrine; persevere in these things: for by doing this you will save both yourself and those who hear you." I Timothy 4:16. In the same vein Paul exhorted Christian servants to honor their masters with one purpose: "Let all servants who are under the yoke consider their masters worthy of all honor, lest the name of God and the doctrine be blasphemed." I Timothy 6:1 , the present context is in line with the expression in question "let no one judge you" , Paul's concern and exhortation is anchored in the sense of the brother obeying the doctrine not allowing blasphemies, judgment or criticism against God and its doctrine.

At this pace, it is appropriate to reveal the meaning of the fragment "who eat and drink" , in fact, it is not about ceremonial food and drinks, contrary to the narrative of the Adventist text, given that the only sacred food allowed to the common people were the sacrifices of the Peace offering and the Passover lamb, that is, except for the two sacrifices mentioned, the common people did not taste sacred foods.

It is urgent to highlight the icy arguments of the theologians of the nominal Adventist church, in their view, Fixed Feasts, New Moon, and Saturday were nailed to the cross, they claim: "the Saturday carved by Paul in Colossians refers to the ceremonial Saturdays" , meanwhile, it deals with It is a bizarre, unreasonable statement, coming from renowned theologians, in fact, when Paul addressed the feasts, certainly the ceremonial Saturdays are included, they are an inherent part of the feasts. The theological error persists for a long time, fostered by Guilherme Stein Filho, one of the first manifest Adventists in Brazil who defended the bizarre thesis, he said, if you have written Saturdays in the plural, it is ceremonial, if you have written Saturday in the singular, it is weekly. The alluded argument borders on the absurd, in fact it depends on the context, see this example:"I also gave them my sabbaths as a sign among us, that they might know that I, the Lord, made them a holy people." Ezekiel 20:12 . One can see, the prophet writing my Saturdays, in the plural, however, it is not about ceremonial Saturdays, but of the weekly Saturday, carved in the decalogue, only he is a sign between God and his people, so this fake theory does not deserve thrive. Furthermore, Paul, in the quoted text, exhorts the church to avoid transgressions of the weekly Sabbath, ceremonial, New Moon, in eating and drinking, in order to withhold judgment and criticism from the wicked and opposers.

Another gross error tainting the text listed in the treatise on theology is materialized in the expression "the body is Christ's" , for nominal Adventists, the indicated expression represents the sacrifice of Christ nailing everything to the cross, when in fact it is the church of Christ: "I now rejoice in what I suffer for you, and in my flesh I fulfill the rest of the afflictions of Christ, for the sake of his body, which is the church." Colossians 1:24 . In fact, the Scriptures uncovered yet another untruth raised by opposers. In their dark vision, they interpret the fragment "reality is Christ" nailing Festa, Lua Nova and Saturday to the cross. In fact the text of Colossians says: "they are shadows of what is to come", therefore, it is in the future, when Paul wrote the text, Jesus was past, he had died more than twenty years ago, if he wanted to point out the shadows and types of the first advent, surely he would have written: They were shadows of what came (Christ ) , given that the events belong to the past. However, he spoke of future shadows, that is, beyond their time, with a solid confirmation pointing out the types of the second advent, these must be fulfilled in their time, verbis: "do not allow anyone to judge you in relation to what you eat and drink, or in connection with a Jewish festival, the new moon or the Sabbath. These are shadows of what is to come." Colossians 2:16-17. The celebrated apostle takes as his prism the church's zeal for doctrine, exhorts not to cause scandals or transgressions, avoiding judgment of the wicked because of food, drink, Feast, New Moon and Saturday. He says "they are shadows of what is to come" , Christ had already died when Paul wrote the alluded text, therefore, it is extremely foolhardy to interpret Christ nailing Feast, New Moon and Saturday to the cross, Paul pointed to the future, types of the second advent. In order to definitively consolidate the alleged, it is necessary to study briefly the fulfillment of the types of the first and second advent, aiming to definitively clarify the text of Colossians 2:16-17 in the true context.

Within this context, we can see the Scriptures teaching in an exhaustive way, Christ nailing ordinances to the cross with the respective animal sacrifices. As a matter of fact, the Feasts are part of the Plan of Redemption, for this reason, Paul emphasized them in the text of Colossians, it is worth studying which of the three feasts remains shadow. The Feasts were promoted by God before Adam's sin, the first Feast is Passover, followed by Unleavened Bread, therefore, it is necessary to know its real meaning. When Moses brought the people of Israel out of Egypt, the Lord commanded:"This month shall be the chief of months to you; it shall be the first month of the year. This day shall be a memorial to you, and you shall celebrate it as a solemnity to the Lord; throughout your generations you shall celebrate it as a perpetual statute. And on the seventh day you shall have an holy assembly: no work shall be done therein, except that which pertains to eating, this only ye can do.” So Moses called for all the elders of Israel, and said to him, “Choose, and take lambs. according to your families, and sacrifice the Passover." Exodus 12:1;14;16;21. When the Hebrews left Egypt, the Lord instituted the Passover Feast. According to writings by Sister White, Passover and Unleavened were symbols of the work of Christ, types of the first advent, the two first ceremonial Saturdays (the first and seventh days) of the feast of Unleavened were fulfilled when the Messiah surrendered. the spirit on the cross of Calvary, this feast reached its object, the type found the antitypical in the symbols, namely the lamb, the unleavened bread and the waved sheaves. This assertion is consistent with Sister White's pity when she exhorts:

Passover was followed by the Feast of the Seven Days of Unleavened Bread. On the second day of the feast, the first fruits of the annual harvest, a sheaf of barley, were presented to the Lord. All the ceremonies of the feast were symbols of the work of Christ. Israel's deliverance from Egypt was an object lesson in redemption, which the Passover was intended to keep in memory. The slain lamb, the unleavened bread, the sauce of the first fruits, represented the savior.
WHITE, Ellen Golden The Desire of Ages 14. ed. Ed. Brazilian Publishing House, 1986. pg. 66

According to the distinguished author, when Christ died, he brought to completion the system of symbols and ceremonies referring to the aforementioned Feast, the type met the antitype, the paschal lamb symbolized Christ, just as the sheaves represented the resurrection. When the Feast of Easter was fulfilled, the Lord instituted the Holy Supper in his place, obliging his Church to celebrate until his second coming.

Indeed, Christ was the watershed, the Feast of Passover symbolized the death of the Lamb, putting an end to this Feast:

Christ was at the point of transition between two systems and their two great feasts. He, the spotless Lamb of God, was about to present Himself as a sin offering, and he wanted to bring to completion the system of symbols and ceremonies which for four thousand years had pointed to his death. In eating the Passover with his disciples, he instituted in its place the service which was to commemorate his great sacrifice. The national festival of the Jews would pass forever. The service which Christ established was to be observed by his followers in all lands and through all ages.
WHITE, Ellen Golden The Desire of Ages 14. ed. Ed. Brazilian Publishing House, 1986. pg. 629

The Feast of Unleavened Bread, in turn, was a symbol of Christ's resurrection, the sheaf waved by the priest represented the first saved, the "firstfruits of those who slept" , the first resurrected with Christ: "Speak to the children of Israel, and say unto them, When ye have come into the land which I am giving you, and have reaped its harvest, then shall ye bring to the priest a sheaf of the firstfruits of your harvest: and he shall wave the sheaf before the LORD , that ye may be accepted. the priest shall wave it. And in the day that you wave the sheaf, you shall offer a lamb without blemish, a year old as a burnt offering to the LORD.
And you shall not eat bread, nor roasted wheat, nor green ears, until that very day when bring the offering of your God; it isperpetual statute of your God; it is a statute for ever throughout your generations in all your dwellings." Leviticus 23:10-14 . As promoted elsewhere, the Passover was instituted at Israel's departure from Egypt, when Jesus died and rose again the day after the ceremonial Sabbath, Passover andwas effectually fulfilled, "the graves were opened, and many bodies of the saints which slept rose again." Matthew 27:52 . The type met the antitype. According to Haskell, the faithful servants of every century from Adam to Christ have risen. On this memorable day, the Plan of Redemption being partially fulfilled. In the alluded event, all sacrificial offerings ceased, in which sense Sister White reports:

The ceremonial consisted of symbols that pointed to Christ, to his sacrifice and priesthood. The ritual law, with its sacrifices and ordinances, was to be fulfilled by the Hebrews until the type met the antitype, in the death of Christ , the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world. Then all sacrificial offerings would cease
WHITE, Ellen Golden Patriarchs and Prophets 9th ed. Ed. Brazilian Publishing House, 1989. p. 379

The sheaves were symbol of Christ, he rose the same day, when the sheaves were waved before the Lord, the type met the antitype:

Christ rose from the dead as the firstfruits of those who slept. He was represented by the waved sheaf , and his Resurrection took place on the very day he was to be presented before the Lord. For over a thousand years this symbolic ceremony has taken place. The first ears of ripe grain were gathered from the harvest, and when the people went up to Jerusalem at the Passover, the sheaf of the firstfruits was waved as an offering of thanksgiving before the Lord. As long as this offering was not presented. The sickle could not be put into the cereal, nor could it be gathered into sauces. The sauce dedicated to Godrepresents the harvest. Thus Christ, the firstfruits represented the great spiritual harvest to be reaped for the kingdom of God. His Resurrection is the type and pledge of the resurrection of all the righteous dead . For if we believe that Jesus died and rose again, even so those who sleep in Jesus will God bring back with him.
When Christ rose, he brought a multitude of captives from the tomb.. The earthquake at his death had opened their sepulcher, and when he rose, they rose together. They were those who had collaborated with God, and who, at the cost of their lives, had borne witness to the truth. They ascended with him, as trophies of his victory over death and the grave. These, said Christ, are no longer the captives of Satan. I redeemed them. I brought them from the grave as the firstfruits of my power, to be with me wherever I am, never to see death or experience pain again.
WHITE, Ellen Golden The Desire of Ages 14. ed. Ed. Brazilian Publishing House, 1986. pg. 754

The alluded types of the first advent, with their respective Saturday ceremonies, were fulfilled, nailed to the cross by Jesus.

In this path the Lord commanded his people, fifty days after the Feast of Unleavened Unleavened , to offer a new offering before his glorious presence: whole weeks shall be. Until the day after the seventh sabbath, ye shall count fifty of them: then ye shall bring a new meal offering unto the Lord. On the same day it shall be proclaimed that ye shall have an holy convocation; ye shall do no servile work: it is an everlasting statute in all your dwellings. , through your generations." Leviticus 23:15-16;21. It is the Feast of Pentecost, the third ceremonial Saturday, a type of the first great harvest of souls. Pentecost was fulfilled after the resurrection of Jesus, it was not nailed to the cross of inopino, it was consummated in the outpouring of the Holy Spirit as designated in the Plan of Redemption. The aforementioned phase of the Plan of Redemption is wisely clarified by Sister White:

Jesus said to the disciples: I sent you to reap where you did not work; others worked, and you went into their work. The Savior here anticipated the great harvest of the day of Pentecost .
By the outpouring of the Holy Spirit at Pentecost, thousands were to be converted in one day. This was the result of the seed sown by Christ, the harvest of his labor
WHITE, Ellen Golden The Desire of Ages 14. ed. Ed. Brazilian Publishing House, 1986. pg. 171

The Feast of Pentecost was celebrated fifty days after Easter, on a single day, a ceremonial Saturday, leavened bread was presented symbolizing the first great harvest of souls spread in the outpouring of the Holy Spirit in the Temporã rain, in the days of the apostles: "When When the day of Pentecost was fulfilled, they were all together in one place: suddenly there came a sound from heaven like a rushing mighty wind, and it filled the whole house where they were sitting. one rested on each of them. And they were all filled with the Holy Spirit, and began to speak with other tongues, as the Spirit gave them utterance." Acts 2:1-4. Certainly, the outpouring of the Holy Spirit was an integral part of the new covenant, with Jesus as the mediator. In this event, as already explained, the first great harvest of souls was fulfilled, sown by Jesus in the early rain typified at Pentecost, in this sense reports the renowned Adventist pioneer:

There was a work for the disciples to do in order to be fitted for the great antitypical harvest feast . They needed to study the Scriptures, remove all disagreement, and be in harmony that they might receive the outpouring of the Holy Spirit, which would enable them to know how to care for the great harvest of three thousand souls , the fruit of Christ's ministry. They also needed that special outpouring of the Spirit to prepare them to carry forward the wonderful work begun on the day of Pentecost , until every creature under heaven heard the good news of salvation (Acts 1:14-26). In Palestine there was a first rain and a last rain, which occurred at the time of ripening of the harvest.. The prophet Joel, speaking of God's work in the last days, uses the terms "early rain" and "latter rain" to represent the outpouring of God's Spirit. And in the following words he gives assurance that in the final work of the Gospel on earth, God will again pour out his Spirit. "He will give you the rain in just measure; he will cause the former and the latter rain to come down, as of old, ... The threshing floors shall be filled with wheat"
HASKELL, Stephen N. The Cross and its Shadow, p. 113

In view of the above, it can be concluded that the third ceremonial Saturday also found its antitype in the great harvest of souls, represented by the feast of Pentecost.

Researching Sister White's Testimonies, the existence of types of the first and second advent of Christ can be glimpsed, the three ceremonial Saturdays, already studied, were part of the types of the first advent, in this sense the renowned author adds:

Arguments adduced from Old Testament symbols also pointed to autumn as the time when the event presented by the "cleansing of the sanctuary" should take place. This became very clear when attention was paid to the way in which the symbols relating to Christ's first advent had been fulfilled.
The death of the paschal lamb was a shadow of the death of Christ. Says St. Paul; "Christ our Passover was sacrificed for us" 1 Corinthians 5:7. The sheaf of firstfruits that was waved before the Lord at the Passover symbolized Christ's resurrection. Speaking of the resurrection of the Lord and of all his people, Saint Paul says: "Christ the firstfruits, afterward those who are Christ's at his coming." I Corinthians 15:23.
Like the sheaf that was stirred, constituted by the first ripened grains that were gathered before the harvest, Christ is the firstfruits of the immortal harvest of the redeemed who, at the time of the future resurrection, will be gathered into the storehouse of God.
Those symbols were fulfilled, not only in terms of the event but also in terms of time. On the fourteenth day of the first Jewish month, on the same day of the month in which, for fifteen long centuries, the paschal lamb had been slain, Christ having eaten the Passover with the disciples, instituted the solemnity which was to commemorate his own death as the Lamb of God. God who takes away the sin of the world. That very night He was taken by wicked hands, to be crucified and put to death. And, as the antitype of the sheaves which were shaken, our Lord rose again from the dead on the third day, as--the firstfruits of them that slept (1 Corinthians 15:20) an example of all the risen righteous, whose lowly bodies shall be changed, to be conformed to your glorious body. Philippians 3:21.
likewise,the types referring to the second advent are to be fulfilled at the time appointed in the symbolic service . In the Mosaic ceremonial, the cleansing of the Sanctuary, or the great day of atonement, took place on the tenth day of the seventh Jewish month (Leviticus 16:29-34), the day on which the high priest, having made atonement for all Israel, and so removed their sins from the sanctuary, went out and blessed the people."
WHITE, Ellen Golden The Great Controversy 30. ed. Ed. Casa Publicadora Brasileira, 1985. pp. 398-379

As a reference, there are 7 (seven) ceremonial Saturdays, three were nailed to the cross as a type of the first advent, leaving four ceremonial Saturdays, these were future for Paul, they are part of the types of the second advent of Christ and must be fulfilled in their time according to the symbolic cult. Paul was deeply knowledgeable about the Plan of Redemption, aware of the existence of the four remaining Sabbaths, types of the second advent, he wrote "they are shadows of what is to come", that is, the fourth Sabbath heralds the Trumpet of the Feast of Tabernacles, symbol of the silver Trumpet announcing the message of the first angel, the fifth Sabbath refers to the Atonement, symbol of the Judgment of Investigation, underway in the heavenly Sanctuary, the sixth and the ceremonial seventh Sabbaths relating to the Feast of Tabernacles symbolize the second and last great harvest of souls, or Latter Rain, subject discussed elsewhere, where they will be detailed in the study of the Plan of Redemption in the timeline. It must be said with certainty that these remaining Saturday ceremonies were not fulfilled with the work begun in 1844, with the message of the first angel or when Christ entered the Most Holy, fulfillment does not occur automatically, but in the end, when Jesus leaves of the sanctuary and say, It is finished.

Finally, the types of the first advent nailed to the cross were definitively detailed. As for the types referring to the second advent, they await consummation when Christ effectively concludes the work of the Sanctuary, modeled on the memorable Plan of Redemption. In the text in question from Colossians 2:16-17, Paul advised the church to watch over the doctrine, avoiding bad testimonies, blocking judgment from unbelievers to the brothers for eating, drinking, because of the Feasts, transgression of the day of the New Moon or Saturday, in effect, food and drinks are not shadows of anything, the New Moon is not a shadow either, it is not part of the atoning sacrifice of Christ, much less the weekly Sabbath is a type or shadow of anything, they are signs as reported in the Scriptures, the Sabbath was instituted before sin, therefore, is not type or shadow. It remains proven.
