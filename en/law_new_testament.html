THE LAW IN THE NEW TESTAMENT


Christ and the Law

It is necessary to unveil a bizarre thesis advocated by pastors and theologians, in their view, the Law was nailed to the cross by Christ, they defend the transgression of the Law, meanwhile, in their arguments, they contradict themselves when they forbid their sectarians (adepts), to manufacture and worship images of sculptures condemning idolatry, calling the name of God in vain, dishonoring father and mother, killing, stealing, adultery, bearing false witness, and coveting. Are not these teachings engraved in the holy law of God? In view of the above, the question is: how could Christ annul obedience to the law or nail it to the cross? In the collision line with the Scriptures, pastors and theologians assert: we only need faith, they do not perceive Satan's sophistry aimed at neutralizing the atoning sacrifice of Christ for the benefit of the sinner by proliferating sin.

The veil of unbelief covers the faces of theologians and pastors, they cannot see the validity of the Law and their obedience shined in the righteousness of Christ. According to Sister White's writings:

The cross of Calvary forever condemns the idea which Satan has set before the Christian world, namely, that the death of Christ has abolished not only the typical system of sacrifices and ceremonies, but also the immutable Law of God, the foundation of his throne, the transcription of his character.
By every possible device Satan has sought to invalidate the sacrifice of the Son of God, to render his atonement useless and his mission a failure. He has held that Christ's death made obedience to the law unnecessary and made it possible for the sinner to come into the good graces of a holy God without forsaking his sin. He has declared that the Old Testament standard has been lowered in the gospel, and that men can come to Christ, not to be saved from their sins, but in their sins.
WHITE, Ellen Golden. Faith and Works. Ed. Brazilian Publishing House, 2003. p. 79-80

Indeed, Satan recruits skillful disciples, inspiring unnerving testimonies in sumptuous pulpits condemning the Law, thereby seeking to invalidate Jesus' sacrifice by rendering useless his atonement on behalf of the sinner. Indeed, the Lord calls upon the sinner to come as he is, yet it is necessary to be born again through the righteousness of Christ, enabling him to obey the law out of love.

It must be repeated, from modern pulpits, hissing an enervating and ominous message inspired by Satan through his agents, extolling faith denying obedience to the Law on the collision line with the apostle James: "My brethren, what good is it if anyone says that has faith but not works? Can such faith save him? Do you find that a person is justified by works and not by faith alone. For as a body without a spirit is dead, so faith without works is also dead." James 2:1;24;26. Even without alluding to the doctrine of satisfaction, it is safe to say, faith without works is dead, faith, moved by love, becomes a guiding thread of obedience to the Law. Today's pulpits commit a serious mistake by hostile to the Moral Law, attending to the serpent's voice, thus, they feed their flock with straw and hay, strengthening the sinful nature, according to Sister White's reports:

From today's pulpits the words are uttered: Only believe! Have faith in Christ; you have nothing to do with the old law; only trust in Christ. How different is this from the words of the apostle, who declares that faith without works is dead! He says: Become therefore doers of the word, and not hearers only, deceiving yourselves. (James 1:22). We must have that faith that works by love and purifies the soul. Many seek to replace the righteousness of life with a superficial faith, thinking in this way to obtain salvation.
WHITE, Ellen Golden. Faith and Works. Ed. Brazilian Publishing House, 2003. p. 79

Pastors and leaders promise salvation, without forsaking sins, offer heaven of orgies and entertainment, yet such heaven does not exist. Salvation is achieved by Christians nourished by the righteousness of Christ, in their hearts there is no room for secularism and spurious pleasures.

In this vein, the unwary assert vehemently: Jesus came to abolish the Law. Therefore, it is necessary to ask Jesus himself, Lord, did you come to abolish the Law? Jesus replied, "Do not think that I came to abolish the Law or the Prophets; I did not come to abolish, I came to fulfill. For truly I say to you, until heaven and earth pass away, not one jot or one tittle will pass from the law, until all is accomplished." Matthew 5:17-18 . Christ did not come to revoke the Ten Commandments, in fact, he fulfilled the Plan of Redemption by enduring the curse of the Law, he died to put man in a position to obey the Law supported by the Redeemer's justice. Jesus did not revoke the law, however, pseudo scholars of the Scriptures, contradict the clear teachings of the Master citing the fragment "I came to fulfill", in your view, to fulfill means to put an end to the Law, in which case, is it necessary to let Jesus explain the true meaning of the text? "Then Jesus explained to them, "These are the words that I taught you while I was still among you: Everything that is written about me in the Law of Moses, the Prophets and the Psalms must be fulfilled!" Luke 24:44 . In his answer, Jesus brings to light the disciples' minds explaining the fulfillment of his Ministry written in the Law of Moses, prophets and Psalms. It remains clear, Jesus did not come to abolish the Law, the expression "I came to fulfill" means, "to fulfill all that was written in Him in the Law, the Prophets and the Psalms" , if the expression "I came to fulfill", meant to abolish the Law, Jesus would be abolishing the Prophets and the book of Psalms along with the Law.

After all, does it urge to ask Jesus about the writings pointed out in the Law, Prophets and Psalms about him? "And he said unto them. Thus it is written, that the Christ should suffer and rise from the dead on the third day, and that repentance and remission of sin should be preached in his name to all nations, beginning at Jerusalem." Luke 24:46-47 . Praise Jesus for explaining the Scriptures concerning His death, resurrection and forgiveness of sinners written in the Law, Prophets and Psalms, which He came to fulfill. "Then Jesus said to them, These are the words that I spoke to you while I was still with you: I must fulfill all that is written about me in the Law of Moses, the Prophets and the Psalms. Then He opened their minds to understand. the Scriptures." Luke 24:. It must be concluded that Jesus did not come to abolish the Law, but "to fulfill all that was written about him in the Law of Moses, Prophets and Psalms" , according to the Plan of Redemption. It is imperative to emphasize that the Prophet Isaiah, referring to the Law, points out the mission of Jesus: "It pleased the Lord for his own righteousness' sake, to magnify the Law and make it glorious." Isaiah 42:21 . It remains to ask, how did Jesus magnify the Law? Indeed, Christ has made the obedience of the Law spiritual, has imputed its righteousness to the human heart, shielding the sinner from insane formalism, making him as holy as the Law. In this way, it enables the sinner to obey the Law out of love, read, Jesus promoted spiritual improvement in obedience to the Law of God.

Indeed, after Christ's death, as a sin offering, the ceremonial law lost its force. The types and shadows represented a veiled view of mercy and grace pointing to the work of Christ, in this connection Sister White exhorts:

The types and shadows of the sacrificial service, with the prophecies, gave the Israelites a veiled and indistinct vision of the mercy and grace that would be brought to the world by the revelation of Christ.
WHITE, Ellen Golden. Selected Messages, V.1. 2nd ed. Ed. Brazilian Publishing House, 1985. p. 237

To Moses was opened the sense of the types and shadows pointing out Christ and his work. Only through Christ can man keep the Law, this axiomatic truth was revealed to the prestigious patriarch.

Indeed, the exuberant and glorious proclamation of the Law of the Ten Commandments on Mount Sinai struck fear into the elect people: "And all the people saw the thunder and lightning, and the sound of the horn, and the mountain smoking; and the people, seeing and it departed and stood afar off. And they said to Moses, Speak thou with us, and we will hear: and let not God speak with us, lest we die." Exodus 20:18-19 . Astounded at the manifest glory of the Lord, the Hebrews commissioned Moses as their mediator. Unknown to the appointed mediator, Christ, deprived of the Messiah, they would certainly be consumed by their self-righteousness and formalism, as indeed it happened. Suddenly, Moses tried to calm the people:"And Moses said unto the people, Fear not: God is come to prove you, and that his fear may be before you, that ye sin not. And the people stood afar off. But Moses came near to the darkness, where God was." Exodus 20:20-21 . The people largely lost the knowledge of the one God, the way of approaching, the sinfulness of sin and the need for Christ's righteousness, as well as the forgiveness of sin through the promised Messiah, a representative symbol of his sacrificial offerings, which they vaguely understood. . Moses came down from the mountain with a radiant face, illuminated by the divine glory in a transient way. Commenting on this, Paul exhorted:"Having therefore such a hope, we use much boldness in speaking. And we are not like Moses, who put a veil over his face, lest the children of Israel look firmly to the end of what was transitory. senses have been hardened; for to this day the same veil is yet to be lifted in the lesson of the old testament, which was abolished by Christ. And to this day, when Moses is read, the veil is over their hearts. Lord, then the veil will be taken away. Now the Lord is the Spirit; and where the Spirit of the Lord is, there is freedom. But we all, with uncovered faces, beholding as in a mirror the glory of the Lord, are changed from glory to glory in the same image, as by the Spirit of the Lord." II Corinthians 3:12-16. The Jews refused Christ as Messiah, blinded, not realizing their ceremonies, offerings and sacrifices lost their meaning. The veil remained covering their faces instilling stubborn disbelief. The aforementioned veil is currently covering the hearts, faces and feelings of the lovers of pleasure. Obstinate, they do not see the cancellation of the ballot, ceremonial law, in their comparison with sin, they relegate the Moral Law to the sewer of insignificance and repudiation, however, the true worshipers accept the gospel and doctrine contemplating Christ with his face uncovered, without veil, achieving visualize the truth. The glory of Christ is revealed in the Law, transcribing its character, feeling its transforming efficacy in the soul, until it becomes a perfect man in Christ, as Sister White reports:

They are made partakers of the divine nature, and become more and more like their Saviour, walking step by step in accordance with the will of God, until they reach perfection.
WHITE, Ellen Golden. Selected Messages, V.1. 2nd ed. Ed. Brazilian Publishing House, 1985. p. 240

The law and the gospel harmonize perfectly, one supports the other. The law points out sin, according to Paul: "I would not have known sin but through the law." Romans 7:7. Acknowledging sin through the Law, the sinner turns to Christ for justification. The Law of God, solemnly codified at Sinai, pronounces the doom of the sinner. It is the responsibility of the Law to condemn, stripped of any pardoning or saving power. Christ saves justified sinners by covering with his robe of righteousness, enables them to obey his law, yet the law brings damnation and death to the remaining sinners under its condemnation. In short, man needs Christ and the Law. The Law points out sin and Christ covers the sinner with his robe of righteousness enabling him to obey the Law, avoiding sinning after being justified by faith.

Still in the same context, it is emphasized, the law is the express manifestation of God's justice, it is holy, just and good. The sinner boasts a sinful nature and is unable to love it, understand it, much less obey it, for this reason Jesus said: "Seek first the kingdom of God and his righteousness, and all things will be added." Matthew 6:33 . Read, without justice it is impossible to obey the doctrine, Jesus and the Law. In this way, the pioneer Wagoner, draws attention to the divine nature of Christ, the great goal to be achieved in this life, according to his reports:

The righteousness of God, declares Jesus, is the goal to be pursued in this life. Food and clothing are minor issues compared to her. God will supply them in due time, so excessive care and concern need not be directed in this direction; but to ensure that the kingdom of God and his righteousness are the sole object of life.
WAGGONER, Ellet J. Christ and His Justice. 1. ed. Ed. Historical Adventists, 2011. p. 33-34

The righteousness of Christ is the goal to be reached, clothed in the divine nature, every physical and spiritual need will be supplied. The putting on of the divine nature results in new birth based on love, zeal, obedience and other gifts of the cross, supplying the needs in due time, Christ justifies the sinner by faith by removing the dirt of original sin, He dwells in the heart lending His righteousness: "But of him are ye in Christ Jesus, who became to us wisdom of God, and righteousness, and sanctification, and redemption." I Corinthians 1:30 . Thus, in consequence of grace, God offers the Redeemer's justice, improving obedience to the law, according to Wagoner's accounts:

Christ was made unto us righteousness as well as wisdom, and since Christ is the wisdom of God, and in him dwells all the fullness of the Godhead bodily, it is evident that the righteousness that is available is that of God.
WAGGONER, Ellet J. Christ and His Justice. 1. ed. Ed. Historical Adventists, 2011. p. 34

According to Jeremiah, Christ is our righteousness, the sinner clothed in the divine nature equals the holiness of the Law, able to obey the righteous commandments of God, according to the psalmist. "May my tongue celebrate your law, for all your commandments are righteousness." Psalms 119:172 . Jesus said: I and the Father are one. The two have the same nature and substance. In the same way the Law is holy, the commandment is holy, just and good, therefore, the problem lies in the man, inclined to evil, he needs the righteousness of Christ to obey the law.

Again, the fragment "seek first the kingdom of God and his righteousness" , means to seek divine nature resembling Jesus in character, absorbing his mind, the true disciple has the mind of Christ through the covering of his righteousness: "Now the natural man does not accept the things of the Spirit of God, for they are foolishness to him, and he cannot understand them, because they are spiritually discerned. But the spiritual man judges all things, but he himself is not judged. for anyone. For who knows the mind of the Lord, that he can instruct him? But we have the mind of Christ." I Corinthians 2:14 . Wagoner's writings exhort:

What do we learn from this? That those who know the righteousness of God are the people whose heart is his law, and therefore that the law of God is the righteousness of God. This can be proved again, as follows: All unrighteousness is sin. I John 5:17. Everyone who practices sin also transgresses the law: for sin is the transgression of the law. I John 3:4. Sin is transgression of the law, and it is also unrighteousness; therefore sin and unrighteousness are identical. But if injustice is transgression of the law, justice must be obedience to the law.
WAGGONER, Ellet J. Christ and His Justice. 1. ed. Ed. Historical Adventists, 2011. p. 34

Wise words from the celebrated pioneer. In fact, God created man in his image and likeness, the image portrays intelligence and free will, gifts preserved after the fall, as for the likeness, the divine nature Adam lost, replaced by original sin (sinful nature or self-satisfaction), whose consequence separated the man of God, bearing fruit in transgression of the law and rebellion. Furthermore, it is necessary to know which law the Scriptures are talking about? "And when he had finished speaking with him on Mount Sinai, he gave to Moses the two tablets of the Testimony, tablets of stone, written by the finger of God." Exodus 31:18 . Certainly from the Decalogue. This is the understanding of pioneer Wagoner, referring to the moral law of the Ten Commandments:

Now, what law has its obedience representing righteousness, with disobedience to it signifying sin? It is that law which declares: Thou shalt not covet, for the apostle Paul tells us that this law convicts him of sin. Rom 7:7. The law of the ten commandments, then, is the measure of God's righteousness. Being the law of God and constituting justice, rebellion against God's law is rebellion against God's justice.
WAGGONER, Ellet J. Christ and His Justice. 1. ed. Ed. Historical Adventists, 2011. p. 35

The breaking of the law gave rise to sin, forcing the action of the Mediator symbolized in the ceremonial law. By way of information, say, the Moral Law points out sin, the Ceremonial Law was instituted because of sin, it pointed out the mediator, Christ, paying the price on the cross in the sinner's place.

The importance of seeking the righteousness of Christ first, translates into man's ability to obey and understand the spirituality of the law, transcending the literality of the law written on tables of stone. Killing does not only mean committing murder, much less adulterating, it only means literal illicit sexual relations. In the Sermon on the Mount, Christ declared, "You have heard that it was said to them of old, Thou shalt not kill; and, Whoever kills will be liable to judgment. , and whoever utters an insult to his brother will be liable to judgment of the court; and whoever calls him a fool, will be liable to hellfire." Matthew 5:21-22 . And again added:"You have heard that it was said, 'You shall not commit adultery. But I say to you, whoever looks at a woman lustfully has already committed adultery with her in his heart." Matthew 5:27-28 . The law forbids killing, however, it is possible to pay the price of this crime by hating one's brother, along the lines of the spirituality enshrined in the moral Law of the Ten Commandments: "Whoever hates his brother is a murderer; now you know that every murderer has eternal life abiding in it." I John 3:15 . As seen, the spirituality of the Law transcends the literality of the norm carved on boards.

The renowned pioneer Wagoner, in this regard, clarifies:

In this Christ did not reveal a new truth, but only brought to light and unfolded an old one. The law had exactly the same character when he proclaimed it at Sinai and when he expounded it on the mountain of Judea. When, in earth-shattering tones, he declared, Thou shalt not kill, he meant, harbor no rancor in your heart; allow not envy, nor strife, nor anything remotely connected with murder. All this and much more is contained in the words: Thou shalt not kill. And this was taught in the inspired words of the Old Testament, for Solomon showed that the law deals with things invisible as well as with things visible, when he wrote, Of all that he hath heard, the sum is, Fear God, and keep his commandments; for this is the duty of every man. For God will bring every deed into judgment, even those that are hidden,
The argument is this: The judgment passes over every secret thing; the law of God is the standard in the judgment it determines the quality of every act, whether good or bad; therefore, the law of God forbids evil in thought as well as in deed. So the conclusion of the whole question is, that the commandments of God contain the duty of every man. (Ecclesiastes 12:13-14).
WAGGONER, Ellet J. Christ and His Justice. 1. ed. Ed. Historical Adventists, 2011. p. 36

If it is necessary to analyze the spirituality of the law, according to the prophet Isaiah, Jesus perfected and enhanced the Law, read, sin does not sleep only in actions, it is also allocated in thoughts. When the law forbids killing it includes thoughts, cogitation, actions and execution.

Likewise, when the first commandment forbids worshiping other gods before the Lord: "Thou shalt have no other gods before me." Exodus 20:3 . Are you referring to the graven image only? Certainly not. In conclusion, Christ magnified the law by revealing the spirituality of the law through its justice. In this sense, Wagoner explains the aforementioned commandment of the Decalogue:

Let us take the first commandment: Thou shalt have no other gods before me. The apostle tells us about some whose god.... is the womb. Philippians 3:19. But gluttony and intemperance are suicide, and so we find that the first commandment runs through the sixth. This, however, is not all, for he also tells us that covetousness is idolatry. Col. 3:5. The tenth commandment cannot be violated without violating the first and second. In other words, the ten commandments coincide with the first, and we find that the Decalogue is a circle having a circumference as large as the universe, and containing within it the moral duty of every creature. In short, it is the measure of the righteousness of God, who dwells in eternity.
WAGGONER, Ellet J. Christ and His Justice. 1. ed. Ed. Historical Adventists, 2011. p. 37

They noticed the wonderful clarification, it is possible to perceive the spirituality of the Law, as Jesus magnified it. The law is a circle, one commandment is linked to the other, the first commandment says: "you shall have no other god before me" , however, when some make their womb god, rejecting the healthy diet offered by God, feeds idolatry with the idol of gluttony and intemperance leading to suicide, with clear transgression of the commandments, do not set another god before the Lord, do not covet and do not kill. Finally, the text clarified, when you cherish the god belly, you will surely covet the deleterious delicacies, feeding the idol of gluttony and intemperance culminating in suicide (death).

In addition, as explained elsewhere, Jesus cites the commandments of the Law, teaching to obey them, in this way, he praises the spirituality of the Law: "You have heard that it was said to the ancients: Thou shalt not kill; and, Whoever kills will be subject to judgment. But I say to you that whoever is angry with his brother without cause will be liable to judgment; and whoever insults his brother, will be liable to judgment of the court; and whoever calls him, Fool, will be liable to the hell of fire." Matthew 5:21-22 .

Here is another example of the aggrandizement of the law, the commandment to kill someone, transcends literality, according to Jesus, one does not kill with weapons alone, it is possible to kill with food, or tongue, uttering insults or slander, false witness and hatred, "everyone who hates his brother is a murderer; now you know that every murderer does not have eternal life abiding in him." I John 3:15 . Having said that, it can be concluded that Jesus did not come to abolish the law, on the contrary, he magnified the Law by spiritually citing another commandment: "You have heard that it was said: You shall not commit adultery." Matthew 5:27 . Before Christ magnified the Law, sins, such as adultery in its literality, would only be conceived through the presentation of two or three witnesses:"One witness shall not rise up against any man for any iniquity or for any sin whatsoever he commits; by the testimony of two or three witnesses the fact shall be established." Deuteronomy 19:15 . With the advent of the Messiah, the Law was refined, it became spiritual, as Jesus taught in the Sermon on the Mount: "You have heard that it was said, 'You shall not commit adultery. in his heart he has already committed adultery with her." Matthew 5:27-28. In the literalness of the Law, the sin of adultery was conceived by presenting two witnesses. After the aggrandizement, the law became spiritual, just look at a woman desiring, harboring impure thoughts to consummate adultery. This wonderful example of the spirituality of the law makes clear the need for the covering of Christ's righteousness overcoming the weaknesses of the flesh. The carnal man, fed by the sinful nature, cannot free himself from sensual temptations, however, the spiritual man, clothed in the divine nature of Christ, follows the example of Joseph, I will never sin against my God.

It is also important to consider the case of the sinful woman, in the aforementioned event, Jesus enjoyed an excellent opportunity to teach the extinction of the law and its fulfillment, encouraging free transgression, meanwhile, when the adulterous woman was presented: "The scribes and Pharisees brought to their presence of a woman caught in adultery, and making her stand in the midst of them all, they said to Jesus, "Teacher, this woman has been caught in the act of adultery. And in the law Moses commanded us that such women should be stoned, you therefore who you say?" John 8:3-5 . Jesus could simply say: she did nothing wrong, there being no law (it was abolished) there is no transgression, conduct is sinful when there is a law imputing the wrongful or sinful act, yet He replied:"He that is without sin among you, let him be the first to throw a stone at her. And bending down again, he continued to write on the ground." John 8:7-8 . Christ wrote in the sand the sins (Transgression of the Law) of each accuser of that woman, when they saw in the sand their own transgressions of the Law, they came out one by one, "Then when Jesus arose and saw no one else but the woman, he asked "Woman, where are those your accusers?" John 8:10-11 . When Jesus said "go and sin no more", He publicly declares, go and transgress the Law no more, therefore, how can they try to deceive souls by inducing Christ to abolish the Law? When Jesus taught just the opposite, go and obey the Law.

The Law in the Early Church

As the best doctrine of the apostle Paul states: "Without faith it is impossible to please God, because anyone who comes to God must believe that he exists and that he rewards those who earnestly seek him." Hebrews 11:6 . Anchored in the aforementioned text, evangelical pastors and theologians base their theses highlighting faith as the only requirement required to achieve salvation by discarding works; in his view, the only essential foundation is faith. However, the early church, rooted in solid biblical foundations, teaches the opposite, in its view, faith is not enough to save, it needs to be confirmed by works:"My brethren, what good is it if anyone says he has faith but does not have works? Can such faith save him? So also faith, if it does not have works, is dead by itself." James 2:14;17 . Skeptical of the teachings of James, many sincere Christians, contaminated by the false teachings of pastors, refuse to obey the Commandments of God; prioritizing faith alone. Stripped of a solid foundation, they commit a grave error, resembling demons: “Do you believe that God is one? without works is dead?" James 2:19-20. According to the exhortation of the apostle James, the follower of the above thesis (salvation by faith alone) is labeled vain men. Demons have faith, however, they do not have the right to salvation, they do not obey the Law of God. Anchoring salvation in faith alone is like a boat adrift on the ocean, aimlessly, it is a fictitious faith lacking in support, only of lips, condemned never to dock at the port of salvation. Powerful teachings from the early church confirm that man is not justified by faith alone: ​​"You see then that a man is righteous by works, and not by faith alone." James 2:24 . According to the vigorous apostle, the penitent sinner proves the size of his faith by works:"But someone will say, You have faith, and I have works; show me your faith without your works, and I will show you my faith by my works." James 2:18 . Indeed, Scripture excludes faith and formalist works, genuine faith exposes trust, springs from a heart watered by the righteousness of Christ, whose fruit results in good works, obedience to the Sound Doctrine and the law of God out of love. In this way, both faith and works, go together hand in hand, inseparable, if there is separation they die, a belief based on faith alone or on works alone, both wither and die of spiritual starvation: "For as the body without spirit is dead, so also faith without works is dead." James 2:26. At the other extreme, there is the famous doctrine of Satisfaction, spread in the first centuries of the Christian era, preaching and demanding from the Christian works of charity, benevolence and an appearance of piety, as the primary and only requirement to achieve salvation. As long as the remiss, lawbreakers, opposers of sound doctrine, lying preachers, turning unwary from the truth to die in their transgressions, will remain outside the city, they will have no right to partake of the fruits of the tree of life."Blessed are those who keep his commandments, that they have the right to the tree of life, and can enter the city through the gates. Outside are the homosexuals, those who use hallucinogenic drugs to tune in to the occult, the sexually immoral people, murderers, idol worshipers, and everyone who loves and practices falsehood." Revelation 22:14-15. With grief and great disappointment the liars, enemies of God's law and adherents, will face the divine court, will participate in the second resurrection, of the wicked. The deceived, angry members turn against their former demigods, idol pastors dumping the blame of their condemnation on their shoulders, yet it is your fault, for rejecting the warnings against popular sins and secularism fueled by popular religious institutions. In this way, they are worthy of perdition, without the right to enter the holy city with the saved, keepers of God's law, defenders of the faith (doctrine) of Jesus.
