THE SABBATH, FROM EDEN TO EDEN

The strength of his words the Lord formed the heavens the earth the sea and everything in them, when the earth was formed it was of extraordinary beauty, yet it was on the sixth day of creation that the Lord said: "Let us make man in our image, after our likeness: let them have dominion over the fish of the sea, over the birds of the air, over the livestock, over all the earth, and over all the creatures that move along the ground.” So God created man in in his own image, in the image of God he created him; male and female he created them" Genesis 1:26-27 .

Man was formed in the image of God, both in outward appearance and in character. Christ alone is the express image of the Father. "The Son is the radiance of God's glory and the exact expression of his being, upholding all things by his powerful word. After he had performed cleansing from sins, he sat down at the right hand of the Majesty on high, becoming so superior to angels as the name he has inherited is superior to theirs" (Hebrews 1:3-4), but man was formed in the likeness of God. His nature was in harmony with the will of God. The mind was capable of understanding divine things. The affections were pure; the appetites and passions were under the control of reason. He was holy and happy, having the image of God, and being in perfect obedience to his will.
Ellen G. White, Patriarchs and Prophets, page 28

He did not know sin, he was dressed in the garments of God's justice, which provided him with perfection, that is, he was endowed with the divine nature of the Creator "Bless the Lord, O my soul! of glory and majesty. He covers himself with light like a garment, he spreads out the heavens like a curtain" Psalm 104:1-2 . Writings of Sister White's writing, assert:

This couple, who were without sin, did not wear artificial garments; they were clothed with a covering of light and glory, such as angels wear. As they lived in obedience to God, this garment of light continued to envelop them.
Ellen G. White, Patriarchs and Prophets, page 29 / History of Redemption, page 21 / Final Events, page 292

So the Lord God planted a garden in Eden and placed the novice couple there to dwell and rule over all the animals he had created. "And the Lord God planted a garden in Eden, towards the east, and put in it the man whom he had formed" Genesis 2:8 . "And God said, 'And the Lord God took the man and put him in the garden of Eden to till it and keep it. And the Lord God commanded him: Of every tree of the garden thou shalt freely eat, but of the tree of knowledge of good and evil, thou shalt not eat of it: for in the day that thou eatest thereof thou shalt surely die" Genesis 2:15-17. And when the work of creation was complete the Lord established the memorial of his creation. According to the writings of Uriah Smith, the Sabbath was instituted at the beginning, at the close of the first week of creation, "Thus were the heavens and the earth completed, and all that is in them. On the seventh day God had already finished the work which accomplished, and on that day he rested. God blessed the seventh day, and sanctified it, because on it he rested from all the work that he had done in creation" Genesis 2:1-3 .

It is noteworthy that God's rest on the seventh day made it the Sabbath day, or the Sabbath (rest) of the Lord. It can never fail to be his day of rest, as it can never be changed. He then sanctified, or set aside, that day, as the account tells us, and that sanctification can never cease, unless it be taken away by an act on the part of Jehovah as direct and explicit as that by which he set it on the day. at the beginning.
SMITH, Uriah, The Prophecies of Revelation, page 290

According to just inspired:

The Sabbath was entrusted to Adam, father and representative of the entire human family. Observance of it should be an act of grateful acknowledgment, on the part of all who dwell on earth, that God was their Creator and rightful Sovereign; that they were the work of his hands, and subjects of his authority. Thus, the institution was entirely commemorative, and was given to all mankind. There was nothing in it prefigurative, or of application restricted to any people.
Ellen G. White, Patriarchs and Prophets, page 30

The Almighty, in his infinite wisdom, saw that the Sabbath rest was essential for man, even in Eden. He needed to set aside his own interests for one day out of seven and dedicate it to total worship of the Creator. This is the real reason why Christ said, "The Sabbath was made for man, and not man for the Sabbath" Mark 2:27 . It is seen that the Sabbath was instituted before sin, and would be observed forever by Adam and Eve, so it cannot be prefigurative, type, shadow or symbol, as some uncaused want to attribute with interpretations out of context, see what Uriah says Smith:

The Sabbath contains nothing of a typical or ceremonial nature, because it was instituted before man sinned, and therefore belongs to a time when, in the very nature of things, there could be no type or shadow.
The laws and institutions that existed before the fall of man were primary in their nature, they came out of the relationship between God and man and men to each other, and would always remain so if man had never sinned and were unaffected by his sin. In other words, they were, in their very nature of things, immutable and eternal. Ceremonial and typical laws owed their origin to the fact that man had sinned, and would never have existed if sin had not been a fact. They were subject to change from dispensation to dispensation. And these, and these alone, were abolished at the cross. The Sabbath law was primary and therefore immutable and eternal.
SMITH, Uriah, The Prophecies of Revelation, page 290

Before sin man covered himself with a garment of light and glory, with the fall of Adam, this garment was lost, the plan of redemption was established to rescue penitent sinners to the fold of the sheep of Christ. God designed our garments to be a remembrance of the heavenly garment, divine nature, with which he will finally cover the redeemed. "He that overcometh shall be arrayed in white robes, and I will in no wise blot out his name from the book of life; and I will confess his name before my father and before his angels" Revelation 3:5. With the fall of Adam, the Plan of redemption was presented, with symbols, ceremonies, types and shadows, see an example:

Those Israelites who would conscientiously refuse to mix wool and linen in their daily garments, and aim in this the lesson which God designed to teach them, would also turn from sin. All their apparel, made of only one quality of cloth, continually reminded them of the perfect garment of Christ's righteousness bestowed on the faithful.
Haskell, The Cross and Its Shadow, page 282

Fallen man, had lost his divine nature and was separated from God by original sin (Isaiah 59:2), the sinful nature contracted by Adam with sin, now needed the substitute, only Christ, being equal with God, could atone for sin. of man and pay the price demanded for the transgression of God's holy Law, then the Plan of redemption was instituted with types and shadows pointing to the Lamb of God who takes away the sins of the world in the so-called ceremonial Law. The ceremonial Laws were regulations used by God to teach the Plan of Redemption through symbol and worship practices. According to WHITE,

Sacrificial offerings were ordained of God in order to be to man a perpetual remembrance of his sin, and an acknowledgment of it, as well as being a confession of his faith in the promised redeemer. It was designed to impress the fallen race with the solemn truth that it was sin that caused death. For Adam, the offering of the first sacrifice was a most painful ceremony. His hand should go up to take a life, which only God could give.
Ellen G. White, Patriarchs and Prophets, page 64

According to Old Testament texts these laws came into force soon after the sin; with Cain and Abel offering sacrifices. "It came to pass after a while, that Cain brought an offering to the LORD of the fruit of the ground. Abel, in turn, brought of the firstfruits of his flock, and of the fat thereof. The LORD was pleased with Abel and with his offering" Genesis 4:3 -4 . This text proves that the ceremonial law and the burnt offerings had their origin at the gate of Eden and extended to the cross. Throughout the patriarchal age, the Lord's faithful servants erected rough stone altars, upon which sacrifices were offered pointing to the Savior. "The LORD appeared to Abram, and said to him, I will give this land to your descendants. There Abram built an altar to the LORD, who had appeared to him" Genesis 12:. However, after the period of servitude in Egypt, God's people were so prone to idolatry that the Lord commanded the construction of a bronze altar in the court of the tabernacle. The offering of the sacrifice of holocaust by the father of the family (patriarch) was altered, now he had to bring it to the sanctuary and the officiating priest was in charge of offering the sacrifice. "But ye shall seek the place which the LORD your God shall choose out of all your tribes, to call it his name and his dwelling place; and thither ye shall go. To that place ye shall bring your burnt offerings, and your sacrifices, and your your tithes, and the offerings of your hands, and the vow offerings, and the freewill offerings, and the firstborn of your herds and of your flocks" Deuteronomy 2:5-6. Note that the tithe was included in the ceremonial law, however, it was not nailed to the cross.

Even before Sinai, God's Law, Commandments, and Statutes were known, loved, and obeyed by the people of the Most High throughout the patriarchal age, from Eden to Sinai, before codification. "For Abraham obeyed me, and kept my precepts, my commandments, my decrees, and my laws" Genesis 26:5 . The same law obeyed by Abraham, taught by him and followed by his descendants, see the example of Tamar, was accused of having broken the seventh commandment of the Law of Jehovah, adultery. "After about three months it was said to Judah, Tamar your daughter-in-law has committed adultery, for she is with child..." Genesis 38:24 . Genesis 39:9 . In this text, it has been proved in a forceful way that the violation of God's commandments is a sin, that is. "Everyone who practices sin transgresses the law; for sin is the transgression of the law. You know that he was manifested to take away our sins, and in him there is no sin" 1 John 3:4-5. Joseph, avoided adultery, because he knew it was a transgression of the Law of the Lord, so the law was obeyed before being codified at Sinai. Jesus appeared to take away our sins, that is, to stop breaking the Law, the same that Joseph and Abram kept, Christ never sinned, because he never broke the Law. We still have the example of Jacob, in compliance with the observance of the first two commandments, also contained in the law codified at Sinai."So Jacob said to his household and to all who were with him, Get rid of the foreign gods that are with you, cleanse yourselves and change your clothes. Come, let us go up to Bethel, where I will make an altar to the God, who heard me in the day of my trouble, and who has been with me wherever I have gone.” Then they gave to Jacob all their foreign gods that they had, and the earrings that they wore in their ears, and Jacob shut them up at the foot of the great tree, near Shechem" Genesis 35:2-3 . This text clearly proves that the Law was taught from father to son, the teaching of the Law was verbal, there was no writing."In that same day thou shalt tell thy son, saying, This is what the LORD did for me, when I came out of Egypt. "Teach them to your children, talking of them when you sit in your house, and when you walk by the way, and when you lie down, and when you rise up" Deuteronomy 11:19 .

Just as the Law, the Sabbath, the fourth commandment of the aforementioned Law, was certainly taught and observed since Eden by the servants of the Creator, let's see examples of texts that prove the observance of the Sabbath before Sinai. "On the sixth day they gathered twice as much bread, two omors for each; and the leaders of the congregation came and told Moses. "And Moses said, Eat it today, for the Sabbath is the Lord's; today you will not find it in the field" Exodus 16:25. Before arriving at Sinai, the Lord provided manna to his people, but on the sixth day he gave them double to rest on the holy day of the Lord, seeing that on the Sabbath manna was not found in the fields. THE SABBATH, CODED IN SINAI

Soon after they camped at Sinai, Moses was called into the presence of the Lord and received the two tablets of stone written by the finger of God, the Law of the Ten Commandments was codified. "And when he had finished speaking with him on Mount Sinai, he gave to Moses the two tables of the Testimony, tables of stone, written by the finger of God" Exodus 31:18 . "The tables were the work of God, also the writing was the same writing of God, carved on tables" Exodus 32:16 . This was the only part of the Bible written by God. Moses wrote the Pentateuch by divine inspiration. The law was not given exclusively to the Hebrews, but to all men, "The conclusion of all that you have heard is, Fear God, and keep his commandments, Ecclesiastes 12:13 .

The moral precepts of the Decalogue were carved out for all mankind to love and obey, in view that the ten precepts encompass man's duties to God in the first four commandments, and the last six portray our duties to our fellow men. . All ten commandments are based on the fundamental principle of love. "And behold, a certain man, the interpreter of the law, rose up to put Jesus to the test, and said unto him, Master, what shall I do to inherit eternal life? To which he answered, Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your strength and with all your mind..." Luke 10:27. When Jesus replied, he was referring to the foundation of the Law which is love of God and neighbor, the first four commandments if obeyed, prove that we love God, in the same way the six last commandments, if obeyed, prove that we love the next. Paul repeats Jesus' teaching based on the Old Testament, citing some of the commandments in the Decalogue that prove to love one's neighbor. "Owe no man anything, except to love one another; for he that loveth his neighbor hath fulfilled the law. For this: Thou shalt not commit adultery, thou shalt not kill, thou shalt not steal, thou shalt not covet, and if there be any other commandment , everything is summed up in this word: You shall love your neighbor as yourself. Love does not do evil against a neighbor; therefore love is the fulfillment of the law" Romans 13:8-10. In fact these two teachings were not innovated by Jesus or Paul, they simply quoted the Old Testament, "Love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your strength" Deuteronomy 6:5 . "Thou shalt not avenge thyself, nor bear a wrath against the children of thy people: but thou shalt love thy neighbor as thyself. I am the Lord" Leviticus 19:18 .

The ceremonial law, by divine inspiration, was written by Moses and delivered to the Levites, "When Moses had finished writing the entire words of this law in a book, he commanded the Levites who carried the ark of the covenant of the Lord, saying, Take this book of the Law, and set it by the side of the ark of the covenant of the LORD your God, that it may be there for a witness against you" Deuteronomy 31:24-26 . While the moral law of the ten commandments was written by God and given to Moses on Mount Sinai, "You came down on Mount Sinai, spoke to them from heaven and gave them righteous judgments, true laws, good statutes and commandments. You made known to him the Sabbath; commandments, statutes and law through your servant Moses you commanded them" Nehemiah 9:13-14. The Sabbath figured in the fourth commandment of the Law, calling the attention of the people, who for four hundred years were deprived of keeping the Sabbath, by the slavery suffered in Egypt. Therefore the Lord said, "Remember the Sabbath day, to keep it holy. Six days you shall labor and do all your work. But the seventh day is the Sabbath of the Lord your God; you shall not do any work, nor you, nor thy son, nor thy daughter, nor thy manservant, nor thy maidservant, nor thy cattle, nor the stranger within thy gates: for in six days the LORD made heaven and earth, the sea and everything in them, and rested the seventh day; therefore the Lord blessed the Sabbath day and hallowed it" Exodus 20:8-11. See, that was not an institution for the Hebrews at Sinai, the Lord said "remember" because they had forgotten them through the years of famine in Egypt. From then on, the Sabbath was once again respected, loved and kept by God's people, who did not buy or sell on that day, it was a consecrated day of worship to Jehovah. "Six days ye shall labor, but the seventh shall be the Sabbath of solemn rest, a holy convocation; ye shall do no work: it is the Sabbath of the Lord in all your dwellings" Leviticus 23:3 . "If the people of the land brought any merchandise or grain to sell on the Sabbath day, they would buy nothing from them on the Sabbath day, nor on the holy day..." Nehemiah 10:31 . "And Syrians also dwelt in Jerusalem, bringing fish and all sorts of goods, which they sold to the children of Judah in Jerusalem on the Sabbath. I contended with the nobles of Judah, and said to them, What is this evil that you do, by profaning the Sabbath? did your fathers thus, and did not our God bring all this evil upon us and upon this city? And ye bring still greater wrath upon Israel, by desecrating the Sabbath. When I had already overshadowed the gates of Jerusalem before the Sabbath, I commanded them to be shut, and I determined that they should not be opened until after the Sabbath; at the gates I placed some of my servants, so that no burden could enter on the Sabbath day" Nehemiah 13:16-19 . "Thus says the Lord: Take heed to your souls, do not carry burdens on the Sabbath day, nor bring them in through the gates of Jerusalem." Jeremiah 17:21 .

The Lord's people gathered to worship him always on Saturdays, New Moons and Feast Days, there is nothing left in the Ceremonial Law or in the entire Old Testament that authorizes us to accept that the resurrection of Christ changed the Sabbath day to the sunday. If the Sabbath were nailed to the cross, as some allege, it would be like, which it is not, and would certainly have some indication of such a change within the Plan of Redemption, and in the ceremonial system, however, Scripture teaches us that the Saturday and New Moons have always been the days of worship and worship of the Creator."They were to be present every morning to give thanks to the Lord and to praise him, and likewise in the evening and for each offering of the Lord's burnt offerings, on Saturdays, at the New Moon Feast and at the Fixed Feasts, before the Lord, according to the number determined" 1 Chronicles 23:30-31 . The Temple door was only opened on the days of worship, namely Saturday and New Moon. Ezekiel prophesies not only for Israel, but for the peoples of the earth to worship the Lord in the mentioned days."Thus says the Lord God: The door of the inner court that faces east will be closed during the six working days; but on the Sabbath it will be opened, and also on the day of the New Moon Feast. the porch of the gate, and he shall stand by the threshold of the gate: the priests shall prepare his burnt offering and his peace offerings, and he shall worship at the threshold of the gate, and he shall go out: but the gate shall not be shut until evening. from the same gate, on the Sabbaths and at the New Moon Feasts, before the Lord" Ezekiel 46:1-3 . "I also gave them my Sabbaths as a sign between me and them, that they might know that I am the Lord who sanctifies them" Ezekiel 20:12. It is imperative to emphasize that the Sabbath is a sign that distinguishes God from false gods, reports the prophet, "Thus I will say to them: The gods that did not make the heavens and the earth will perish from the earth and from under these heavens. The Lord made the earth by his power he established the world by his wisdom, and by his understanding he stretched out the heavens" Jeremiah 10:11-12 .

The Sabbath is a sign that reminds men that God made the heavens, the earth and everything in it, therefore, worthy of worship. "Hallow my Sabbaths, for they shall be a sign between me and you, that ye may know that I am the Lord your God" Ezekiel 20:20 .

The Sabbath is the sign which God has established in recognition of him, on the part of his people, as their only legitimate God.

"Therefore the children of Israel shall keep the Sabbath, keeping it for an everlasting covenant throughout their generations. It is a sign between me and the children of Israel forever; for in six days the LORD made heaven and earth, and seventh day, rested" Exodus 31:16-17 .

The Sabbath was the sign for Israel of old , and it is also the sign between God and his people in the last days, signed with the remnant.

Just as the Sabbath was the sign that distinguished Israel when they left Egypt to enter Canaan, so it is the sign that must distinguish God's people who leave the world to enter the heavenly rest. The Sabbath is a sign of the relationship between God and his people, a sign that they honor God's law. It is what distinguishes between God's faithful subjects and transgressors. The power that created all things is what restores the soul to its own likeness. For those who keep the Sabbath, that day is the sign of sanctification. The Sabbath is the sign that God recognizes them as his chosen people, the pledge that they will fulfill their part of the covenant.

THE SABBATH IN THE NEW TESTAMENT, IN THE TIME OF JESUS

The New Testament contains plausible information about Sabbath observance. especially the teachings of Christ concerning the Law and the Sabbath. The Master himself said that he did not come to abolish the Law or the prophets, therefore, he did not come to do away with the Law or to nail it to the cross. "Do not think that I came to abolish the law or the prophets; I did not come to abolish, but to fulfill. I tell you the truth: As long as heaven and earth exist, in no way will the smallest letter or the smallest stroke disappear from the Law, until all is fulfilled" Matthew 5:17-18 . Certainly the Law was not abolished, not even by faith. If Christ had nailed the Law and the Sabbath to the cross, Paul would not have repeated his teachings. "Do we then make void the law through faith? Not at all! On the contrary, we confirm the law" Romans 3:. Jesus came to magnify the Law in the hearts of men. "It pleased the Lord, for his righteousness' sake, to make his Law great, and to make it glorious" Isaiah 42:21 . "This is the covenant that I will make with them after those days, says the Lord: I will put my laws into their hearts, and I will write them on their minds" Hebrews 10:16 . Jesus came to justify sinners by faith, however, He can only justify penitent sinners who obey His Law, "For the mere hearers of the Law are not righteous before God, but those who do the Law will be justified" Romans 2 :13. It is imperative to mention that if Jesus is not going to justify the simple hearers of the Law, imagine what will happen to those who hate the Law and teach the people not to keep it. "Whoever therefore breaks one of these commandments, of the least, and so teaches men, will be considered least in the kingdom of heaven; but whoever observes and teaches them, he will be considered great in the kingdom of heaven." Matthew 5:19 . The word of the Lord announces something terrible to those who violate the commandments and omit the teaching of keeping God's law. "And if anyone takes away from the words of the book of this prophecy, God will take away his share from the tree of life, from the holy city, and from the things which are written in this book" Revelation 22:19 .

While the Messiah magnified the Law in the hearts of men, making it spiritual, Satan used instruments within Judaism, such as the rabbis, to make the Law and the Sabbath cold, formal, and a yoke. The Jews created traditions over the centuries and constantly accused Jesus, so it was with the tribute to Caesar, however, the first Sabbath controversy concerns the lawfulness of the disciples to gather grain on the Sabbath day. "It came to pass on the second-first Sabbath that Jesus went through the fields, and his disciples were plucking ears of grain, rubbing them with their hands, and eating them. And some Pharisees said to them, Why do you do what is not lawful for Saturdays?" Luke 6:1-2 . In their blind zeal, Orthodox Jews were very attached to their traditions, most, the Mishnah and the Talmud, these books contain rabbinical traditions prohibiting certain actions on the Sabbath day, forgetting the main points of the Law mercy, love and faith, so Jesus tried to teach them the true meaning of the Law, that is not focused on formalism, but on love of God and neighbor. The text alluded to in Luke speaks of a second-first Saturday, it is a New Moon, second to the first of the month. The accusation that Jesus was committing an illicit act was unfounded, Jesus knew the Scriptures and was supported by a Law in the Old Testament that says: "When you enter your neighbor's field, with your hands you shall pluck the grain; but you shall not sickle" Deuteronomy 23:25. It can be seen that neither Jesus nor the disciples committed any illicit act, the disciples were careful to pull the ears out with their hands, because it was allowed to pick the ears and thresh them with their hands to eat, they could not use the sickle, so there was no the transgression of the Sabbath, what Jesus infringed were the traditions contained in the mishnah and the Talmud, according to these books they committed two illicit acts, reaping and threshing. Jesus draws their attention to what David did when he was hungry, ate the showbread, and was blameless. And he ended by saying that the Son of Man is greater than the temple and Lord of the Sabbath which was made for man to rest and not of Sunday.

For the Pharisees, in gathering the ears, they were guilty of reaping, in rubbing them with their hands, they were guilty of threshing, in separating the grain from the chaff, they were guilty of winnowing; and, by the whole process, they were guilty of preparing a meal on the Sabbath day. But how much better is a man than a sheep? Therefore it is lawful on the Sabbath to do good." Matthew 12:9 -12. Jesus' answer is enlightening, the Sabbath is the day to do good, because the Sabbath is the Lord's day of worship, visiting, evangelizing, helping the needy, caring for the sick, it is a day dedicated to spiritual work. The Pharisees accused Jesus of transgressing their traditions on the Sabbath and Jesus asked why they transgress the Commandments of God because of traditions invented by them as a sign of holiness. God blessed the seventh day and sanctified it, because on it he rested from all his work. which he had accomplished at creation" Genesis 2:1-3 . At Sinai, the Sabbath was expounded in the fourth commandment of the Law of the Lord, codified and given to Moses so that all the people could return to keeping it after the years of hardship in the Egyptian captivity. "For in six days the LORD made heaven and earth, the sea, and all that in them is, and rested the seventh day; therefore the LORD blessed the Sabbath day and hallowed it" Exodus 20:11. It was an everlasting covenant made with Israel, the Sabbath is the sign between God and his people. "Therefore the children of Israel shall keep the Sabbath, keeping it for an everlasting covenant throughout their generations.
