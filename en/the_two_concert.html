THE TWO CONCERTS


The Eternal Covenant

Without a doubt, Adam's sin generated misery, disease and death, degenerating his descendants, according to the Scriptures: "The wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord. ." Romans 6:23 . As explained elsewhere, only a being with the same substance as God could lift man from his fallen nature, restoring the divine nature: "The Son is the radiance of the glory of God and the exact expression of his being, sustaining all things. by his mighty word. After he had performed cleansing from sins, he sat down at the right hand of the Majesty on high." Hebrews 1:3. Christ alone, Son of the Most High, enjoys the same nature as the Father, therefore, he has the power to regenerate the fallen sinner, exchanging his sinful nature for his righteousness. This is the only way to rescue humanity from death, the price demanded for transgressing the Law. It should be noted here that the Plan of Redemption was drawn up before the fall of man. The aforementioned plan was not created solely for Israel, in fact this nation did not even exist in the elaboration of the Plan, but for every man overcome by original sin. In fact, the way chosen by God to save humanity was the divine covenant, first with Adam, stigmatized with a promise: "I will put enmity between you and the woman, between your offspring and her offspring. He will bruise your head, and thou shalt bruise his heel." Genesis 3:15 . The aforementioned covenant, known theologically as the proto-gospel covenant, symbolizes the first proclamation of the gospel. Where Christ defeats Satan. Satan, in turn, tries to bite the woman, causing the servants of God to sin fed by the sinful nature, in another turn, Christ crushes the serpent's head, clothing his church with the divine nature, enabling them to love and obey the commandments. God's. According to Paul's writings: "And the God of peace will soon crush Satan under his feet." Romans 16:20 . While the restless Satan lays snares for the stumbling of the people of God, the faithful must persevere in obedience to the doctrine confident in the righteousness of Christ, their only begotten Saviour, perpetuating the divine covenant.

At the end of the calamitous days of Adam, God later made a covenant with Noah. It was a covenant based on the promise of grace and life. The Creator solemnly promised to preserve the life of creatures on the face of the earth: "Behold, I establish my covenant with you, and with your descendants, and with every living creature that is with you; both the birds, the domestic animals and the animals wild beasts that came out of the ark like all the beasts of the earth. I establish my covenant with you; Genesis 9:9-11 . In view of the fact that it is a promise of mercy for all living beings, the aforementioned covenant was coined an eternal covenant:"The bow will be in the clouds; I will see it, and I will remember the everlasting covenant between God and every living creature of all flesh that is on the earth. And God said to Noah, This is the sign of the covenant between me and all flesh on the earth." Genesis 9:16-17 . Since then, the Lord has left the rainbow as a perpetual sign in heaven, remembering his promise never again to destroy the world by the waters. As he did in the days of Noah.

After the flood, the Lord made a covenant of grace with Abraham, based on the glorious promise: "Now the Lord said to Abram, 'Leave your country, your relatives, and your father's house, and go to the land that I will show you. I will make you a great nation, and I will bless you, and make your name great. Be a blessing! I will bless those who bless you, and those who curse you I will curse; in you all the families of the earth shall be blessed." Genesis 12:1-3 . Indeed, the Lord assured Abram that he would greatly multiply his descendants: "Then he brought him outside, and said, Look up to the heavens, and count the stars, if you are able. And he said to him, So shall your descendants be." Genesis 15:5. Meanwhile, up to this time Abram in old age, had no children, in this context the Lord said: "I will establish my covenant as an everlasting covenant between me and you and your future descendants, to be your God and the God of your descendants. " Genesis 17:7 . When the time of trial was over, the Lord fulfilled the promise: "The name Abraham gave to the son that was born to him, which Sarah bore to him." Genesis 21:3. Elsewhere (elsewhere) we address the topic at hand, Abraham's seed is not limited to literal Israel. The Lord, pointed out one particular descendant of Abraham, Christ, the promised Messiah, at this time, the Lord announced the Gospel in advance to his faithful servant. Henceforth the Gentiles would be blessed, they would be part of the seed of Abraham, therefore they would be heirs of the Patriarch by faith, according to the promise: "As for Abraham, he believed in God, and it was counted to him for righteousness. it is those of faith who are the children of Abraham: and the Scripture, foreseeing that God would justify the Gentiles by faith, preached the gospel to Abraham, saying, In you shall all people be blessed: so that in Abraham those of faith are blessed. " Galatians 3:6-9. Regardless of the nationality of the penitent sinner, "Simon related how God first visited the Gentiles, to take from them a people for his name." Acts 15:14 , any man justified by faith, through repentance, confession of sins, and conversion, is an heir of Abram in Christ.

According to Paul's writings, the promises made to Abraham led to a single heir: "Now the promises were made to Abraham and to his seed. only: And to thy seed, which is Christ." Galatians 3:16 . The divine promise, the well-known everlasting covenant, being fulfilled: "If ye be Christ's, then are ye Abraham's seed, and an heir according to the promise." Galatians 3:29 . Read, in the tenor of the everlasting covenant, we are children of Abraham by faith: "Know therefore that they of faith are the children of Abraham." Galatians 3:7. All (Jews and Gentiles), by faith, are part of the lineage of Abraham, considered people of God, especially the Gentiles, rescued by the Lord from darkness to his wonderful light: "He showed Simon how God, first, visited the Gentiles, in order to appoint a people from among them for his name." Acts 15:14 . In fact, the gospel was announced beforehand by God to Abraham: "Now the Scriptures, foreseeing that God would justify the Gentiles by faith, preached the gospel beforehand to Abraham, saying, In you shall all people be blessed." Galatians 3:8 . In this tuning fork, the true worshipers, descendants of Abraham, would not be those of the flesh, but, by spirit and by faith, those who fear the Lord in any nation. In this sense, Peter asserts:"I truly know that God is no respecter of persons, but in every nation he who fears him and does what is right is acceptable to him." Acts 10:34-35 . Without a doubt, Paul stands out as an apostle to the Gentiles, therefore, he was commissioned by the heir of Abraham, Christ, with the following function: "To open their eyes and to turn them from darkness to light and from the power of Satan to God, that they may receive remission of sins and an inheritance among those who are sanctified by faith in me." Acts 26:18 . On his calamitous journey, Jesus reported, "Salvation comes from the Jew" , however, He refers to spiritual Jews and not to those of the flesh, as can be seen:"You worship what you do not know; we worship what we know, because salvation comes from the Jews. But the hour is coming, and now is, when the true worshipers will worship the Father in spirit and in truth; for these are the ones the Father seeks. for your worshippers." John 4:22-23 . Following in the footsteps of the Messiah, Paul ratifies his teachings by saying, "not all of Israel are indeed Israelites." , the true Israelites and heirs of the promise are by faith in Christ and not by the flesh:"And let us not think that the word of God has failed, for not all Israel are indeed Israelites; nor, because they are descendants of Abraham, are all his children; but, In Isaac shall thy seed be called. That is, these The children of God are not properly those of the flesh, but the children of the promise are to be regarded as offspring." Romans 9:6-8 . In fact, the celebrated apostle refers to the eternal covenant signed with Abraham, to multiply his descendants through the Messiah, scattering them among the peoples of the earth: "The remnant of Jacob shall be in the midst of many peoples, as the dew of the Lord, as drizzle on the grass, which waits not for man, neither depend on the children of men." Micah 5:7. Indeed, the servants of the Lord trust in deliverance, for the Lord promised to help the descendants of Abraham, heirs through faith in Christ, repentant and bowing down at the Master's feet confessing their sins: "For evidently he does not help angels, but the seed of Abraham." Hebrews 2:16 . They are scattered among the peoples, known as the blessed lineage of the Lord, they bear the credentials of the divine nature, zealously defending the Sound Doctrine: "And their seed shall be known among the nations, and their seed among the peoples; all that shall come , will soon know them, for they are the lineage that the Lord has blessed." Isaiah 61:9.. This little flock, keep the commandments of God boldly confirming the faith of Jesus; according to Revelation 14:12, where the apostle John refers to the remnant, the church of the last days, keepers of the commandments, statutes, and precept, as well as Abraham, upheld by the divine nature of Christ (has the faith of Jesus), i.e. , it is only possible to love and obey God and his Law, if the believer is born again, renewed by the divine nature of Christ in substitution of the evil sinful nature.

As already exposed, in confirming the eternal covenant with Isaac, the Lord points to the election of Abraham as the father of faith, and his descendants, the heirs of the promise in Christ Jesus: "For Abraham obeyed my word and kept my commandments, my precepts, my statutes and my laws." Genesis 26:5 . Therefore, it became evident, the Lord did not exempt the seed of Abraham in Christ Jesus from obedience to his holy law, Christ came to impute his divine nature in the hearts of his servants, enabling them to keep the commandments, statutes, precepts and Laws for Love.



The Circumcision of the Heart

As explained elsewhere, the Lord entered into an alliance with Noah, symbol of the rainbow. Likewise, he made a covenant with Abraham, confirmed through circumcision: "And God said to Abraham, You shall keep my covenant, you and your descendants throughout their generations. This is my covenant, which you shall keep between me and you and thy seed: Every male among you shall be circumcised." Genesis 17:9-10 . The covenant, via circumcision, wielded the power to keep the Hebrews separate and free from the idolatrous contamination of heathen nations.

With this sum, Abraham obeyed the commandments, statutes, precepts and law, the result of the solid alliance signed with his Creator, according to Sister White's reports:

He (Abraham) was given the rite of circumcision, which was a sign that those who received it were dedicated to the service of God—assurance that they would remain separate from idolatry and would obey the law of God.
WHITE, Ellen Golden. Patriarchs and Prophets. 9. ed. Ed. Brazilian Publishing House, 1989. p. 377

Even in ancient times, in the time of ancient Israel, the Lord enlightened the Hebrews about true circumcision, extolling circumcision of the heart over mere circumcision in the flesh: "Circumcise therefore your heart, and stiffen your neck no more. " Deuteronomy 10:16 . The circumcision of the heart rooted in the solid foundations of love, zeal and devotion, in fact, would result in memorable obedience to the holy divine Law, keeping Israel separate from idolatry: "Take heed lest your heart be deceived, and you go astray, and serve other gods, and bow down to them." Deuteronomy 11:16. The aforementioned covenant was established by faith. With the descendants of Abraham, the Lord made an exclusive promise, he promised to circumcise their hearts and not only the flesh, imputing his righteousness, with the aim of keeping them separate from paganism, able to love and obey his Commandments, Statutes, Precepts and Laws: "The Lord your God will circumcise your heart and the heart of your descendants, that you may love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul, that you may live." Deuteronomy 30:6 . The famous circumcision, should translate into a robust covenant of love and obedience, however, Israel did not fulfill the terms agreed with the Lord. Mere circumcision in the flesh failed, the Hebrews relied on self-righteousness, succumbing to icy formalism, drawing stern rebuke from the Lord:"Circumcise yourselves to the Lord, circumcise your hearts, O men of Judah and inhabitants of Jerusalem, lest my wrath go out like fire and burn, and none can quench it, because of the wickedness of your works." Jeremiah 4:4 . With this approach, the Israelites despised vigorous circumcision of the heart, submerging themselves in despicable insane ceremonialism, resulting in apostasy and inevitable turning away from the Lord. On the subject, it is necessary to question the function of circumcision for the descendants of Abraham in Christ, the remnant. Paul asks, "What then is the advantage of a Jew? Or what is the use of circumcision?" Romans 3:1 . The answer is seen to emerge in the sacred pages, when the said apostle exhorts:"And he received the sign of circumcision, the seal of the righteousness of faith, while he was uncircumcised, that he might be the father of all those who believe, while he was also uncircumcised; in order that righteousness also might be imputed to him." Romans 4:11 . Within this context, it is worth highlighting the fact that the renowned apostle links circumcision with righteousness by faith, ie, circumcision is umbilically linked to the righteousness of Christ, removing the impurities of the sinful nature, reconciling the penitent sinner with God. In the illuminating writings of Wagoner's work, Elliet J. Letter to the Romans, he says:

If we remember that circumcision was given as a sign of righteousness by faith, and that the inheritance promised to Abraham and his seed was according to the righteousness of faith; we shall understand that circumcision was the pledge (or mortgage) of that inheritance. The apostle declares, we obtain the inheritance in Christ: “Having also believed in him, ye were sealed with the Holy Spirit of promise; who is the pledge of our inheritance, to the redemption of his possession…” (Eph. 1:10-14). The possession promised to Abraham and his seed was secured only through the Spirit of righteousness; therefore, from the beginning there was no authentic circumcision divorced from the Spirit.
Wagoner, Elliet J. Letter to the Romans

Looking in this light, it follows, circumcision in Christ or the imputation of his righteousness in the heart, regenerates the sinner by perfecting the believer, "In him also you are perfected. He is the head of all principality and power. circumcised, not through hands, but in putting off the body of the flesh, which is the circumcision of Christ." Colossians 2:10-11 .

In this measure, it is inappropriate to equate circumcision with mere incision in the flesh fulfilling ceremonialism. From its birth, circumcision is linked to the imputation of Christ's righteousness in the heart. Circumcision carries the same meaning, both at the beginning of the Lord's demand, and at a subsequent time. At any stage of the church circumcision means the righteousness of faith, for example the Patriarch Abraham: "He believed in the Lord, and it was counted to him for righteousness." Genesis 15:6 . It is necessary not to lose sight of the enlightening teachings of the prophet Jeremiah: "The Lord Our Justice." Jeremiah 23:6. In the prophet's view, the sinner has no righteousness, the righteousness exercised by Adam's descendants is borrowed from Christ. In this way, Christ, the only true descendant of Abraham, according to the plan of Redemption, replaces the sinful nature with his divine nature, perfecting the penitent sinner by reconciling him to God. Thus, it remains to be clarified, the sinner naked of the righteousness of Christ (divine nature), is not circumcised, certainly will not be sealed by the writing angel. The only way to keep the church immaculate, separate from the world and the rites of paganism is to acquire the righteousness of Christ through the circumcision of the heart, if Christ dwells in the heart imputing his Spirit, there will surely be renewal, love and obedience to the Creator and the your law. The Remnant cannot make the mistake of the Jews. WAGGONER adds:

The Jews made the mistake of thinking that it was enough to have the sign (of circumcision in the flesh). They finally came to harbor the idea that the possession of the sign would bring about reality, in precisely the same way many professing Christians of our day suppose that the performance of certain ordinances will make them members of the body of Christ and partakers of its righteousness. However, circumcision of the flesh alone cannot represent righteousness (divine nature) but original sin (sinful nature), when sustained by mere formalism.

In fact, the Jews despised Gentiles, brothers-in-law of the uncircumcised, because they did not present the mark in the flesh, in fact they did not need to, according to the Council of Jerusalem: "For it seemed good to the Holy Spirit and to us not to impose any greater burden on you than these essential things. ." Acts 15:28 , although debtors of the circumcision in the flesh, the Gentiles were circumcised in the heart, accepting Christ and his righteousness, a new creature arose: "For in Christ Jesus neither circumcision nor uncircumcision has any power, but the being a new creature." Galatians 6:15. Without a doubt, the Jews were lost suffocated by formalism, they despised circumcision of the heart, clinging to circumcision only in the flesh, with this, they rejected the Messiah, they died suffocated by their own righteousness. They were not born again, much less were they transformed by the divine nature into a new creature.

It is worth noting the content of Paul's writings, in his view, circumcision in the flesh does not make us a true Jew, the true Jew is in the spirit and true circumcision is that of the heart: "For he is not a Jew who is one only outwardly, nor circumcision is that which is in the flesh only. But he is a Jew that is inwardly, and circumcision is that which is of the heart, in the spirit, not according to the letter, and whose praise is not of men but of God." Romans 2:28-29 . The terms used here by the apostle Paul, “circumcision” and “uncircumcision” do not have the power to address rites or their absence, it refers to two classes of people. Uncircumcision, without a shadow of a doubt, points out the Gentiles, worshipers of other gods:"On the contrary, when they saw that the gospel of the uncircumcision was entrusted to me, as the gospel of the circumcision was committed to Peter. For he who worked effectually in Peter for the apostleship of the circumcision, he also worked in me with effect toward the Gentiles." Galatians 2:7-8 . The fact that the believer is or is not circumcised in the flesh is not relevant, but the circumcision of the heart by Christ is relevant: "In him also you were circumcised, not with hands, but putting off the body of the flesh, which is the circumcision of Christ." Colossians 2:11 . Christ dwells in the hearts of his servants, the remnant, through his Holy Spirit compelling them to love and obey the Law of God, only in this way can they keep the commandments of God:"To be circumcised means nothing, and to be uncircumcised means nothing; what it means to do is to keep the commandments of God." I Corinthians 7:19 . Whoever is not circumcised in heart by Christ, will resist the Holy Spirit and the truth, becoming a transgressor of God's commandments, these are: "You stiff-necked and uncircumcised in heart and ears, you always resist the Holy Spirit. as your fathers did, so do you." Acts 7:51. Circumcision of the heart is healthy and of vital importance to the church of God, in another turn, circumcision in the flesh is useless. Circumcision of the heart makes it possible for the believer to receive divine nature to obey God's commandments. The truly circumcised, members of the Remnant church stand firm in maintaining the doctrine and obedience to the Law of the Lord led by the Holy Spirit: "For we are the circumcised, we who worship by the Spirit of God and who make the Messiah Jesus our trust! we trust in human qualifications." Philippians 3:3. Finally, it can be safely concluded that the fact that circumcision means the seal of the righteousness of Christ, reveals its importance for the servants of the Lord, believers circumcised in their hearts by Christ, who love and keep his commandments, therefore, the law does not was made void by faith, much less abolished, on this track Paul preaches: "Since God is one, who justifies the circumcision by faith, and the uncircumcision through faith. Do we then make void the law through faith? , before we lay down the law." Romans 3:30-31 . True faith is shared with the servants of God, whose hearts have been circumcised by Christ, dedicating themselves perseveringly to the maintenance of the doctrine and love of the holy Law.



Sinai Covenant

It is good manners to begin the present study by approaching the Sinaitic alliance (at Sinai), given the analysis elsewhere of ancient alliances signed by the Lord from Adam to Abraham. Thus, it is necessary to invoke the Treatise on Adventist Theology defining the aforementioned covenant as follows:

The covenant of grace with Abraham (Gen 12:1-3; 15:1-5; 17:1-14) is fundamental to the entire course of salvation (Gal 3:6-9; 15-18). Through Abraham's seed, which refers not only to his numerous descendants, but in particular to his only descendant, Christ (v.16), God blessed the world. All who would be part of Abraham's seed would discover that God is his God, and that they are his people. Circumcision would be a sign (Gen 17:11) of that rightand already existing relationship with God constituted by faith (Gen 15:6; Rom 4:9-12). The Sinaitic covenant, entered into in the context of redemption from Egyptian captivity (Ex. 19:4; 20:2), contained divine sacrificial provisions for atonement and forgiveness of sin. It was also a covenant of grace and a reiteration of the main emphases contained in the Abrahamic covenant, including God's special relationship with his people.
DEDEREN, Raoul. Treatise on Seventh-day Adventist Theology. V.9. 1.ed. Ed. Brazilian publishing house, p. 311

In the alluded period the law was codified, the sacrifices, ceremonies and cults underwent a striking transformation in their form and organization pointing to the Messiah.

In this step, it should be said, the law codified at Sinai is the same law first mentioned in Scripture, when the Lord revealed his will to Isaac at Gerar: "For Abraham obeyed my word and kept my commandments, my precepts , my statutes and my laws." Genesis 26:5. According to assertions from the Adventist Theology Treaty (2011). Keeping the law as Abraham obeyed means adhering to Jehovah, to his person, to his instructions and will. For Abraham, the covenant and the law of God were one, coexisting harmoniously. Abraham did not keep divine revelations and instructions accidentally or sporadically, but voluntarily, intensely and out of divine love stimulated by the Spirit of God. The promise of God made with Isaac was extended to Jacob by decree and to Israel by an everlasting covenant: "He is the LORD our God; his judgments pervade all the earth. for a thousand generations; of the covenant which he made with Abraham, and of the oath which he swore to Jacob as a decree, and to Israel as an everlasting covenant." 1 Chronicles 16:14-17. The inevitable advent of the promised Messiah, bound Christ to the covenant and perfecting of the Law for all eternity: "That the covenant being formerly confirmed by God in Christ, the law, which came after four hundred and thirty years, does not, way to abolish the promise." Galatians 3:17 . It should be noted that the advent of the Messiah did not invalidate the covenant with the descendants of Abraham, much less invalidate the Commandments. The Law was carved on tablets after four hundred and thirty years of Egyptian captivity, read, it existed since creation, however it was codified on Sinai by the finger of God and delivered to Moses, solidly confirming the covenant signed between God and the descendants of Abraham in Christ Jesus.

That said, it is unavoidable to highlight the importance of the law, as well as its validity; based on God's will. The covenant demands obedience, yet man is unable to obey alone, the law is the expression of divine will and the basis of his covenant. Thus, when the people detach themselves from the righteousness of Christ, they obey the law by mere formal principles of conduct or cultic ordinances, stripped of consecration and love, breaking the covenant, like the Hebrews, therefore, God sent his prophet to rebuke Israel: "They have transgressed my covenant and rebelled against my law." Hosea 8:1 . Obedience without love is rebellion, in the divine view, the sinner stripped of the divine nature, is moved by the sinful nature in filthy rags.

Within this context, it is necessary to highlight the existence of two covenants: According to the Scriptures, the old and the new covenants, meanwhile, at first it is necessary to analyze the old covenant.

When Israel was freed from Egypt, led by Moses to Mount Sinai, the commandments of the Lord were passed from father to son orally, vitalizing the sacred covenant, at Sinai, the covenant or covenant of God with Israel was codified with the following proposal: "Having They came from Rephidim into the wilderness of Sinai, where they encamped: there, therefore, Israel encamped opposite the mountain. Now therefore, if ye will indeed obey my voice, and keep my covenant, then ye shall be my peculiar possession. above all peoples; you will be to me a kingdom of priests and a holy nation." Exodus 19:2;5-6 . Hearing the terms of the covenant, all the people accepted with an oath: "All that the Lord has spoken we will do." Exodus 19:8. After ratifying the covenant with Israel, the Lord gave Moses the ten commandments written with his own finger: "And the Lord gave me the two tables of stone, written with the finger of God; and I had written on them according to all the words that the Lord had written. spoke to you on the mountain out of the midst of the fire in the day of the congregation." Deuteronomy 9:10 , the covenant established was intended to guide the people, the holy nation, to live consecrated lives in strict obedience to the Creator's commandments, precepts, and laws. Avoiding to absorb defilements and practices of heathen nations, in this sense the Lord warned:"You shall not do after the works of the land of Egypt, where you dwelt, nor do after the works of the land of Canaan, where I am bringing you, nor walk in their statutes. walk in them. I am the Lord your God." Leviticus 18:3-4 . The Sinaitic covenant celebrated with Israel was the first codified covenant, linking the keeping of the ten commandments, statutes, precepts and laws, similar to the verbal covenant signed with Abraham in (Genesis, 26:5), as recommended by the sacred Scriptures:"Then he announced to you his covenant, which he prescribed for you, the ten commandments, and he wrote them on two tables of stone. which you come to possess." Deuteronomy 4:13-14 . Indeed, the Hebrews promised to respect the covenant made at Sinai, obeying commandments, statutes, precepts, and laws out of love, according to the divine requirement: "Now therefore, O Israel, what does the Lord require of you? LORD your God, and walk in his ways, and love him, and serve the LORD your God with all your heart and with all your soul, to keep the commandments of the LORD and his statutes that I give you this day. command for your good?" Deuteronomy 10:12-13. Meanwhile, Israel did not pay attention to this particular, failed, flooded with formalism and self-righteousness, breaking the covenant signed with the Creator, kept the law formally devoid of love, this was the cause of the fragility and failure of this alliance forcing the renowned apostle Paul to exhort: " What then shall we say? That the Gentiles, who did not seek justification, came to it, yet that which comes from faith, and Israel, which sought the law of righteousness, did not attain to that law. did not come from faith, but as from works. They stumbled at the stumbling block." Romans 9:30-32 . As seen elsewhere, in Christ the Gentiles reached the promise made between God and Abraham, they did not trust in his righteousness, but in the redeemer:"Jesus the Messiah did this so that, in union with him, the Gentiles might receive the blessing announced to Abraham so that through trust and faithfulness we might receive what was promised, that is, the Spirit." Galatians 3:14 . On the other hand, Israel tried to achieve justice by meritorious works, fulfillment of legalistic duty, they tried the impossible, no man is able to obey the law apart from faith in the redeemer and his righteousness . Furthermore, righteousness by faith is the foundation of Moses' teaching on obedience to the law and all the Old Testament foundations, so it is the foundation of Paul's teachings in the New Testament.

Furthermore, obeying the law does not nullify the faith relationship between God and his people. It is the opposite: "Do we then make void the law because of faith? Not at all! On the contrary, we confirm the law." Romans 3:31 , only by putting on the faith and righteousness of Christ is it possible to obey the Law. When obedience is shined by self-righteousness, that is, obedience by effort, human capacity or self-righteousness, an abyss opens between God and sinner, based on the divine vision as filth: "But we are all like an unclean thing, and all our righteousnesses as filthy rags." Isaiah 64:6. In the sense of the text, human effort directed at obedience to the Lord and his Law, dissociated from the divine nature of the Messiah, is nothing but filthy rags, the sinful nature inherited from Adam (original sin), is the thread separating creature and Creator: "But your iniquities have separated between you and your God; and your sins have hid his face from you, that he will not hear." Isaiah 59:2 . When the obedience of the law is sustained by self-righteousness, human effort is stripped of faith, divine nature, for this reason, the law cannot achieve its purpose of pointing out sin by shining the religious life, the poor soul will receive death. spiritual, distancing from the Lord and from his true doctrine:"And the commandment which was for life, I have found that this same commandment has become unto me unto death." Romans 7:10 . Lost is the soul suffocated by self-righteousness, they think they keep the commandments, however they exalt formalism and boasting, they are nothing but fetid spiritual corpses, dead to God: For this simple reason the apostle Paul wrote to the Galatians, warning them of the danger of obedience legalist of the law, or as he called it by the works of the law, such are in a curse for rejecting the righteousness of the Messiah. The only way for man to obey the law of God is by faith and love compelled by the divine nature imputed in the heart:"For everyone who trusts in the legalistic observance of the commandments of the law lives under a curse, because it is written: Cursed is everyone who does not keep doing all the things written in the book of the law. It is evident that no one will be declared righteous by God through legalism , because the person who is just will obtain life through trust and faithfulness." Galatians 3:10-11 . In this wake, Paul equates the Sinai covenant with the curse or slavery in other translations, mentioned in the aforementioned text in Galatians.

The Sinai covenant, on the other hand, does not portray curse or slavery to the faithful, obedient to the great Lawgiver covered by faith in the Redeemer, free from self-righteousness and works of the law. In fact, when the Law is dissociated from promise and faith, it feeds self-righteousness, filthy rags, consequently the covenant is distorted, bringing as a result slavery in place of freedom. In this bias, trusting in self-righteousness enslaves the sinner, captive to the satisfaction of his will and not that of God. They think they are serving God, when in reality they are satisfying themselves by feeding their sinful nature, and they are shipwrecked in fanaticism. In fact, the Hebrews did not keep the law of God out of love, directly hurting the Covenant signed at Sinai by fetid formalism, with this, a second covenant was necessary,"Indeed, if the first covenant had not given room for the discovery of errors, there would have been no need for the second. By using the term new, he made the first covenant old; and everything that becomes old, and goes through the process aging process is on its way to total disappearance." Hebrews 8:7;13 . The great error of Judaism was to trust in its righteousness, they tried to obey the law by its merits, they nurtured a sinful nature, and not the imposing divine justice of the Messiah: "For, not knowing the justice of God, and seeking to establish their own, they did not the one that comes from God." Romans 10:3, in their view, circumcision in the flesh, formalistic obedience or fulfillment of duty would enable them to perpetuate the covenant with the Creator, however, they failed by rejecting the life-giver and divine nature of Jesus, killing him on a cross. By stoning Stephen in the year 34, they consecrated their final rejection as a unique, chosen and peculiar people, separating themselves forever from the covenant, forcing the Lord to establish a new covenant with another people, ratified by the blood of Christ. In short, the problem is not found in the Law, it lies dormant in the way they intended to keep it.



Abrahamic Covenant

At first, it is necessary to bring up the scope of the Divine plan, to sanctify the people, both in the old and in the new covenant. In the old covenant, the Most High made a covenant with Israel at Sinai through Moses, the Lord said: "Ye shall be to me a kingdom of priests and an holy nation. These are the words which ye shall speak unto the children of Israel." Exodus 19:6 . In the new covenant, entered into through the Messiah with the justified descendants of Abraham, Jesus said: "Be ye therefore perfect, as your heavenly Father is perfect." Matthew 5:48. The new covenant wields the power to restore the sinner to the likeness of Adam before the fall, however, there is a rough stir gravitating around a problem, man has lost his divine nature, and with it his sanctification per se. The sinner attains perfection only if Christ imputes his righteousness to his heart, enabling him to obey God's law, holy, just, and perfect.

On the subject, we see Christ's work in the new covenant regenerating the sinner, presenting his church before the Most High, holy, pure and perfect, clothed in her divine nature: "To sanctify her, cleansing her with the washing of water, by the word , with glory, without spot, without wrinkle, or anything like that, but that it may be holy and without blemish." Ephesians 5:26-27 . The Plan of Redemption aims to restore the penitent sinner to the same divine nature displayed by Adam before the fall. However, to fulfill the divine purpose, it is necessary: ​​"Christ shall dwell in your hearts by faith, rooted and rooted in love." Ephesians 3:17. The only way for the sinner to obey the Commandments of God is anchored in the righteousness of Christ, the Lord our Righteousness justifying the sinner by faith. In the new covenant, Jesus restores the soul by replacing the sinful nature with his righteousness, ie, man receives the nature of Christ filling his heart with righteousness, enabling him to love and obey the Lord. God measures human righteousness by the ability to obey his doctrine and the law, that is, "the law is holy, and the commandment is holy and just and good." Romans 7:12, man, in turn, is sinful, unjust and evil. Man can only become righteous by being born again by receiving the Spirit of Christ (Holy Spirit), enabling him to obey the Law. God expresses his justice in the Law by demanding obedience out of love. Without a doubt, sin is manifested by the absence of Christ's righteousness in the human heart breaking the law: "Whoever practices sin also transgresses the law, for sin is the transgression of the law." I John 3:4. As already exhaustively exposed, the sin of Adam absorbed the sinful nature (original sin) after losing the divine nature, impairing his ability to obey out of love, the alluded sin was transferred to his descendants, whose fruit produces rebellion and transgression of God's law. . Only by the new covenant is the believer justified by faith, clothed in the righteousness of Christ shining his character. As eye drops dispelling darkness, the believer is able to look by faith at the manifest justice of the Creator carved in the law, rescuing the pleasure of obeying with intense love. The justified believer, endowed with the righteousness of Christ, becomes so holy with the Law, reactivating the ability to love, obey and defend.

That said, it is unavoidable to invoke the covenant of Sinai, where the Hebrews sought justice anchored in the formalism of the law, for their merits (works), flooded with self-righteousness, ignored God's justice, seeking to establish their own justice, denied submission to the justice of God (Romans 10:3), this was the reason for the break with the old covenant and the fall of Judaism, they established their righteousness (self righteousness) ignoring faith in the righteousness of the Messiah. Israel anchored her salvation in obedience to the law, by formalism, by her merits and not by Christ's by faith. By forcing the Lord in the new covenant, to establish a bond by the Spirit together with the free will of man, to obey out of love. In the new covenant, the law of the Lord would no longer be carved on stone tablets, cold, lifeless, slumbering in the heart:"I will put My law within them, and I will write it on their hearts; and I will be their God, and they shall be my people." Jeremiah 31:33 . In the new covenant, Christ perfected the form of obedience to God and his Law, in his ministry he labored by imputing his righteousness to magnify the Law in penitent hearts: "It pleased the Lord, for his righteousness' sake, to magnify the law and make it great. la glorious." Isaiah 42:21 , therefore, there was no elimination of the Law and obedience. The obedience of the Law, in the new covenant, was perfected with the salutary help of the Holy Spirit, imputing in the heart justice borrowed from Christ, enhancing human spirituality:"And indeed, rebuking them, God says, Behold, the days come, saith the Lord, and I will make a new covenant with the house of Israel and with the house of Judah. ​​Not like the covenant which I made with their fathers in day when I took them by the hand to bring them out of the land of Egypt: because they did not abide in my covenant, and I ceased to be anxious for them, says the LORD: for this is the covenant that I will make with the house of Israel, after those days, saith the Lord. I will put my laws in their minds, and write them in their hearts; I will be their God, and they shall be my people." Hebrews 8:8-10 . In the new covenant the Lord not only placed his law, but also his Spirit in the human heart, enabling the believer to love and obey with a heart sanctified by the new nature, the divine:"I will give you a new heart and put a new spirit within you; I will take away your stony heart and give you a heart of flesh. you watch them." Ezekiel 36:26-27 , the aforementioned covenant was signed by the Spirit, supplying hearts with righteousness, compelling man to obey out of love, devotion and joy, the law was engraved in the heart, even of the Gentiles, "these show the standard of the engraved law in his heart, and his conscience and his thoughts also testify to him." Romans 2:15 , born again, the sinner enjoys new nature, captive to the noble feelings of doing God's will, "I delight to do Thy will, O my God; within my heart, Psalms 40:8 . In this bias, the new covenant was guided by two fundamental principles, the expiatory sacrifice confirmed with blood and the presence of the Spirit, hidden or not perceived in the first covenant. The old covenant was sanctioned at Sinai, by the letter of the law, and confirmed by the blood of animals offered in sacrifice, with Moses as mediator: "And he took the book of the covenant, and read it before the people, and the people said, We will do and we will obey all that the LORD has said. And Moses took the blood, and sprinkled it on the people, and said, Behold the blood of the covenant which the LORD has made with us concerning all these things." Exodus 24:7-8 . The apostle Paul, writing to the Hebrews, shares the aforementioned understanding:"Therefore even the first covenant was not made without blood; for when Moses had finished proclaiming all the commandments according to the law to all the people, he took the blood of calves and goats, with water, red wool, and hyssop, and sprinkled not only the book itself, but also all the people." Hebrews 9:18-19 . Indeed, the old covenant was confirmed with the blood of animals, however, deprived of the Holy Spirit, thus, it fell into formalism. On another turn, the new covenant was sanctioned with the precious blood of Christ, confirmed by the Holy Spirit, remaining forever alive and effective.

On this path, the sanctioning sacrifice of the new covenant is carried out on Calvary via the sacrifice and blood of the Messiah on the cross, with Jesus as mediator, according to the writings of Paul: "Much more the blood of Christ, which by the Spirit eternal, offered himself without blemish to God, will cleanse our conscience from acts that lead to death, that we may serve the living God! For this reason, he is the Mediator of the new covenant so that those who are called may receive the promise of the eternal inheritance, since death took place as a ransom for the transgressions committed under the first covenant." Hebrews 9:14-15. The other sacred presupposition endorsing the new covenant, according to Paul, is the covenant of the Spirit, transcending the dry, lifeless code as written in the old covenant, rooted in the Spirit of Christ, or his righteousness "which enables us to be ministers of a new covenant, not of letters, but of the Spirit; for the letter kills, and the Spirit gives life." II Corinthians 3:6 . Without the influence of the Holy Spirit, every attempt at covenant or obedience to the Law of God is fruitless, doomed to failure.

In fact, the old covenant was written on tables of stone, while the new covenant is engraved by the Spirit, or divine nature imputed to the heart. Thus, the content of the two covenants is the same, there was no change in this particular, the problem does not lie in the content, the law of God, but in the way they tried to keep it. The old covenant failed because of the severe insistence of the Hebrews on obeying the Law by meritorious works, they tried unsuccessfully to achieve justice by keeping the law formally and not by spirit, love and faith. In the old covenant the Jews focused on outward appearance, their justice limited to the recognition and praise of men: "All they do is in order to be seen by men; therefore they wear broad sashes and long fringes on their garments." Matthew 23:5. In view of the alleged, Jesus teaches his church to deviate from the fatal error, exposed in the old covenant, supported by self -righteousness: "Beware of doing your righteousness before men to be seen by them. your Heavenly Father." Matthew 6:1. The second covenant focuses on the interior of the heart illuminated by the divine nature, Christ dwells in me, compelling me to love and obey him. Within this context, it is worth mentioning that the law obeyed without the divine nature, sustained by the sinful nature, invalidates the sacrifice of Christ, breaking with the divine covenant, when man puts his effort in simple human behavior, commits a fatal mistake, whoever exercises such a procedure, nullifies the atoning sacrifice of Christ and its benefits, if the sinner is able to attain sanctification by his merits, Jesus would not need to die for him, if man were capable of himself, to change the sinful nature, to justify himself and to sanctify, Christ would not have to die.

Indeed, the covenant loses its purpose when it is exercised by self-righteousness, in formalistic obedience, only to be seen and exalted by men. In the new covenant, the believer receives life when he serves the Lord by the spirit, with a new nature conceived by Christ, and not in obedience to the letter of the law, leveraging carnal nature, the guiding thread of spiritual death, carried out by formalistic obedience to the law, in this tune. preaches Paul: "For when we were living according to our old nature, the passions connected with sins worked through the law in our members, resulting in fruit unto death. But now we have been freed from that aspect of the law, because we have died to what we ruled, so that we now serve in a new way provided by the Spirit, and not in the old way of outwardly following the letter of the law." Romans 7:5-6 . Finally, it is worth mentioning that a single law modulated the two covenants, the ten commandments. The difference lies in the kind of obedience rendered, the old covenant absorbed legalistic obedience, for self-righteousness, or filthy rags in the divine view: "But we are all as an unclean thing, and all our righteousness as filthy rags; we are all like filthy rags. We wither like a leaf, and our iniquities sweep us away like the wind." Isaiah 64:6 . The fruits of self-righteousness result in sin coldly drawn from the letter of the law; in the new covenant obedience is bound to the Spirit, by the divine nature of Christ, the Lord our Righteousness ( Jeremiah 23:6), through love and faith. In the new covenant, the law is engraved on the mind imputed to hearts and not on tables of stone, bearing fruit in obedience to the commandments of the Lord, founded on the divine nature of Christ.

According to the apostle Paul, the grace of God extends to every sinner through faith: "For by grace you have been saved through faith; and that not of yourselves; it is the gift of God." Ephesians 2:8 . Indeed, man is not saved by works or legalistic obedience to the law, much less justified by works of law, that is not the function of the law. "For no one will be justified in his sight by the works of the law, because by the law comes the knowledge of sin." Romans 3:20 , by works of the law is meant legalistic obedience to which no one will be justified: "Therefore we affirm the concept that a person is to be considered righteous by God on the basis of trust, which has nothing in common with keeping legalist of the law." Romans 3:28. It is necessary to conclude, the law does not wield the power to save the sinner, however, in the aforementioned text, Paul exposes the real function of the Law, to show sin, "because the Law produces wrath; but where there is no Law, neither can there be transgression." Romans 4:15 . The function of the law is to point out sin, therefore, where the Law does not rule, there is no transgression, releasing idolatry, calling the name of God in vain, murder, theft, adultery, false witness, covetousness, etc. "For before the law was given, sin was already in the world; yet sin is not taken into account when there is no law." Romans 5:13 . If there is no Law, sin is released, everything is allowed when there is no prohibition by the Law.

On the other hand, those who seek to justify themselves by legalistic obedience to the law, find themselves empty of Christ's justice, have fallen from God's grace: "you, who try to be declared righteous through legalism, are separated from the Messiah! of God's grace." Galatians 5:4 . Salvation strives for the merits of Christ, that is, the sentence of death ravaging the sinner has been paid, Christ died on the cross, making himself accursed by the law: "If anyone has sinned, punishable by death, and has been dead, and you hang him on a tree, his corpse will not remain on the tree overnight, but you will bury him the same day; for he who hangs on the tree is cursed by God." Deuteronomy 21:22-23. The Lord made Christ sin for us, even though he was immaculate, without ever having sinned, he was condemned on the cross in the sinner's place, overcoming death he ransomed Adam's offspring by lending his righteousness: "God made this sinless man the offering for sin to our favor, so that united with him we may participate fully in the justice of God." II Corinthians 5:21 . Only through Christ is it possible to receive by faith the promised Spirit, the divine nature, lost by Adam in the conception of sin, saving us from the curse of the law, that is, from the sentence of death: "For the wages of sin is death, but the free gift of God is eternal life in Jesus Christ our Lord." Romans 6:23. Man sinned by falling into a curse, Cytus substituted and redeemed the sinner by imputing his righteousness, transforming him into a new creature to present to the Father able to love and obey again: "Christ redeemed us from the curse of the law, making himself a curse in our place (for it is written, Cursed is everyone who hangs on a tree), so that the blessing of Abraham might come to the Gentiles in Jesus Christ, that we might receive the promised Spirit by faith." Galatians 3:13-14 . At Calvary, Christ died fulfilling the sentence demanded by the transgression of the law, renewed the covenant with the descendants of Abraham, imputed his righteousness in their hearts, fulfilling the promise to give the Holy Spirit by faith:"But God shows his love for us in the fact that the Messiah died on our behalf while we were still sinners." Romans 5:8 . Only Christ could die in the sinner's place as a vicarious lamb, only He has the same substance as the Father, the only one capable of replacing the sinful nature (original sin) with His divine justice restoring the sinner.

Indeed, the grace of God and of Christ did not nail the Law to Calvary, Jesus paid the price of the condemnation demanded by the transgression, dying on the cross in the sinner's place, giving back life, where sin abounded, grace abounded more: " the law that the offense might increase; but where sin abounded, grace abounded all the more, so that, as sin reigned in death, even so grace might reign through righteousness to eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord." Romans 5:20-21 .The grace of God is summed up in the lapse of time stipulated in the Plan of Redemption, for Christ to replace the sinful nature (original sin), by his righteousness or divine nature, through the atoning sacrifice, resulting in the justification of the sinner with the guarantee of restoring the lost divine nature displayed by Adam before the fall.
