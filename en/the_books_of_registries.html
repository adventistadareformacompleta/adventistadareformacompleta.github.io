THE BOOKS OF RECORDS

The text carved by John in Revelation 14:7 "Fear God and give glory to him, for the hour of his judgment has come; and worship him who made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters" , in the midst of the Plan of Redemption, represents the antitype of the day of trumpets of the Feast of Tabernacles . The announcement of trumpets is intended to draw people's attention to Christ's atoning work on the Day of Atonement and to prepare the church to face the judgment or great day of God, this occurs ten days after the trumpets, the atonement, represents according to the aforementioned Plan of Redemption, the investigative judgment , or cleansing of the sanctuary, elimination of sins accumulated during the year.

In ancient Israel the sins of the people were transferred, in figure, from the holy to the most holy, a work performed in the earthly sanctuary through the blood of the sin offering; thus , our sins are, in fact, transferred to the heavenly sanctuary, through the blood of the sin offering. of Christ. And as the typical cleansing of the earthly sanctuary was effected by the removal of the sins by which it was polluted, consequently, the actual cleansing of the heavenly sanctuary must be effected by the removal, or blotting out, of the sins which are recorded therein.
That said, it follows that the church has a great responsibility to raise its voice and announce like a trumpet:"Cry out at the top of your lungs, stop not, lift up your voice like a trumpet, and declare to my people their transgression, and to the house of Jacob their sins" Isaiah 58:1 . Warning the world of its impending doom if it does not repent of its sins and prepare to meet the Lord, the investigative judgment has begun. The Lord commanded His church to notify men inviting them to repent: "The times of ignorance did not count for God; will judge the world in righteousness by a man whom he appointed and believed before all, when he raised him from the dead" Acts 17:30-31 . understanding endorsedby White, verbs :

Upon us rests the heavy responsibility of warning the world of its impending doom. God invites His church to awaken, and to be empowered. There are immortal crowns to be won; there is the kingdom of Heaven to be attained; there is the world, perishing in ignorance, to be enlightened.
Ellen G. White, Testimonies for the Church 7, page 16

We are being weighed in the balance of God, our Christian conduct is being measured , therefore, we must be careful with our religious life, in the sayings of the Scriptures, take care of yourself and the doctrine , with all zeal and vigor, avoiding fulfilling the words of the prophet: "Tekel: Ye were weighed in the balance, and found wanting" Daniel 5:27 . Bear in mind that our actions are being recorded in books. "I looked on, until thrones were set up, and the Ancient of days sat down; his raiment was white as snow, and his hair was like pure wool; his throne was flames of fire, whose wheels were blazing fire. A river of fire flowed and went out from before him; thousands upon thousands ministered to him, and myriads of myriads were before him; he sat in the judgment seat, and the books were opened" Daniel 7:9-10. According to White's writings, in the book of life are carved only the names of those who have served the Lord in all generations:

As the books of record are opened at the judgment, the lives of all who have believed in Jesus are reviewed before God. Beginning with those who first lived on Earth, our Advocate presents the cases of each successive generation, ending with the living. Every name is mentioned, every case thoroughly investigated. Names are accepted, and names are rejected. When someone has sins that remain in the books of record, for which there has been neither repentance nor forgiveness, his name will be omitted from the book of life, and the record of his good deeds blotted out from God's memorial book. The Lord declared to Moses, "He that sinneth against Me, him will I blot out of My book." exo. 32:33. And the prophet Ezekiel says: "When a righteous man turns away from his righteousness, and commits iniquity, . . . all his righteousness which he hath done shall not be remembered." Ezek. 18:24.
Ellen G. White, 1978, The Conflict of the Ages, pp 482-483


DIVINE JUDGMENT INVOLVES THE EXAMINATION OF THREE BOOKS, WHICH THEY ARE:



THE BOOK OF LIFE

In the book of life, the parameter of divine judgment, the name of everyone who accepted to walk the bloody path of truth is recorded, accompanied by his good works that slumber beside him. By divine inspiration John wrote, "Another book was opened, which is the book of life, and the dead were judged from the things which were written in the book, according to their works" Revelation 20:12 . According to reports by White:

The great day of the execution of God's judgment seemed to have arrived. Several books were before Him, and on the cover of each was written in letters of gold, which looked like a burning flame: "Current Accounts from Heaven." One of these books was then opened, containing the names of those who professed to believe the truth.
Ellen G. White, 1984, Select Testimonies 1, page 518

The books of record in heaven, in which the names and actions of men are recorded, shall determine the decision of the judgment, the saints shall be judged by the works written in the book. The book of life contains the names of all who have ever entered the service of God. Therefore, it is worth our effort to fulfill Jesus' words: "Rejoice, because your names are written in heaven" Luke 10:20 . Likewise, Paul calls his brother's attention with a request regarding the saints whose names are in the book of life: "And I also ask you, my true companion, to help these women who work with me in the gospel, and with Clement, and with the other co-workers, whose names are in the book of life" Philippians 4:3. The prophet of the Babylonian captivity, looking down through the centuries, saw a time of trouble such as never was, and reported who will be helped and saved by the Lord: "At that time shall arise Michael, the great prince, the defender of the children of thy people, and there will be a time of trouble, such as never was since there was a nation until that time; but at that time your people will be saved, everyone who is found written in the book" Daniel 12:1. It remains proven that the church of God will undergo a hard test. However, the prophet of Revelation reports that only those whose names are written in the book of life will enter the holy city, as a reward for their labors in defending the faith."And nothing shall enter into it that defileth, and committeth abomination and lies: but only those which are written in the Lamb's book of life" Revelation 21:27. Sure enough, the overcomers were clothed in the divine nature, or white robes, reconciled by Christ and confirmed before the Father forever. "He that overcometh shall be clothed in white robes, and I will in no wise blot out his name from the book of life; and I will confess his name before my Father and before his angels" Revelation 3:5.



THE BOOK OF MEMORIES

The memoir has the power to break the tie, in case there is a balance between the good and bad works of the sinner at the moment of judgment. So when Christ judges a case in the Judgment of Investigation , and good works tie with bad works, then he will have recourse to the memoir, the decision for life or death will depend on faithfulness, zeal in meetings and in testimonies not neglected. If the good works overlap the bad works in the memoir, then the decision will be for life and he will be saved, otherwise, if they lean towards bad works, surely, the sentence will be death. Therefore, we must always have something to say in the Lord's meetings, a simple act like this can represent our salvation, or perdition if we are remiss. Let's see what White says in this regard:

Everyone must have something to say to the Lord, for in so doing they will be blessed. A memoir is written concerning those who do not desert meetings, but often speak to each other.
Ellen G. White, 1988 Early Writings, page 114

Just as the book of life contains the names of all who have ever entered the service of God, so are the good works of the saints recorded. The memorial book also includes the good works of God's children, as well as a record of evil deeds. Everything we do, think, speak is recorded by the reporting angel, according to White men will be judged by the works written in the books.

In heaven a book is written concerning those who are interested in the needs of their fellow men, a book whose record will be revealed in that day when man will be judged according to the works written therein.
Ellen G. White, 1987, Welfare, page 312

According to the prophet of God, there is a written memorial: "Then those who feared the Lord spoke to one another; the Lord looked and heard; there was a memorial written before him for those who fear the Lord and for those who remember his name" Malachi 3:16 . Records in the writing of White, mention the writings of Malachi assuring that the works of the righteous are immortalized in heaven, written in the book of remembrance, both evil deeds and temptations overcome, sacrifices and suffering endured for love of Christ and his doctrine . To see:

"There is a memorial written before God, in which are recorded the good deeds of those who fear the Lord, and for those who remember His name" Malachi 3:16. Your words of faith, your acts of love, are recorded in heaven. This is what Nehemiah refers to when he says, "My God, remember me; and do not blot out the mercies which I have done for the house of my God" Nehemiah 13:14. In God's memorial book every righteous deed is immortalized. There, every temptation resisted, every evil conquered, every word of tender compassion that is uttered, is faithfully recorded. And every act of sacrifice, every suffering and sorrow, borne for Christ's sake, is recorded. Says the psalmist: "Thou hast counted my wanderings; put my tears in Thy bottle; are they not in Thy book?" Psalms 56:8.
Ellen G. White, 1978, The Conflict of the Ages, page 485

Especially comforting are Ellen White's statements that good is recorded as faithfully as evil. She declares:

Every temptation resisted, every evil overcome, every word of tender compassion that is spoken, is faithfully recorded. And every act of sacrifice, every suffering and sorrow, borne for Christ's sake, is recorded.
Ellen G. White, 1978, The Conflict of the Ages, page 481

Even the most secret sins will come to light on the day of judgment. Unconfessed sins will not be blotted out, they are recorded in the book for judgment day. "Therefore, judge nothing before the time, until the Lord comes, who will bring to light the hidden things of darkness, and will reveal the counsels of the hearts, and then each one will receive praise from God" I Corinthians 4:5 . The duty of every man who truly loves God is to keep his commandments. "Fear God and keep His commandments, for this is the whole duty of man. For God is there to bring every work into judgment" Ecclesiastes 12:13-14 . As for unconfessed sins, the wise Solomon said:"He who covers his transgressions will not prosper; but he who confesses and forsakes them will obtain mercy" Proverbs 28:13 In the same vein as Solomon's teachings, White reveals to us:

Sins which have not been repented and not forsaken, will not be forgiven nor blotted out of the books of record, but will remain there to testify against the sinner in the day of God. He may have done evil deeds in the light of day or in the darkness of night; but they were open and manifest to Him with whom we have to do. Angels of God witnessed each sin, recording it in infallible reports. Sin can be hidden, denied, covered up, to father, mother, wife, children and companions; no one, except the guilty authors, will be able to entertain the slightest suspicion of the fault; but she lies uncovered before the heavenly beings. The darkness of the darkest night, the secrets of all deceitful arts, are not enough to veil even a thought from the knowledge of the Eternal.
Ellen G. White, 1978, The Conflict of the Ages, page 486

There is also a report of the sins of men. According to White's records:

"For God will bring every work into judgment, with every secret thing, whether good or bad." "For every idle word that men speak they will give an account in the day of judgment." Said the Saviour, "By thy words thou shalt be justified, and by thy words shalt thou be condemned." Ecclesiastes 12:14; Matthew 12:36, 37. The secret purposes and designs appear in the infallible record; for God "will bring to light the hidden things of darkness, and will make manifest the counsels of hearts." 1 Corinthians 4:5. "Behold, it is written before Me, ...your iniquities, and the iniquities of your fathers together, saith the Lord." Isaiah 65:6, 7.
Ellen G. White, 1978, The Controversy of the Ages, page 481

We cannot hide sins from God and the reporting angels; God knows exactly what we think and what we do. Therefore, according to the earnest injunctions of the apostle John: "If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness" 1 John 1:9 . Ellen White stated:

Every evil word, every selfish act, every unfulfilled duty, and every secret sin, together with every artful hypocrisy” are written in the books of heaven with “terrible exactness.”
Ellen G. White, 1978, The Conflict of the Ages, page 481

The only way of victory for the believer, is to love God, clothed with his nature we will be compelled to obey his commandments and love our brother. Everything we do and say is recorded for Christ to judge with the most honest justice. White tells us:

Every man's work passes in review before God, and is recorded by his faithfulness or unfaithfulness. By the side of every name in the books of heaven are written, with terrible exactitude, every evil word, every selfish act, every unfulfilled duty, and every secret sin, together with every artful hypocrisy. Heaven-sent warnings or admonitions that were neglected, moments wasted, opportunities missed, influence exerted for good or evil, along with their far-reaching results, are all recorded by the recording angel.
Ellen G. White, 1978, The Conflict of the Ages, pp 481-482

Ellen White urged the youth in particular not to forget that their lives were being watched very closely, and that one day the records of our lives must be faced. To her Youth's Instructor audience she wrote:

We are never alone. We have a Companion, whether we choose Him or not. Remember, boys and girls, wherever you are, whatever you are doing, God is there. For every one of your words or deeds you have a witness—the holy God, who hates sin. Nothing you say or do or think escapes his infinite gaze. Your words may not be heard by human ears, but they are heard by the Ruler of the Universe. He sees the inner wrath of the heart when the will is thwarted. He hears the profane expression. In the deepest darkness or loneliness, there He is. Day by day the record of your words, your actions, and your influence is made in the books of heaven. With that you will have to face yourself.
The Youth's Instructor, May 26, 1898

Lastly, it is worth emphasizing that men may forget, may deny their wrong course of action, but a record of this is kept in the memorial book, and in the great day of judgment, unless men repent and walk humbly before God, they will will face this terrible record just as it is. As White asserts, God also sees temptations overcome, devotion, commitment to the cause, battles fought against self:

God sees many resisted temptations that the world and even close friends never know about—temptations in the home and in the heart. He sees the lowliness of the soul in view of its own weakness; sincere repentance, even of a thought that is evil. He sees entire devotion in his service. He noted the hours of hard fighting with self—fighting that brought victory. All this the angels and God know.
Ellen G. White, Letter 18, 1891


THE BOOK OF DEATH

Ellen White wrote in her renowned book Early Writings, page 52, that the evil works of the wicked are recorded in the book of death, while in the book of records every name is written, and the deeds of everyone, their sins, and their obedience, are recorded. faithfully written. The renowned author also reports that she saw a book in which the unconfessed sins of all those who professed to walk in the truth were recorded, however, they denied with her works:

Another book was opened, in which were recorded the sins of those who professed the truth. Under the general heading of selfishness came a legion of sins. There were also headings over each column, and under these, beside each name, were recorded, in their respective columns, the lesser sins.
Ellen G. White, 1984, Select Testimonies 1, page 518

The names written in this book are of those who, after drinking pure water, muddy the rest with their feet, in the words of the prophet: "This says the Lord God: Behold, I will judge between flock and flock, between rams and goats. have excellent pastures? But you have trampled the rest of your pastures under your feet; and when you have drunk the purest water, you muddy the rest with your feet" Ezekiel 34:17 . In the language of the prophet of God, excellent pastures, represent the truth, pure waters, symbolize the purity of the doctrine . In this book are recorded all who walked in the path of truth, however, they were weighed in the balance and found wanting, they muddy the water with their backslidings and rebellion against thedoctrine and commandments of the Lord. According to the apostle Peter: "For it would be better for them never to have known the way of righteousness, than, after knowing it, to turn back from the holy commandment which was given to them. The dog returned to its own vomit; and the sow that was washed again wallowed in the mire" 2 Peter 2:21-22 .

Furthermore, the Lord said, "Whoever sins against me, him I will blot out of my book" Exodus 32:33 . Read, each one will answer for his own sins before the Lord on the day of judgment. "Parents shall not be put to death for their children, nor children for their parents: every man shall be put to death for his sin" Deuteronomy 24:16 . Whoever does not confess and forsake his sins will be blotted out of the book of life, his name will be transferred to the book of death. When the Ancient of Days is seated on the throne, the books of life and death will then be opened, in the phase of the millennium or review judgment , as White explains :

After the saints are changed to immortality and taken with Jesus, after they have received their harps, robes, and crowns, and have entered the city, Jesus and the saints sit in judgment. The books are opened—the book of life and the book of death. The book of life contains the good works of the saints, and the book of death the evil works of the wicked. These books are compared with the Standard Book, the Bible, and according to this men are judged.
Ellen G. White, 1988, Early Writings, page 52

Also, as seen elsewhere , God has an accurate record of every unjust account and of every dishonest business done by those who have not confessed their sins. Ellen White emphatically points out that:

Sins which have not been repented and not forsaken, will not be forgiven nor blotted out of the books of record, but will remain there to testify against the sinner in the day of God.
Ellen G. White, 1985, The Conflict of the Ages, page 486

Indeed, to keep our names in the book of life, avoiding the fateful event of our name being transferred to the book of death. We must recognize the futility of our righteousness, that the sinful nature is but filthy rags, and follow the prophet's counsel: "Only acknowledge your iniquity, acknowledge that you have transgressed against the Lord" Jeremiah 3:13 . Because if we feed our conceit; "if we say that we have no sin, we deceive ourselves, and the truth is not in us" I John 1:8 . And we certainly won't benefit from Christ's atoning sacrifice. Although,"If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness" I John 1:9 . Finally, those whose life records are examined in the investigative judgment and who have neither confessed nor repented of their sins will have their names blotted out of the book of life, while those who have confessed and repented will have their sins blotted out and be clothed. of Christ's eternal righteousness , according to White's delineation :

When someone has sins that remain in the books of record, for which there has been neither repentance nor forgiveness, his name will be omitted from the book of life. On the other hand, All who have truly repented of sin, and who by faith have claimed the blood of Christ, as their atoning sacrifice, have had pardon added to his name in the books of heaven; as they become partakers of the righteousness of Christ, and their characters are found to be in harmony with the law of God, their sins will be blotted out, and they themselves will be counted worthy of eternal life.
Ellen G. White, 1985, The Conflict of the Ages, page 483

What will happen to those whose names are not in the book of life? The prophet of Patmos answers, "And whosoever was not found written in the book of life was cast into the lake of fire" Revelation 20:15 .
