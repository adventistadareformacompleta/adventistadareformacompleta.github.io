THE SEVEN PLAGUES

Grace is a lapse (period) of time freely provided by the Creator to save humanity, motivated by his infinite mercy, guaranteeing the penitent sinner through repentance, the remission of sins, restoring him through faith in his son Jesus to the state of Adam before sin, clothing them with their divine nature . However, when "the Lord looks down from heaven upon the children of men, to see if there are any who understand, if there are any who seek after God, they have all gone astray, and have become corrupt together; there is none that doeth good, not even one" Psalms 14:2 . Thus , the renowned author White, sharesof the same understanding asserting that the Spirit of God will withdraw from the Earth, motivated by the resistance and rejection of men to their divine call, this will happen when the Lord looks at Earth and sees that there is no one else with an open heart to receive the justice of Christ (there is no righteous), therefore, there will be no more reason to intercede in favor of the sinner, Jesus will leave the sanctuary closing the door of grace . The aforementioned author reports.

The Spirit of God, insulted, rejected, reviled, is already withdrawing from the Earth. As the Spirit of God departs, Satan's cruel work will be done on land and sea. The wicked have passed the limits of their probation; the Spirit of God, persistently resisted, was at last withdrawn. Homeless from divine grace, they have no protection from the evil one.
Ellen G. White, Final Events, page 209

Then Satan will take control of the earth in the midst of the chaos, inflicting the most bloody persecution on God's people amid the plagues in the time of trouble . According to White's accounts, Final Events, shortly after the angels ceased to contain the four winds.

Satan will then plunge the inhabitants of the earth into a great final anguish. As the angels of God cease to contain the impetuous winds of human passions, all the elements of contention will be loosed. The whole world will be involved in a more terrible ruin than that which befell Jerusalem of old.
Ellen G. White, Final Events, page 206

In this way, Jesus will leave the sanctuary, ending his intercessory work and saying: It is done! according to the author of Revelation, at this very moment "there were voices, and thunderings, and lightnings, and a great earthquake, such as had not been since there were men on the earth; and so great was this earthquake" Revelation 16:18 . Complementing this enormous event, White in his book The Great Controversy, relates these glorious scenes, and the events that will follow, saying:

That voice shakes the heavens and the earth. There is a great earthquake "such as has not been since there were men upon the earth; such was this great earthquake." Revelation 16:18. The firmament seems to open and close. The glory of the throne of God would seem to permeate the atmosphere. The mountains sway like reeds in the wind, and jagged rocks are scattered everywhere. Babylon the great came in remembrance before God, "to give her the cup of the wine of the indignation of His wrath." Revelation 16:19, 21.

All cases have been decided, the door of grace closes , the four angels release the four winds and seven plagues will fall in an overwhelming way bringing the most severe divine judgments punishing humanity mercilessly. Sister White crystal-clearly highlights these terrible final scenes of earth's history, describing why the wicked are worthy of receiving the seven plagues, as follows:

It is impossible to describe the horror and despair of those who have trampled underfoot God's holy commandments. The Lord gave them His law; they might have gauged his character for her, and known her defects while there was still opportunity for repentance and correction; but in order to win the favor of the world, they set aside their precepts and taught others to transgress. They endeavored to compel God's people to profane His Sabbath. Now they are condemned by that law which they despised. With terrible clarity they see that they are without excuse. They chose whom to serve and worship. "Then ye shall see again the difference between the righteous and the wicked; between him that serves God, and he that serves him not." Bad. 3:18.
Ellen G. White, The Great Controversy, page 645

It is important to emphasize that the righteous, who received the latter rain , contemplated by the righteousness of Christ , will be wonderfully protected by the Lord from the hardships that will devastate the land inflicted by the plagues; while the wicked will suffer the most terrible scourges ever seen by men, and will feel the weight of God's wrath for rejecting his divine invitation in the third angel's message .

In view of the facts narrated, it is urged to emphasize that just as the Lord is great in mercy, he is also great in justice, so "If we are unfaithful, he remains faithful; he cannot deny himself, failing to punish us" II Timothy 2:13 . We well know that God is merciful, as he is just, therefore he cannot but repay to each one according to his works, so we infer from the renown of the renowned author White.

The glory of God is to be merciful, full of mercy, kindness, goodness and truth. But the justice manifested in punishing the sinner is as truly the glory of the Lord as the manifestation of his mercy. The Lord God of Israel will execute judgments on the gods of this world like the gods of Egypt. With fire and floods, plagues and earthquakes, He will spoil the world.
Ellen G. White, Final Events, page 206

The judgment of God will be executed, for the sinfulness of the sinner, who rejected the grace of a merciful and holy God, therefore there will be no more sacrifice in favor of the sinner, Jesus made the last effort. According to the prophet Jeremiah, "A noise shall come to the end of the earth: for the LORD hath controversy with the nations, he will enter into judgment with all flesh; he will deliver the wicked to the sword, saith the LORD" Jeremiah 25:31 .

The rays of God's wrath are about to fall, and when He begins to punish transgressors, there will be no rest period until the end.

THE FIRST FIVE PLAGUES - PART II


There is a terrifying fact that will occur in the course of the outpouring of the fourth plague. This fact, which was already witnessed at the siege of Jerusalem, fulfilling the words of the multitude, when they chose to release Barabbas and crucify Jesus, Pilate washed his hands and said, let it be with you: "And the people answered, His blood be on us and about our children!" Matthew 27:25 . With this answer the Jews brought the wrath of God upon them, which was fulfilled in the year seventy after Christ, when Jerusalem was besieged, the famine was so extreme that the condemned Jews ate all the animals within the city, then ate the hides of the animal cages and shields, and finally the mothers ate their own children. According to texts taken from the Scriptures. "The LORD will raise up a nation against you from afar, from the ends of the earth he will come, like the swift flight of an eagle, a nation whose language you will not understand, a nation fierce in face, that will not respect the old, nor have mercy on the young. he will be in all your cities, until the high and strong walls in which you trusted come down throughout your land; and he will besiege you in all your cities, throughout all the land that the LORD your God has given you. You shall eat the fruit of your womb, the flesh of your sons and your daughters, which the LORD your God gives you in the anguish and distress with which your enemies will press you. and of the children that you have, for he will eat them secretly for want of everything, in the anguish and distress with which your enemy will press you in your cities." Deuteronomy 28:49-50; 52-53;. What nation was this? The prophet Daniel in vision beheld four beasts, the fourth beast was terrifying. "In my vision at night, I still saw a fourth beast, terrifying, frightening, and very powerful. It had great iron horns, with which it tore and devours its victims and trampled on all that was left. It was different from all previous beasts and had ten horns. That horn had eyes like the eyes of a man, and a mouth that spoke proudly. " Daniel 7:7-8 .
