THE INVESTIGATIVE JUDGMENT

Investigative Judgment is held in heaven. It involves the righteous dead and the righteous living, whose names are written in the book of life. It takes place in the heavenly sanctuary, where each case will be decided, either for life or for death, work carried out in the second apartment called the Most Holy. Ellen White highlights.

Each case had been decided for life or death. While Jesus had been ministering in the sanctuary, judgment had been going on for the righteous dead, and then for the righteous living. Christ had received His kingdom, having made atonement for His people, and blotted out their sins. When Jesus withdrew from the most holy place, I heard the tinkling of bells on his robes; and as He went forth, a cloud of darkness covered the inhabitants of the earth. There was then no mediator between guilty man and God, who had been offended.
Ellen G. White, Early Writings, pages 279-280

This work necessitates an examination, through the investigative judgment, of the books of record to determine who, through repentance of sins and faith in Christ, are entitled to the benefits of His atonement. The cleansing of the sanctuary , therefore, involves a work of investigative judgment. In antiquity this work was done in the Sanctuary and later in the Temple of Jerusalem, being symbolized by the Day of Atonement.

Just as on the day of atonement, foreshadowed in the earthly sanctuary, the confessed sins of only the truly penitent sinner were removed, so the first phase of the judgment in heaven will remove and blot out the recorded sins of those who have accepted the truth and are clothed in the clothes of the divine nature of Christ. However, not all professed followers of Jesus are genuine. Thus, the investigative phase will separate the true disciple (wheat) of Jesus from those who profess, but deny with their works (tares).

The vicarious (substitute) death of Jesus, fulfilling one more stage of the plan of redemption, referring to the types of the first advent, broke with the sacrificial system and the temple cult according to verse 51 that sleeps in chapter 27 of Matthew asserting that: "And, behold, the curtain of the sanctuary was torn in two from top to bottom, and the earth quaked, and the rocks were split" Matthew 27:51 . The way was open to the heavenly Sanctuary where Jesus, after being enthroned in heaven as king and priest according to the order of Melchizedek, ministers as High Priest in the writings of the apostle John: "Jesus said to him, 'I am the way, and the truth and life. No one comes to the Father except through me." John 14:6 . The Temple of Jerusalem had three main doors, the door to the courtyard was called the WAY, the door of the Holy place TRUTH and the door of the Most Holy called LIFE. Jesus represents these doors, He Himself clarified this truth when He said: "Then Jesus said to him again, Truly, truly, I say to you, I am the door of the sheep. I am the door; if anyone enters through me, it will be saved; he will go in and out, and find pasture" John 10:7;9 . Christ represents the three doors of the sanctuary that give access to the Father, no one goes to the Father without passing through these doors. Access to the Father is umbilically linked to the Sanctuary through these doors, symbol of Jesus; ministering as High Priest in the heavenly sanctuary that served as a model, see: "Your way, O God, is in the sanctuary, who is God as great as our God" Psalm 77:13 . The way of access to God passes through the sanctuary with the blood of Christ: "Having therefore, brethren, boldness to enter the Holy of Holies through the blood of Jesus" Hebrews 10:19 .

The Lord limited a time for cleansing the sanctuary: "Until two thousand three hundred evenings and mornings; and the Sanctuary shall be cleansed" Daniel 8:14 . In fact, the cleansing of the sanctuary also represents the cleansing of the church and the elimination of sin. Within the plan of redemption, it was established that the sanctuary would be cleansed at the end of the seventy prophetic weeks, said the prophet Daniel: "Seventy weeks are determined upon thy people and upon thy holy city, to finish the transgression, to make an end sins, to atone for iniquity, to bring in everlasting righteousness, to seal up vision and prophecy, and to anoint the Most Holy" Daniel 9:24 . The renowned pioneer Jones enlightens us:

"Seventy weeks", or 490 of these days, are set apart or cut off (limited) for the Jews and Jerusalem. This marks the time limits for the Jews and Jerusalem as God's special people and city. These are prophetic days, in which each day corresponds to a year: the 70 weeks, or 490 days, are 490 years, separated (taken) from the 2,300 days, which in turn are 2,300 years. The beginning of 490 years is therefore coincident with that of 2,300 years.
Jones, Alonzo T. 2011, The Consecrated Way to Christian Perfection, page 74

According to Paul's writings, the annual service of atonement in the second apartment, performed by the high priest, could not begin until the daily work of the priests in the first apartment was finished. This is why Christ entered the most holy in 1844; year in which, following the prophetic course, Christ ended his office in the first apartment. "Now after all this thus prepared, the priests continually enter the first tabernacle, to perform the sacred services; but in the second, the high priest, he alone, once a year, not without blood, who offers for himself and for the people's sins of ignorance, wanting by this to make the Holy Spirit understand that the way to the Holy Place has not yet been made manifest, while the first tabernacle is still standing." Hebrews 9:6-8 . There was the replacement of the earthly sanctuary by the heavenly one, like the offerings, no longer with the blood of bulls or rams. "but Christ, the high priest of good things to come, by a greater and more perfect tabernacle, not made with hands, that is, not of this creation, neither by the blood of goats and calves, but by his own blood, entered once in the sanctuary, having wrought an eternal redemption" Hebrews 9:11 . It being proved, that the earthly sanctuary with all the system and ritual was a figure, according to the teachings of Paul: "Therefore it was necessary that the figures of the things which are in heaven should be purified in this way; but the heavenly things themselves with sacrifices better than these. For Christ is not entered into the sanctuary made with hands, which is a figure of the true, but into heaven itself, now to appear in the presence of God for us" Hebrews 9:23-24 .

However, the second half of the 70th week remained, within the time limit for which the Jewish people and Jerusalem would have special favor. PART II

It was exactly in 1844, the period of the Philadelphia church, that Christ closed a door, finished the work of the first apartment of the heavenly sanctuary, opening the second door, because, to no other was given power to open it, only he was qualified as supreme. priest to begin the work of atonement or investigative judgment, with his own blood, to cleanse the sanctuary and put away sin. The prophet John saw and related this glorious event: "To the angel of the church in Philadelphia write: These things saith the holy, the true, he that hath the key of David, who opens, and no one will shut; and who shuts, and no one will open. : I know your works - behold, I have set before you an open door, which no one can shut" Revelation 3:7-8. Furthermore, while the first door was open, Christ could not begin the work of the second apartment or second door, Paul called our attention to this event when he said: "Wanting by this to make the Holy Spirit understand that the way of the Holy Place was not yet was manifested, while the first tabernacle is still erected" Hebrews 9:8 . In light of the book The Great Controversy, White complements the allegation, highlighting:

In the ceremonial of the earthly sanctuary, which, as we have seen, is a figure of the service in the heavenly sanctuary, when the high priest on the day of atonement entered the most holy place, the ministry in the first apartment ceased. God had commanded: "And no man shall be in the tent of meeting when he goes in to make atonement in the sanctuary, until he goes out." Lev. 16:17. So when Christ entered the most holy place to do the final work of atonement, His ministry in the first apartment ended. But when the ministry in the first apartment ended, the ministry in the second apartment began. When, in the typical ceremonial, the high priest left the holy place on the day of atonement, he entered before God to present the blood of the sin offering, on behalf of all the Israelites who truly repented of their transgressions. Thus Christ had only completed one part of his work as our intercessor to begin another, and yet he pleads with His blood, before the Father, in behalf of sinners.
Ellen G. White, The Great Controversy, page 428

Jesus closed the door of the first apartment of the heavenly sanctuary in 1844, and opened the works of the second apartment, prefigured by the day of atonement, for the cleansing of the sanctuary. The cleansing of the sanctuary and the consummation of the mystery of God are related. In the earthly sanctuary, the succession of services formed a cycle which it completed annually. And the cleansing of the sanctuary was the consummation of this figurative annual service. This cleansing of the sanctuary is prefigured in the cleansing and putting away of the filthiness of the children of Israel, and of their rebellions, and of all their sins which, through the priestly ministry, had been carried into the sanctuary during the year, that is, the day of atonement. , according to writings of Moses: "So shall he make atonement for the sanctuary for the uncleanness of the children of Israel, and for their transgressions, and for all their sins. In the same way, he will make atonement for the tent of meeting that resides with them in the midst of their uncleanness." Leviticus 16:16 . White, tells us who was benefited by the atonement, both in the typical ceremonial and on the great day of atonement:

In the typical ceremonial, only those who had come before God with confession and repentance, and whose sins, through the blood of the sin offering, were transferred to the sanctuary, had a part in the ceremony of the day of atonement. Thus, in the great day of final atonement and investigative judgment, the only cases to be considered are those of God's professing people. The judgment of the wicked is a distinct and separate work, and takes place at a later time.
Ellen G. White, The Great Controversy, page 484

In the typical ceremonial, the sins whose offerings were offered daily were transferred to the second apartment of the sanctuary, according to White's clarification:

As the sins of the people of old were placed, by faith, on the sin offering, and, through its blood, symbolically transferred to the earthly sanctuary, so in the new covenant the sins of those who have repented are, by faith, placed about Christ and transferred in fact to the heavenly sanctuary. And as the typical cleansing of the earthly sanctuary was effected by the removal of the sins by which it was polluted, so the actual cleansing of the heavenly sanctuary must be effected by the removal, or blotting out, of the sins which are recorded therein. But before this can be accomplished, there must be an examination of the books of record to determine who, through repentance of sins and faith in Christ, is entitled to the benefits of His atonement. The cleansing of the sanctuary, therefore, involves an investigation—a judgment.
Ellen G. White, The Great Controversy, page 420

The Holy Scriptures report that the day of blotting out sins or cleansing the sanctuary could only be done following the dictates of the Creator carved and taught by Moses: "But on the tenth of this seventh month shall be the Day of Atonement; you shall have a holy convocation and you shall afflict your souls; you shall bring offerings made by fire to the LORD. On that same day you shall do no work, for it is the Day of Atonement, to make atonement for you before the LORD your God. shall be cut off from his people. Whoever does any work in that day, him I will destroy from among his people" Leviticus 23:27-30 .

At the appointed time of judgment—the end of the 2,300 days in 1844—the work of investigating and blotting out sins began. The cleansing of the Sanctuary from the filthiness of the children of Israel, in fact, was the cleansing not only of the sanctuary but of the people itself. The work of the Sanctuary culminates in the elimination of sin and the purification of the church of God. Work started in 1844, after Christ finished work on the first compartment. According to Jones' teachings:

The meaning of this purification is clearly expressed in the part of Scripture we are studying: Dan. 9:24-28. The angel of God, in explaining to Daniel the truth concerning the 2300 days, also declared the great object of the Lord at this time, in relation to Jews and Gentiles. The seventy weeks, or 490 years set apart for the Jews and Jerusalem, are specified to be "to put an end to transgression. To put an end to sin, to atone for iniquity, to bring in everlasting righteousness, to seal up vision and prophecy, and to anoint the Holy of holies". Dan. 9:24.
Alonzo T. Jones, 2011, The Consecrated Way to Christian Perfection, pages 77-78

We have studied that the seventy weeks were given to the Jewish people, it was a period of grace granted to the Israelites, to repent and confess their sins, this period ended in the stoning of Stephen, with total rejection of the Jews as a nation. See Jones' writings:

After the rejection of the Jews, the kingdom of God was given to the Gentile nations. And all that should have been done by the Jews in the 490 years dedicated to them, but which they by no means consented to take place, this is what must be done for the Gentiles, to whom the kingdom of God is given in the 1810 years that is granted to them.
Alonzo T. Jones, 2011, The Consecrated Way to Christian Perfection, page 78

In the alluded to year 34 AD, the time of probation for the Gentiles begins, to also repent of their sins, an event mentioned by Paul: "Now the Scriptures, foreseeing that God would justify the Gentiles by faith, preached the gospel to Abraham beforehand: all peoples will be blessed" Galatians 3:8 .

In this text, Paul rebukes the Jews for their unbelief and rebellion and announces the gospel to the Gentiles, fulfilling the scriptures, following the prophetic course of the Plan of Redemption, a fact considered absurd by the hardened Jews, who were based on their useless ritual traditions and ceremonies. . "Then Paul and Barnabas, speaking boldly, said, It was fitting that the word of God should be preached to you first; but since you reject it, and think yourselves unworthy of eternal life, behold, we turn to the Gentiles" Acts 13:46 .

When Paul perceived the obstinacy of the Jews, that; "When they opposed and blasphemed, Paul shook off his robes and said to them, Your blood on your own heads! I am clean from it, and from now on I go to the Gentiles" Acts 18:6 . So Paul turned to the Gentiles: "I address you who are Gentiles! Since I am an apostle to the Gentiles, I glorify my ministry" Romans 11:13 .

Fulfilling the Scriptures copiously, after the end of the time of the Jews, the time of probation for the Gentiles began. In the investigative judgment "we must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ, that each one may receive according to the good or evil that he has done through the body" II Corinthians 5:10 .

When Jesus cleanses the sanctuary and the people of God, both Jew and Gentile have already obtained their time of probation, after that event, whoever is righteous remains righteous, whoever is wicked remains wicked, according to Revelation 22:11 , that is, whoever does not participate , accompanying Christ's work in the heavenly sanctuary cannot be benefited by his intercession. Whoever does not have a hut feast , does not follow the atonement, will certainly be cut off from the people of God, as the type tells us.

God's plan following the sayings of the prophet Daniel is: To put an end to prevarication, to put an end to sin (original, clothing believers with his divine nature ), to atone for iniquity, to bring in eternal righteousness . With the cleansing of the sanctuary (church), transgression, iniquity and sin will be atoned for (done away), in the work of the heavenly sanctuary accomplished by Jesus in the investigative judgment or day of atonement, then will come the third angel's message , sealing , latter rain or time of refreshing where the imputation of eternal righteousness will take place, which we will better understand in the study that follows.
