According to the book of Leviticus."Speak to the children of Israel, and say to them, When you come into the land which I am giving you, and reap its harvest, then you shall bring a sheaf of the firstfruits of your harvest to the priest; he shall wave the sheaf before the LORD, that you may be accepted. ; on the day after the Sabbath, the priest shall wave it. On the day you wave the sheaf, you shall offer a lamb without blemish a year old as a burnt offering to the Lord" Leviticus 23:10-12 .

The sheaf of firstfruits was waved on the day immediately following the ceremonial Sabbath, which could fall on any day, at the time of Christ's death, it fell on the same day as the weekly Sabbath, so the apostle John calls the great day the Sabbath, because it coincided with that day. year the two Saturdays fall together, that of the Passover feast with the weekly one, that's why they called it the great Saturday. "Now the Jews, as it was preparation, and that the bodies should not remain on the cross on the Sabbath, for that Sabbath day was great..." John 19:31. They killed the Passover lamb on Nisan 14, celebrated the Passover Sabbath on Nisan 15, and offered the waved sheaf of the firstfruits on Nisan 16, regardless of which day of the week those dates fell. The Passover typified the lamb (Christ) who had to be killed paying the price for sin, while the feast of the firstfruits represented the resurrection of Jesus, raising the first righteous, as seen: "But in fact Christ was raised from the dead, he being the firstfruits of them that slept" I Corinthians 15:20 . At the moment of Christ's death the graves were opened and many righteous were resurrected fulfilling a part of the Plan of Redemption which the Feast of the Firstfruits typified the resurrection."The tombs were opened, and many bodies of the saints, which slept, rose again; and coming out of the tombs after the resurrection of Jesus, they entered the holy city, and appeared to many" Matthew 27:52-53 . In fact, we conclude that Jesus died on the occasion of the feast of the firstfruits and rose again, that is, his resurrection took place on the first day of the week, because in this ritual the priest moved the sheaves before God. It was the day after the Sabbath of the Feast of the Passover and Jesus according to this prophecy would have to be resurrected, and he was at rest (in the grave) on the Sabbath according to the Law. For this reason Jesus was resurrected on Sunday, not because he was making any change from Saturday to Sunday, as the evangelicals, image of the beast, claim.

Summarizing the teachings of the observance of the Easter types, we have the following reflection: Easter is symbolic of the death of Christ. As the Passover lamb died, Christ died. The blood of the lamb once freed Israel from the destroying angel. The blood of Christ today reconciles us to God. As typified by the waved sheaf, the Feast of Firstfruits is symbolic of the resurrection, according to teachings:

The waved sheaf of the firstfruits was the main service of the day, but a lamb was also sacrificed as a burnt offering. No firstfruits potion was at any time burned in the fire, for it represented resurrected beings clothed in immortality never to be subject to death or weakness again. "Christ (the antitype) rose from the dead, becoming the firstfruits of them that slept." I Corinthians 15:20. The symbol met the antitype.

The Plan of Redemption does not allow for the existence of two types for an antitype.
