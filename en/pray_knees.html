KNEES PRAYER

Facing the defender of Rome, the famous Dr. Eck, Oecolampadius, modest and not self-confident, had feared combat. Though gentle and courteous in manner, he proved capable and persistent. The reformer clung tenaciously to the Scriptures.

"Custom," he said, "has no force in our Switzerland unless it accords with the constitution; now, in matters of faith, the Bible is our constitution."
Ellen G. White, The Great Controversy 1985, page 181

Therefore, we can infer that in matters of faith, the Bible is what establishes the doctrine of the Church of Christ, according to the prophet Isaiah "The law and the testimony! If they do not speak this way, they will never see the dawn" Isaiah 8:20 . That's why we need to inquirewhat position should the servants of God pray, given that the comfort and lack of reverence prevails in apostate religious denominations that seek arguments and biblical texts taken from their legitimate contexts to support their prayers sitting or standing, according to their convenience. It is known that some theologians and remiss pastors claim that the Scriptures authorize standing or even sitting prayer, listing biblical texts to support their theses. They call fanatics, those who advocate praying on your knees and hands raised, however, let's carefully analyze the texts held by these pastors and theologians

We will begin with the text carved in the book of Luke, where Simeon contemplated the messiah and praised the Lord. Alleging that Simeon prayed while standing in the temple, let's examine the text:

Moved by the Spirit, he went to the temple; and when the parents brought in the child Jesus to do with him what the law commanded, Simeon took him in his arms and praised God, saying, Now, Lord, let your servant depart in peace, according to thy word: for mine eyes have seen thy salvation, which thou hast prepared before all peoples, a light for revelation to the Gentiles, and for the glory of thy people Israel.” And the child's father and mother were amazed at what he said. Simeon blessed them, and said to Mary, the child's mother, Behold, this child is destined both to fall and to raise up many in Israel, and to be contradicted" Luke 2:27-34 .

The Holy Spirit was upon Simeon. There was a promise: The Holy Spirit had revealed to him that he would not pass through death before seeing the Christ of the Lord. (Luke 2:26). The promise was fulfilled, Simeon took the boy in his arms and praised or thanked God for the fulfillment of the promise, he was not praying, see that he interrupted his speech and addressed the boy's parents. Furthermore, the Bible does not say that he was praying or what his position was. Those who force extensive interpretation of the text are advocates of standing or sitting prayer.

In another text listed, according to its defenders, Solomon prayed standing in the temple, we will examine the text:

"I have indeed built you a house for your dwelling place, a place for you to dwell. Then the king turned his face and blessed all the congregation of Israel, while they all stood" 1 Kings 8:13-14 .

In this text, it was clearly proven that Solomon was not praying. The text is clear in its affirmation when it says that Solomon blessed the people, just as at the close of our services, the congregation stands while the speaker or superintendent asks for the final blessings. Therefore, these passages and others with supposed standing prayers are not confirmed. There are prayer passages standing outside the temple, walking along the path, as taught by Sister White:

In order to pray, it is not necessary for you to be always on your knees. Cultivate the habit of talking to the Savior when alone, when you are walking, and when busy with your daily work.
Ellen G. White, The Ministry of Healing, pages 510-511

Note, that both walking and in daily work it is not obligatory to kneel to speak with the Lord. Texts such as these are presented as a basis for praying standing in official church services, however, this is an error, given that they are particular situations outside the hours of official church services, according to White's enlightenment, let's see:

We can talk to Jesus as we walk, and He says, I am at your right hand (Ps. 16:8). We can have communion with God in our hearts; walk in the company of Christ. When engaged in our daily work, we may exude our heart's desire inaudible to human ears, but these words will not deaden into silence, nor will they be lost. It rises above the hustle of the streets, above the noise of the machines.
Ellen G. White, Gospel Workers, page 258

We are not compelled to pray to the Lord only in church, wherever we are we can talk to the Lord, without having to get on our knees, we must pray constantly, according to our need, according to White:

We should pray constantly, with a humble and meek spirit. We don't need to wait for an opportunity to kneel before God. We can pray and talk to the Lord wherever we are.
Ellen G. White, Selected Messages vol. 3, page 266

Finally, defenders of the doctrine that teaches standing prayer, claim that Nehemiah prayed standing before Artaxerxes "The king said to me, What do you ask of me now? Then I prayed to the God of heaven" Nehemiah 2:4 . According to the alluded current of standing prayer, this text proves that we can pray standing in the services, see that the text has a context and another interpretation, contrary to the truth, the text clearly teaches that we can pray walking on the street in the midst of crowd or in a business transaction. See what Ellen White says about the text:

Among the throngs of passers-by in the street, in the midst of a business transaction, we may raise a request to God, asking for divine guidance, as Nehemiah did when he presented his request before King Artaxerxes.
Ellen G. White, Selected Messages vol. 2, page 316

Those who teach his adherents to pray standing in church, based on passages taken out of their context, claim that Jesus taught to pray standing in the temple as a rule, however, Jesus contradicts this statement and reproves by asserting that only hypocrites pray standing, then approves the kneeling prayer, see what he says: "And when you pray, ye shall not be like the hypocrites: for they love to pray standing in the synagogues and in the corners of the marketplaces, to be seen of men. that they have already received their reward" Matthew 6:5 .

As for the private prayers performed in our room, Jesus said not to pray standing like the hypocrites, "but thou, he said, When thou prayest, enter into thy chamber, and shut the door, thou shalt pray to thy Father which is in secret; and your father who sees in secret will reward you" Matthew 6:6 . The prophet Daniel teaches us what position we should pray in our rooms and how many times a day: "Daniel, when he heard that the deed was signed, went into his house, and up into his room, where there were windows open on the side. three times a day, he knelt down and prayed and gave thanks before his God, as he was wont to do" Daniel 6:10. It remains proven that Daniel prayed his private prayers in his room on his knees, therefore, adding to the teachings of Jesus, we infer that our private prayers in our rooms must be on our knees. Unlike our prayers when walking on the street, daily work or traveling, which can be seated or standing.

We will never see the dawn if our doctrine is not grounded in the Bible and the Testimonies, according to the prophet Isaiah (8:20). Therefore, it is impossible for us to find contradiction in the Bible or disharmony between the Testimonies and the Bible, so Sister White did not teach standing prayers for some and kneeling prayers for others in the services. However, some advocates of standing prayer present the text listed below as proof that Ellen White indoctrinated the brethren to pray standing because the church was so full. We will analyze the text:

I invited all who wanted to give themselves to God in a sacred covenant, and serve Him with all their hearts, to arise. The house was full, and nearly everyone stood up. People not belonging to our faith were present, and some stood up. I presented them to the Lord in earnest prayer, and we know that we had the manifestation of the Spirit of God. We felt that a victory had really been achieved.
Ellen G. White, Selected Messages vol. 3, page 268

Indeed, there was a special case, the church was full, so Sister White invited everyone who wanted to surrender to God to rise, that is, there were people not belonging to the Adventist faith, who were invited to rise to convert and not to pray standing, so Sister White commended them to God in prayer and there was no order for the church to pray standing.

In another text we can clearly infer that Sister White taught the church to pray on their knees, for her, it was strange or surprising for the church to consider praying standing, see the text:

I was surprised to see the entire congregation rise. I then requested that all kneel, and sent my petition to Heaven for this people. I was deeply impressed by this experience.
Ellen G. White, Selected Messages vol. 3, page 267

It would be a tremendous contradiction for Sister White to teach people to pray standing in one text and authorize kneeling prayer in another text, deliberately confronting the Bible. The two texts presented certainly seem to diverge. It is known that there were doubts about the subject under discussion, letters were sent to Sister White asking what position would be adopted at the time of prayer in the church. She responded with a THUS SAITH THE LORD the appropriate stance, sternly and publicly rebuking a brother who tried to initiate standing prayer in the service, this rebuke is a wake-up call to the current advocating the cause of standing praying in church, isolating texts To support your thesis, here is one of the texts:

I have received letters asking me what position a person should assume when praying to the Sovereign of the Universe. Where did our brethren get the idea that they should stand when praying to God? One who had been educated in Battle Creek for about five years was asked to say the prayer before Sister White spoke to the people. But when I saw him rise to his feet as his lips were about to open in prayer to God, my soul was moved within to give him a public rebuke. Calling him by name, he said to him: Prostrate yourself on your knees! This is always the appropriate position.
Ellen G. White, Selected Messages vol. 2, page 311

In fact, when Sister White says "get down on your knees! This is always the appropriate position", it is certainly THUS SAITH THE LORD, leaving no doubts or contradictions on the subject, that is, praying on your knees is the rule, is the position that should be adopted by the church of Christ, not leaving any remnant to try to raise doctrines by teaching to pray standing or sitting for mere convenience and irreverence. According to the text, Sister White shows her indignation to see her brother pray standing to the point of publicly rebuking him never to pray standing again in the services before the Sovereign of the universe. Not confusing official church prayer with exceptions, namely private prayers walking along the road, in daily work or traveling, as fostered elsewhere .

Theologians of the nominal Adventist church, delude their sectarians, teaching that the above-mentioned passage which brings a thus saith the Lord to the church to pray on its knees, was an isolated fact, teach that Sister White was rebuking Dr. Kellog. It is known that Dr. Kellog was a doctor and distanced himself from the church, following the path of pantheism, he came to believe that God is an energy that moves nature, however, it is another lie of the Adventist theologians, they say that the purpose of the warning was to combat the grandiose and irreverent spirit which was being cultivated by the people of Battle Creek, and it was not intended to give a dogmatic general rule on the subject, for the Church, yet we cannot forget, that Battle Creek was the center of the church's work. Adventist at the time, when the prophetess saw her brother praying standing, she rebuked not only Dr. Kellog, but the whole church, forbidding ministers, directors, and teachers to teach church members to pray standing, extolling the clarifications provided by God on this matter, which must be respected. As seen:

Where did Brother H get his education? __ In Battle Creek. Is it possible that with all the enlightenment that God has given his people about reverence, ministers, principals, and teachers of our schools, by precept and example, teach the youth to stand in devotion, as the Pharisees did? Shall we regard this as demonstrative of its self-importance and self-importance? Should these characteristics become distinct?
Ellen G. White, Selected Messages vol. 2, page 313

In another passage, which we will see later, Sister White ratifies THUS SAITH THE LORD both in public worship and in private worship, listing several biblical passages with examples of kneeling prayer, including Jesus and his disciples, at no time does she report standing or sitting prayer, on the contrary, it is shown that God is worthy of all reverence, therefore:

when you come together to worship God, do not fail to fall on your knees before Him. Let this action testify that every soul and body and spirit are in subjection to the Spirit of truth. Who has diligently searched the word for examples and guidance in this regard?
Ellen G. White, Selected Messages vol. 2, page 314

The Lord is holy and the place where we meet the Lord is sacred, so we should look to look up to God's servants to follow suit. As fallen, contaminated by the sinful nature and dependent on God and his divine nature offered by the sacrifice of his son, we must pray prostrate before the Lord who created us. Note that Sister White begins the text by warning that our position in praying to the Creator of the universe must always be on our knees. Defenders of the comfort of standing and sitting prayer, if they refuse to prostrate themselves before the Sovereign because their feelings are not covered by the divine nature of Christ, they think that they are equal to the Creator and can be on equality with the Lord, refusing to bow down. before the Governor of the universe with due reverence, as it is written:"As I live, saith the Lord, every knee shall bow before me, and every tongue shall give praise to God" Romans 14:11 . Analyze Sister White's text confirming Paul's teachings:

Our Attitude in Prayer In both public and private worship, we have the privilege of bowing our knees to the Lord in making our petitions to Him. Jesus, our example, "knelt down and prayed." Luke 22:41. Of His disciples it is recorded that they also fell on their knees and prayed. Acts of the Apostles 9:40; 20:36; 21:5. Paul declared, "...I bow my knees before the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ." Ephesians 3:14. In confessing the sins of Israel before God, Ezra knelt. Ezra 9:5. Daniel "three times a day he knelt down, and prayed, and gave thanks, before his God." Daniel 6:10. True reverence for God is inspired by a sense of His infinite greatness, and of His presence. With this feeling of the Invisible, every heart should be deeply impressed. The time and place of prayer are sacred, because God is there, and as reverence is manifested in attitude and manner, the feeling that inspires that reverence will grow deeper. "Holy and awesome is his name" (Psalm 111:9), declares the psalmist. As they utter this name, the angels veil the face. With what reverence, then, should we, fallen and sinners, take it to our lips!
Ellen G. White, Gospel Workers, page 178

God required Daniel and his companions to pray on their knees, just as he requires his church to kneel in private prayer, as in public worship. "Come, let us worship and bow down, and bow our knees before the Lord our maker" Psalm 94:6 . See another example from Daniel: "And while I was still speaking, and praying, and confessing my sins and the sins of my people Israel, and when I fell down, I presented my supplications in the presence of my God on behalf of the holy mountain. of my God" Daniel 9:20 . Ellen White confirms that God requires his people to pray on their knees, just as he demanded of Daniel, indicating the position, check out the text:

When in prayer to God the indicated position is prostrate on your knees. This act of worship was required of the three Hebrew captives in Babylon... In both public and private worship it is our duty to fall on our knees before God when we address our petitions to Him. This procedure shows our dependence on God.
Ellen G. White, Selected Messages, vol 2, page 312

Pagans revere their deities by prostrating themselves as a sign of respect and dependence, while pseudo-Christians extol theses to honor the only living and true God standing or sitting as if they were on an equal footing with the Creator. These heathens are honoring their deities with more reverence than the Christians. Sister White warns the church not to pray upright like the Pharisees, but, in all reverence, prostrate before the Lord, concerned about the situation of brethren tending to make the thesis of praying standing, she said:

It is our hope that our brethren will show no less reverence and respect in approaching the one true and living God than the heathen do towards their idolatrous divinities, or these peoples will be our judges in the day of final decision. I speak to all who occupy the positions of teachers in our schools. Men and women, do not dishonor God by your irreverence and impotence. Do not stand up straight in your Pharisaism in offering your prayers to God. Distrust your own strength. Do not trust her; but often fall on your knees before God, and worship Him.
Ellen G. White, Selected Messages, vol 2, page 314

Finally, it is important to note that, when a church apostatizes, it gradually abandons the precepts and doctrinal principles, creating divergent forms of the doctrine of Christ, according to the convenience of the congregation, in fact, we live in dangerous times, of rampant apostasy, churches are losing the devotional spirit and one of the abandoned points is the proper position of praying to God, on your knees and hands up. In this Church, the rule for official services, morning and evening services and private prayers in homes is to always pray on your knees and hands raised, allowing standing prayer when walking, traveling, among the people on the street, etc. This is Sister White's teaching, as can be seen:

We are living in dangerous times. Seventh-day Adventists make a profession of being the commandment-keeping people of God; but they are losing their devotional spirit. This spirit of reverence for God teaches men how to approach their Creator—with consecration and reverence by faith, not in themselves, but in a Mediator. Thus man is safe under all circumstances in which he finds himself. Man must come to the footstool of mercy on his knees, as a subject of grace, a suppliant.
Ellen G. White, Selected Messages, vol 2, pages 314-315

In Paul's sayings, pray without ceasing (1 Thess. 5:17) on your knees in services and standing while walking, working, traveling or among the people on the street. Surely the Lord will hear the prayer of faithful servants who depend on the divine nature of Christ and are not arrogant trusting in their faith leveraged by the sinful nature.
