THE PURIFICATION OF THE SANCTUARY


PART I

SANCTUARY IN HEAVEN

It is well known that there was an earthly sanctuary built by Moses according to a model required by God, however, we need to inquire , is there a sanctuary in heaven? If God ordained the building of a sanctuary on earth after a pattern, it is assumed that the pattern was of the sanctuary in heaven. In fact, the sanctuary doctrine is the foundation of Adventist faith in the existence of a heavenly sanctuary and the Investigating Judgment . The renowned prophetess White shares this assertion, verbis :

God forbid that the clatter of words from human lips should weaken the belief of our people in the truth that there is a sanctuary in heaven, and that a sanctuary after this pattern was once built on earth. God desires his people to become acquainted with this pattern, keeping ever in their minds the heavenly sanctuary, where God is all in all.
Ellen G. White, Letter 233, 1904, quoted in Christ in His Sanctuary, page 15

It is appropriate to emphasize that Moses made the earthly sanctuary according to the model shown to him by the Creator. "And they shall make me a sanctuary, and I will dwell among them. According to all that I show thee for a pattern of the tabernacle, and for a pattern of all its vessels, even so shall ye do" Exodus 25:8-9 . The illustrious apostle Paul manifests himself clearly , asserting that the earthly sanctuary was an authentic model of the heavenly sanctuary."As a minister of the sanctuary and of the true tabernacle, which the Lord has set up, not man. Who minister in the figure and shadow of heavenly things, even as Moses was divinely instructed, when he was about to build the tabernacle; for he says, See that thou do all things according to the pattern that was shown to you on the mountain" Hebrews 8:2;5 . According to writings by Sister White, this earthly sanctuary was a model of the heavenly sanctuary.

As stated, the earthly sanctuary was built by Moses, after the pattern shown to him on the mount. He was a figure for the present time, in which both gifts and sacrifices were offered; its two holy places were "figures of the things which are in heaven" (Heb. 9:9, 23); Christ, our great High Priest, is "a minister of the sanctuary, and of the true tabernacle, which the Lord founded, and not man."
Ellen G. White, Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 369

Indeed , Satan has defiled the heavenly sanctuary with his sin, therefore, the Plan of Redemption teaches us through rituals, symbols, sacrifices and ceremonies the work of Christ as intercessor and High Priest. The prophet John was given a broader and more revealing view of the heavenly sanctuary, its furniture, and the ark of the covenant amid thunder and earthquake, verbis :

"After these things I looked, and the sanctuary of the tabernacle was opened in heaven" Revelation 15:5 . "Then the temple of God which is in heaven was opened, and the ark of the covenant was seen in his temple, and there came lightnings, voices, thunder, an earthquake, and great hail" Revelation 11:19 . White's writings complement the alleged topped :

The sanctuary in heaven, in which Jesus ministers on our behalf, is the great original, of which the sanctuary built by Moses was a copy. ... The matchless splendor of the earthly tabernacle reflected to human eyes the glories of the heavenly temple in which Christ our Forerunner ministers for us before the throne of God. ...Moses made the earthly sanctuary after the pattern shown him. Paul teaches that that model was the true sanctuary that is in heaven. And John bears witness that he saw him in heaven.
Ellen G. White, The Great Controversy, pages 412-413

It is common knowledge that there were two apartments in the sanctuary, both in the earthly sanctuary and in the heavenly sanctuary. In the first apartment we find the table of the shewbread, the lampstand and the altar of incense. The first compartment was twice as long as the second. There the common priests ministered daily. This room was called the holy place, "For there was built a Tabernacle. In which were the lampstand, and the table, and the shewbread: this is called the holy place" Hebrews 9:2 . According to the Scriptures, this is how the utensils were distributed and located in the holy place, or first apartment according to the pattern given by the Lord to Moses:“And he set the table in the tent of meeting, by the side of the tabernacle, toward the north, outside the veil, and he set the bread on it in order before the LORD, as the LORD had commanded Moses. in front of the table, by the side of the tabernacle, toward the south, and lit the lamps before the LORD, as the LORD commanded Moses. of fragrant spices, as the Lord commanded Moses" Exodus 40:22-27 .

With surgical precision, White's writings match the Bible, reveal which utensils made up the first compartment, verbis :

In the first apartment, or holy place, were the table of shewbread, the candlestick or lampstand, and the altar of incense.
Ellen G. White, Patriarchs and Prophets, page 359

The alluded first apartment, or holy place, was separated from the second by a veil of fine linen, blue, purple, and scarlet, on which cherubs were embroidered. White reports that:

The building was divided into two compartments by a rich and beautiful curtain, or veil, suspended from gold-plated columns; and a similar veil closed the entrance to the first apartment.
Ellen G. White, Patriarchs and Prophets, page 357

Furthermore, it can be seen that the second compartment, perfectly square, was called the Holy of Holies or Most Holy Place, only the High Priest could enter that compartment, once a year on the Day of Atonement, for the purification of the sanctuary. It remains for us to ask, what was in the second, or most holy, apartment? The priests ministered in the first apartment, while the High Priest ministered in the second apartment. Only once a year was he allowed to enter the most holy to make atonement, on the tenth day of the seventh biblical month, the Apostle reports the services performed in the two apartments. "For by the law almost all things are purified with blood; and without the shedding of blood there is no remission" Hebrews 9:22 . "who has no need, like the high priests, to offer sacrifices daily, first for his own sins, then for the people's, because he did this once for all, when he offered himself" Hebrews 7:27 .

The question to be asked is how will the Sanctuary be purified?



PART II

Researching Alonzo's writings, we find some texts that portray the work and ministry of the sanctuary. He is incisive in relating that the earthly sanctuary, the temple with its ministry and services as such, were but a figure of the true one, which then existed in heaven, with its ministry and services. When the concept of the sanctuary was first presented to Moses, the Lord said to him, "See, and do after the pattern which thou sawest, which was shown to thee in the mount" Hebrews 8:5; Exodus 25:40; 26:30; 27:8 . The sanctuary on earth was, therefore, a figure of the true, in the sense of being a representation of it. The earthly ministry and services were figures of the true, in the sense of being a model, the figures of heavenly things according toPaul 's testimony to the Hebrews: "It was necessary, therefore, that the figures of the things that are in the heavens should be purified with these sacrifices, but the things themselves, with sacrifices superior to them. For Christ did not enter into the sanctuary made with hands, figure of the true one, but in heaven itself, now to appear before God for us" Hebrews 9:23-24 . It is said , that the whole apparatus of the sanctuary service with its types and shadows were part of the Plan of Redemption, yet what was the Lord's true purpose with the work of the sanctuary? A blunt answer emerges in Alonzo's writings, let's see:

The great end and purpose of the true sanctuary, priesthood and ministry, was that God should dwell in the hearts of the people. Now, what was the great end and purpose of dwelling in the hearts of the people? The answer is: perfection. The moral and spiritual perfection of the worshipper.
Alonzo T. Jones, 2011, The Consecrated Way to Christian Perfection, page 53

We discovered that God's purpose in the work of cleansing the sanctuary was to culminate in the cleansing of the people, with the Lord dwelling in the heart of the penitent sinner imputing divine nature or eternal righteousness , however, it is worth asking, when will this work begin? Who will help us to resolve the controversy is the prophet Daniel. He wrote determining the time of the cleansing of the sanctuary, saying, "Until two thousand three hundred evenings and mornings; and the sanctuary shall be cleansed" Daniel 8:14 . the reckoningDoes this prophecy take us to the year 1844, and what was the sanctuary to be cleansed in 1844? Surely it was the heavenly sanctuary, since the earthly temple had no more function after the veil was torn at Christ's death, "behold, the veil of the sanctuary was torn in two from top to bottom; the earth shook, the rocks were split" Matthew 27:51 . when the veil was torn apart at Christ's death, it was a clear demonstration that the sacrifices and offerings offered in the earthly sanctuary would no longer be received, that Christ's atoning work would continue in the heavenly sanctuary which took the place of the earthly sanctuary. Sister White reports that:

The tearing of the temple veil showed that Jewish sacrifices and ordinances would no longer be received. The great Sacrifice had been offered and accepted, and the Holy Spirit, who descended on the day of Pentecost, took the minds of the disciples from the earthly sanctuary to the heavenly, where Jesus had entered with His own blood to pour out on the disciples. the benefits of His atonement. But the Jews were left in complete darkness. They lost all the light they could have received on the plan of salvation, and they still trusted in their useless sacrifices and offerings. The heavenly sanctuary had taken the place of the earthly one, but they were not aware of the change. Thus they could not be benefited by the mediation of Christ in the holy place.
Ellen G. White, 2004, History of Redemption, pages 386-387

Indeed, Christ would be the perfect sacrifice, no longer needing animal offerings. The descent of the Holy Spirit on the day of Pentecost had the function of taking the minds of the disciples to the heavenly sanctuary and its work, with the sacrifice of Christ himself and his blood: "But when Christ came as a high priest of the good things already accomplished, through the greatest and most perfect tabernacle, not made with hands, that is, not of this creation, not through the blood of goats and calves, but through his own blood, entered the Holy of Holies once for all, having obtained eternal redemption" Hebrews 9:11-12 .
Furthermore, the total destruction of the earthly sanctuary took place in the seventy year after Christ, according to the writings of the prophet Daniel:"After sixty-two weeks the Anointed One will be slain, and will be no more; and the people of a prince who is to come will destroy the city and the sanctuary, and their end will be in a flood, and until the end there will be war; desolations are determined" Daniel 9:26 . When the Roman general Titus sacked and destroyed the city of Jerusalem, bringing down its walls in the aforementioned year, the temple was completely consumed by fire, therefore, there was no earthly sanctuary left when the prophecy of the two thousand and three hundred years was fulfilled in 1844. The pioneer JONES, Alonzo T. following the course of Daniel's prophecy, highlights the heavenly Sanctuary, as the only one to be purified, verbis :

And certainly, the sanctuary of which Christ is the High Priest is the only one that could be cleansed in 1844, as it is the only one that existed then. The sanctuary that was a figure for the present time was destroyed by the Roman army, along with the city (Dan. 9:26). Even its replacement was destroyed "until an entire consummation." By Him, the only sanctuary that could be cleansed at the time appointed by the Author of the prophecy, at the end of the 2300 days, was Christ's own sanctuary. The sanctuary and the true tabernacle of which Christ, at the right hand of God, is true priest and minister. This "sanctuary and true tabernacle which the Lord set and not man."
Alonzo T. Jones, 2011, The Consecrated Way to Christian Perfection, page 77

Ellen White (GC 416) noted that the cleansing of the "earthly as well as the heavenly sanctuary" was "fully taught" by God through Paul's writings: " For according to the law almost all things are purified with blood; and without the shedding of blood there is no remission. It was necessary, therefore, that the figures of the things that are in the heavens should be purified with such sacrifices, but the heavenly things themselves, with sacrifices superior to them" Hebrews 9:22 ,23 . Explaining the celestial by the terrestrial, she wrote:

As the sins of the people of old were placed, by faith, on the sin offering, and, through its blood, symbolically transferred to the earthly sanctuary, so in the new covenant the sins of those who have repented are, by faith, placed about Christ and transferred in fact to the heavenly sanctuary. And as the typical cleansing of the earthly sanctuary was effected by the removal of the sins by which it was polluted, so the actual cleansing of the heavenly sanctuary must be effected by the removal, or blotting out, of the sins which are recorded therein. But before this can be accomplished, there must be an examination of the books of record to determine who, through repentance of sins and faith in Christ, is entitled to the benefits of His atonement. The cleansing of the sanctuary, therefore, involves an investigation—a judgment.
Ellen G. White, The Great Controversy, page 420

White, in her writings, calls our attention to the fact that the sanctuary service is linked to heaven because it is the Plan of Redemption, at the end of the above text , she reports the examination of the books or the Investigative Judgment initiated by Christ in 1844, event reported by the apocalyptic seer. "And I saw another angel fly in the midst of heaven, having the everlasting gospel to proclaim to them that dwell on the earth, and to every nation and tribe and tongue and people, saying with a loud voice, Fear God and give Him glory, for the hour of His judgment is come. And worship Him who made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and fountains of water." Revelation 14:6-7. Examining the case of each believer, to finally contemplate who was benefited by the atonement, culminating in the work of cleansing the sanctuary. It should be noted that the earthly sanctuary services were an "example and shadow" of the heavenly, according to White's writings:

And what was typically done in the earthly sanctuary ministry is actually done in the heavenly sanctuary ministry.
Ellen G. White, The Great Controversy, page 419

It remains proved that the services of the earthly sanctuary were typical of the work of Christ carried out in the heavenly sanctuary, work begun in the first apartment of the sanctuary, from his ascension to heaven. Christ ministered in the first apartment until the year 1844, when he ends the prophecy of Daniel 8:14, until two thousand three hundred evenings and mornings; and the sanctuary will be cleansed. In this said year, Christ entered the second apartment of the heavenly sanctuary, beginning the Investigating Judgment to cleanse the heavenly Sanctuary. Paul emphasizes the work of the first apartment of the sanctuary, and that the work of the second apartment, or most holy, did not begin until the work of the first apartment was finished: "Now after all this thus prepared, the priests continually enter the first tabernacle apartment, to perform the sacred services; but in the second, the high priest, himself alone, once a year, not without blood, who offers for himself and for the sins of ignorance of the people, wanting by this to make the Holy Spirit understand that the way to the Holy Place has not yet been made manifest, while the first tabernacle is still erected" Hebrews 9:6-8 . Teachings shared by White indeed , relating that the work of the first apartment of the sanctuary was finished when the high priest ministered in the second apartment on the day of atonement, verbis :

In the ceremonial of the earthly sanctuary, which, as we have seen, is a figure of the service in the heavenly sanctuary, when the high priest on the day of atonement entered the most holy place, the ministry in the first apartment ceased. God had commanded: "And no man shall be in the tent of meeting when he goes in to make atonement in the sanctuary, until he goes out." Lev. 16:17. So when Christ entered the most holy place to do the final work of atonement, His ministry in the first apartment ended. But when the ministry in the first apartment ended, the ministry in the second apartment began. When, in the typical ceremonial, the high priest left the holy place on the day of atonement, he entered before God to present the blood of the sin offering, on behalf of all the Israelites who truly repented of their transgressions. Thus Christ had only completed one part of his work as our intercessor to begin another, and yet he pleads with His blood, before the Father, in behalf of sinners.
Ellen G. White, 1985, The Conflict of the Centuries, page 428

On page 76 of his book, JONES brings to light the typical form of purification of the earthly sanctuary, asserting that in the figure of the true or visible sanctuary, the succession of services formed a cycle that completed annually. And the cleansing of the sanctuary was the consummation of this figurative annual service. This purification of the sanctuary consisted in the cleansing and elimination of sins from the sanctuary, using the atonement as a tool, according to the command of the text that slumbers in the Pentateuch: "Thus shall he make atonement for the sanctuary for the uncleanness of the children of Israel, and for their transgressions, and from all their sins. He shall do the same for the tent of meeting, which is with them in the midst of their uncleanness." Leviticus 16:16. It is clearly seen that the purpose of the Almighty was to cleanse the sanctuary from the filthiness of the children of Israel, and from their rebellions, and from all their sins, which, through the priestly ministry, had been carried into the sanctuary during the year. That is, the sins committed daily were accumulated in the most holy through the work of the priest in the first apartment, requiring a broader work on the tenth day of the seventh month of the biblical calendar, on the day of atonement of the Feast of Tabernacles., on that day all the sins of the Hebrews who participated in this event with fasting and prayers were removed from the sanctuary, while the Israelites who did not accompany or attend to the day of atonement were not benefited by the atoning sacrifice figured by the symbolic sacrifice of animals. which represented the Lamb of God who takes away the sins of the world. In the face of divine dictate , the Israelites readily obeyed without question: "But on the tenth of this seventh month shall be the Day of Atonement; ye shall have a holy convocation, and ye shall afflict your souls; ye shall bring an offering made by fire unto the Lord. On that same day ye shall do no work, for it is the day of atonement, to make atonement for you before the Lord your God. Leviticus 23:27-29 . Sister White, shares the same teaching of Scripture, in ratifying, only those who accompany Christ's atoning work by faith in the heavenly sanctuary, that is, his church, will benefit through the mediation of our High Priest, Christ:

Those who, by faith, follow Jesus in the great work of atonement, receive the benefits of His mediation in their behalf; while those who reject the light presented in this ministry are not benefited by it.
Ellen G. White, 1985, The Conflict of the Centuries, page 429

As stated elsewhere , the atoning work of Christ or Judgment of Investigation began in 1844 , for the purification of the sanctuary and the people. In the year alluded to, the door of the first apartment of the holy place was closed, it closes when, according to the book of Leviticus, the high priest enters the second apartment, beginning the works of cleansing or expiation of sins in the most holy. Christ closed the door of the first apartment of the sanctuary and opened that of the second when he entered this precinct in 1844. As a fulfillment of his words applied in the book of Revelation, in the letters addressed to the churches: "This says What is holy, What is true, What has the key of David; What opens, and no one shuts; and shuts, and no one opens. I know your works: and, behold, I have set before you a door. open, and no one can close it" Revelation 3:7-8 .



PART III

It can be seen that the pioneer Alonzo shares the divine teachings, asserting that the purification of the sanctuary also consisted of the purification of the Hebrew people as a result of the elimination of sins, so he ends by making a comparison between the work of the earthly priest as a figure of the work of Christ in the heavenly sanctuary, let us see:

The consummation of this work, to and from the sanctuary, was also the consummation of the work for the people, since on this day of the cleansing of the sanctuary, which was the day of atonement (or reconciliation), whoever did not participate in the cleansing service through the scrutiny of the heart, confession and expulsion of sin, would be definitively cut off from the people. Thus the cleansing of the sanctuary affected the people and included them as surely as the sanctuary itself. And any of the people who did not partake of the cleansing of the sanctuary, not being himself cleansed as the sanctuary was—from all iniquity, transgression, and sin,—was cut off from the people forever. Lev. 16:15-19; 29-34; 23:27-32. And this "was a figure of that present time." This sanctuary, sacrifice, priesthood and ministry, were a figure of the true one, which is the sanctuary, sacrifice, priesthood and ministry of Christ. And this cleansing of the sanctuary was a figure of the true, which is the cleansing of the sanctuary - and true tabernacle which the Lord set, and not man, - from all the uncleanness of the believers in Jesus, because of their transgressions in all their sins. And the moment of this purification of the true, in the words of Him who cannot err, is: "until 2300 days, and the sanctuary shall be cleansed" - the sanctuary of Christ, - in the year 1844 of our era.
Alonzo T. Jones, 2011, The Consecrated Way to Christian Perfection, page 77

In the sayings of the renowned pioneer, welcoming the true teachings of the sacred Scriptures, he portrays that only those who accompanied the atoning service on the day of atonement with prayers and fasting, were benefited, while the remiss, were not contemplated by the expiatory benefits, leaving them to eliminate the people of God.

We have seen that in the service of the earthly sanctuary, when the work of the gospel in the annual cycle had ended for the benefit of those who had taken part in it, those who, on the contrary, had not participated, were cut off or excluded. "Who was a figure of that present time," and it teaches unmistakably that in the service of the true sanctuary, when the work of the gospel is finished for all who partake of it, then all those who have not taken part will be excluded. So, in both senses, the consummation of the mystery of God means putting an end to sin forever.
Alonzo T. Jones, 2011, The Consecrated Way to Christian Perfection, page 79

We have not forgotten that in the antitype days we are going through, only those who accompany Christ's restoring work in the heavenly sanctuary will be contemplated with the latter rain and the other benefits of Christ's atonement for the benefit of his people. The prophet Daniel portrays the cleansing of the sanctuary or people culminating in the eternal righteousness of Christ imputed to the heart of the servant of the Lord: "Seventy weeks are determined upon thy people and upon thy holy city, to finish the transgression, to give an end to sins, to atone for iniquity, to bring in everlasting righteousness, to seal up vision and prophecy, and to anoint the Most Holy" Daniel 9:24 . incisively _, Alonzo relates the work of the sanctuary to the outpouring of the Latter Rain for God's people, or eternal righteousness :

Since it is at this time that the coming of Jesus and the restoration of all things is at hand; and since this perfecting of the saints must of necessity precede the said coming and restoration, we have solid evidence that we are now in the time of refreshing, the time of the Latter Rain. And just as surely as this is so, we are currently living in the time of the definitive blotting out of all sins that at any time have beset us. The cleansing of the sanctuary consists precisely in blotting out sins: in putting an end to transgression in our lives; in putting an end to all sin in our characters; in the coming of God's own righteousness which is through faith in Jesus, that it alone may abide forever and ever.
Alonzo T. Jones, 2011, The Consecrated Way to Christian Perfection, page 84

The Hebrews did not understand that the symbolic work of cleansing the earthly sanctuary on the day of atonement had as its desideratum , the purification of the people to eradicate sins, in fact, the Jews were lost suffocated by their own righteousness, believing that the simple formalism of the ceremonies would be enough to purify them, this was a fatal mistake for the survival of the people of Israel as a nation. See comments by Alonzo, Jones T:

In the service of the earthly sanctuary we also see that in order to bring about purification, thus completing the cycle of the work of the gospel, it must first achieve its fulfillment in the people who participated in the service. In other words, in the sanctuary itself one could not put an end to transgression, put an end to sin, atone for iniquity, or bring in everlasting righteousness, until all this had been accomplished in every person who participated in the sanctuary service. The sanctuary itself could not be cleansed until each of its worshipers had been cleansed. The sanctuary could not be cleansed as long as a flood of iniquities, transgressions, and sins continued to be brought into it, through the confession of the people and the intercession of the priests. The cleansing of the sanctuary as such consisted in the eradication and expulsion from the sanctuary, from all the transgressions of the people, who through the service of the priests had been brought into him throughout the year. And this torrent must be stopped at its source, in the hearts and lives of the worshippers, before the sanctuary itself can be cleansed.
Alonzo T. Jones, 2011, The Consecrated Way to Christian Perfection, pages 79-80

All the symbolism of the earthly sanctuary work focused on the purification of the people. It was a representation of the cleansing of the remnant and the heavenly sanctuary, a work ministered by Christ, our great High Priest, for the purpose of cleansing the heavenly sanctuary and the church of God, putting an end to transgression, putting an end to sin, atoning for iniquity. and to impute the righteousness of Christ eternally to the hearts of his servants, as reported by the prophet Daniel and ratified by Jones:

The first act performed in the cleansing of the sanctuary was the cleansing of the people. What was essential and indispensable for the cleansing of the sanctuary, for putting an end to transgression, putting an end to sin, atoning for iniquity, and bringing in everlasting righteousness, was to putting an end to transgression, putting an end to sin, atoning for iniquity, and bringing in everlasting righteousness in the heart and life of each one of the people. When the stream which flowed to the sanctuary was stopped at its source, then, and only then, could the sanctuary itself be cleansed from the sins and transgressions of the people, which had been brought into it through the intercession of the priests.
Alonzo T. Jones, 2011, The Consecrated Way to Christian Perfection, page 80

In Jones' illuminating sayings, the sanctuary could only be cleansed with the cleansing of the people, therefore, the earthly sanctuary was a figure of the true sanctuary, we cannot ignore that the blood of animals was insufficient, only the cleansing blood of Christ can remove sin and sanctify the people forever, verbis :

Such is precisely the aim of the true priesthood and ministry in the true sanctuary. The sacrifices, the priesthood and ministry in the sanctuary, which were but a mere figure for that present time, could not really take away sin, could not make those who came to him perfect. But the sacrifice, the priesthood, and the ministry of Christ in the true sanctuary, take away sins forever, make all who come to Him perfect, make those who are sanctified perfect forever.
Alonzo T. Jones, 2011, The Consecrated Way to Christian Perfection, page 81

Christ entered the Most Holy in 1844 to purify it. However, we know that the people need to be purified, so the Lord uses the Latter Rain as a tool , an event in which the righteousness of Christ will be poured into the hearts of the remnant forever, resulting in the closing of the door of probation , when Christ leaves the sanctuary. and pronounce, IT IS FINISHED! . Let's see, writings by Jones :

And now, at this time of the consummation of the hope of the ages, at this time when the true sanctuary must be genuinely cleansed, at this time when the work of the gospel must be completed and the mystery of God truly accomplished, is the moment of all moments. that ever passed away, in which believers in Jesus, who are the blessed recipients of His glorious priesthood and wonderful intercession in the true sanctuary, partake of the fullness of His heavenly grace so that in their lives prevarication is ended, there is an end to sin and iniquity be atoned for forever, and in the perfection of the truth receive everlasting righteousness.
Alonzo T. Jones, 2011, The Consecrated Way to Christian Perfection, page 81

It is worth mentioning that we have already studied in an incisive way that the purification of the sanctuary depends on the purification of the people. In order for God's plan to be fulfilled, the people must be purified. The question that is asked is how to purify the people, heirs of the sinful nature? The prophet Jeremiah brings to light the answer, when he reports: "In his days Judah shall be saved, and Israel shall dwell in safety: this shall be his name, by which he shall be called, LORD, Our Righteousness" Jeremiah 23:6 . The pioneer Alonzo, expands this truth still hidden for many, when he emphasizes that eternal righteousness is Christ formed in every believer, that is, it is the imputation of his divine nature in the heart of the penitent sinner:

The consummation of the mystery of God is the final fulfillment of the work of the gospel. And the consummation of the work of the gospel is, first, the eradication of every vestige of sin and the bringing in eternal righteousness, that is, Christ fully formed in every believer, God manifested in the flesh of every believer in Jesus; and secondly, and on the other hand, the consummation of the work of the gospel means precisely the destruction of all those who have failed to receive the gospel (2 Thess. 1:7-10), since it is not the Lord's will to preserve the gospel. lives of men whose only end would be to accumulate misery upon themselves.
Alonzo T. Jones, 2011, The Consecrated Way to Christian Perfection, page 79

The Lord Our Justice, as narrated by Jeremiah, is the divine nature of Christ imputed in the hearts of the saints; with the outpouring of the latter rain the church will receive eternal righteousness or divine nature permanently, for eternity. At the consummation of this memorable event the church will already be sealed by the writing angel and will be ready to endure Jacob's time of trouble . According to Alonzo, it will not be a justice that can be removed, eternal justice results in the purification of the sanctuary and the eradication of sin.

Remember; eternal justice. Not righteousness for today and sin for tomorrow, and righteousness again, and sin again. This is not eternal righteousness (enduring righteousness). Eternal righteousness is brought in, and remains constantly in the life of those who have believed and confessed, and who go on believing and receiving this eternal righteousness in place of sin and sinning. Herein is eternal righteousness, in this is eternal redemption from sin. And this wonderful blessing is the free gift of God through the heavenly ministry that was established for our benefit in the priesthood and ministry of Christ in the heavenly sanctuary.
Alonzo T. Jones, 2011, The Consecrated Way to Christian Perfection, page 83

And this state of perfection, this unfolding in every believer in the complete image of Jesus, this is the consummation of the mystery of God, which is Christ in you, the hope of glory. This consummation finds its fulfillment in the cleansing of the sanctuary, which signifies the full realization of the mystery of God, and which is to put an end to transgression, to make a complete end to sins, to make reconciliation for iniquity, to bring in everlasting righteousness, to seal up the vision and prophecy. , and anoint the Holy of Holies.
Alonzo T. Jones, 2011, The Consecrated Way to Christian Perfection, page 84
